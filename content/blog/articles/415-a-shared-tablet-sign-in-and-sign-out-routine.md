# A Shared-Tablet Sign-In and Sign-Out Routine

> **In short:** On a shared tablet, separate device access, Norva account authentication, and profile selection. At entry, verify all three; during use, avoid saving secrets or changing another person's preferences; at exit, pause, confirm progress, remove eligible local items when appropriate, and sign out whenever the device or next user is not trusted.

“Shared” can mean a family tablet at home, a device lent to a guest, or a communal screen in a temporary setting. Those situations deserve different persistence. Convenience is reasonable on a trusted household tablet and risky on a device outside your control.

## Classify the tablet first

Choose the closest category:

- **trusted household tablet:** controlled by the household, with known device lock and users;
- **temporary guest tablet:** owned by someone else or lent for a limited period;
- **public or broadly shared tablet:** accessible to people outside the trusted group.

For a public or broadly shared tablet, avoid signing in unless Norva and the device provide a verified session flow appropriate to that environment. A browser's private mode is not a guarantee that every account, download, or device trace will disappear.

## Entry routine

### 1. Verify the device state

Confirm the tablet is the intended device, uses a trusted network for the task, and is not displaying an unknown management or security warning. Do not bypass operating-system warnings to begin viewing.

### 2. Open a verified Norva entry point

Use the known installed app or official web address in a compatible browser. Avoid links from messages or unexpected prompts. Norva is a software media player and organiser; connect only a compatible source you own or are authorised to use.

### 3. Authenticate without exposing secrets

Enter credentials away from onlookers. Do not copy passwords into notes, save recovery codes on the home screen, or accept password storage on a device you do not trust. Follow the account and authentication controls currently presented.

### 4. Confirm the profile

After sign-in, read the profile name or visible context before browsing. Separate profiles can preserve distinct progress, history, favourites, and preferences when the current plan provides them.

**Entry success:** the intended account and profile are visible, with no unexpected session warning.

## During the session

Use only the intended profile. Before changing a favourite, language preference, subtitle preference, or history-related action, consider whether it belongs to the current user.

Offline access is conditional on the device, source, and associated rights. Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored locally rather than uploaded to Norva. On a borrowed tablet, local storage can remain after viewing unless removed through the documented control.

The [complete tablet viewing workflow](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-tablet-viewing-workflows/) adds posture, network, audio, and interruption checks to this account routine.

## Exit routine

1. Pause playback and note the final position.
2. Return to a stable item or library screen.
3. Remove temporary eligible offline items when appropriate and permitted.
4. Review whether account credentials were saved.
5. Sign out of the Norva session when the tablet or next user is not trusted.
6. Close the app or browser session using documented controls.
7. Check that reopening does not expose the private account when a full sign-out was intended.

Do not delete another user's app data, browser history, or files without permission. If a saved credential cannot be removed safely, ask the device owner or Norva support for the correct process.

## Household variant

On a trusted household tablet, remaining signed in may be an intentional choice. Still require profile confirmation at entry and a clear handoff to the next person. Maintain a [trusted-device inventory](/blog/build-a-trusted-device-inventory-for-the-household/) so the household knows which tablets retain sessions.

For a guest, use the [guest-viewing preparation workflow](/blog/how-to-prepare-a-tablet-for-guest-viewing-without-mixing-profiles/) without assuming a special guest feature exists.

## Original evidence: session boundary card

| Stage | Trusted household | Temporary guest | Public/broadly shared |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sign-in persistence | Household decision | Avoid unless needed | Avoid unless verified safe |
| Profile confirmation | Every entry | Every entry | Every entry |
| Save credentials | Household policy | No | No |
| Local items | Review regularly | Remove after use | Avoid |
| Sign-out | At handoff if required | Yes | Yes |
| Reopen test | Periodic | Required | Required |

This matrix is a decision framework, not a security guarantee. Align it with the tablet owner's rules and Norva's current privacy and terms pages.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid treating a device lock as a Norva sign-out, assuming profile selection is account authentication, saving secrets on a borrowed tablet, leaving local media behind, or deleting shared data without consent.

Device management, browser storage, authentication providers, and operating systems differ. Norva's privacy policy explains its data practices, but it cannot define every tablet owner's controls. Contact support when the visible sign-out path or session state is unclear.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is switching profiles the same as signing out?

No. Profile selection changes viewing context within the available account structure; sign-out ends the account session.

### Can I stay signed in on a family tablet?

That can be a household decision on a trusted device. Use a device lock, confirm the profile at entry, and review retained sessions regularly.

### Does closing the browser sign me out?

Do not assume it does. Reopen the verified site and check the session state when sign-out matters.
