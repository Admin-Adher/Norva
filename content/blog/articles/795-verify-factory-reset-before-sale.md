# How to Verify a Factory Reset Before Selling a Device

> **In short:** Follow the manufacturer's transfer sequence, not a generic reset. Back up needed data, remove platform, Norva, browser, password-manager, payment, and source access, and handle SIM and storage as instructed before erasing. Restart to the new-owner setup screen without entering personal credentials. From a trusted device, review account and device lists, resolve activation locks, document the result, and transfer custody only after on-device and remote checks agree.

A reset button can restore settings, erase user data, or merely restart configuration depending on the device. Verification requires evidence of the intended ownership-transfer state, not just a confirmation dialog.

## Identify the exact transfer procedure

Record manufacturer, model, software version, ownership, internal and removable storage, and destination: sale, donation, trade-in, return, or recycling. Open the current manufacturer documentation for that exact device.

For a managed work or school device, obtain administrator authorization. Do not remove management or sell equipment the household does not own.

## Complete pre-reset dependencies

Back up only data the owner needs and is permitted to retain, then verify the backup. Unpair accessories and review payment, home-control, voice-assistant, casting, browser, email, password-manager, Norva, and compatible-source relationships.

Use the [secure disposal checklist](/blog/secure-disposal-viewing-device/) to include memory cards, USB storage, external boxes, eSIM or SIM, and failed media. Do not delete synchronized cloud data manually when the goal is only to remove it from the device.

## Remove accounts in the documented order

Follow manufacturer instructions for sign-out, account removal, device protection, activation lock, and reset. The correct sequence differs across platforms and versions.

Sign out of Norva where current controls permit and review the device or session from a trusted account. Handle each authorized source independently. An app uninstall or home-screen removal is not sufficient account evidence.

## Run the correct erase or factory reset

Use the manufacturer's ownership-transfer action, not a network reset, settings-only reset, service-menu shortcut, or third-party cleaner. Meet official charging, connectivity, credential, and storage prerequisites.

Wait for completion and restart. If the process fails, do not hand the device to the buyer with a promise to finish remotely later.

## Verify the new-owner setup state

The device should show the expected initial setup or welcome flow for its platform without exposing former profiles, media history, networks, files, or account identifiers. Check removable storage slots and attached devices again.

Do not proceed through setup with personal credentials. If a connectivity check is necessary, follow manufacturer guidance or let the buyer complete it while the seller observes that no former account is requested unexpectedly.

Use the [smart-TV preflight guide](/blog/secure-smart-tv-before-sign-in/) in reverse for televisions: verify model, ownership, reset provenance, platform state, and application absence before transfer.

## Verify account dashboards separately

From another known-good device, inspect Norva, platform, authorized-source, home-control, and trusted-device lists where current services expose them. Remove the old device when appropriate and confirm the result.

Understand activation protection before removal. A dashboard action can affect loss or theft controls; current manufacturer guidance governs the sequence.

## Use a second-observer handoff

Have another authorized household administrator or the buyer observe the setup screen, device identifiers, absence of removable storage, and receipt. This reduces disputes and catches a skipped check.

Record only the model, serial reference, method, date, observed state, account-review status, recipient, and exceptions. Never include a password, code, or reset link.

Include the result in the [viewing-device audit](/blog/viewing-device-security-audit/) and remove the device from the active household inventory.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Choosing “Reset Settings” instead of the ownership-transfer erase.
- Deleting cloud data manually before sign-out.
- Leaving memory cards or attached storage.
- Assuming an app icon removal ended the account session.
- Re-entering personal credentials after reset.
- Ignoring activation locks or device protection.
- Transferring custody before remote checks finish.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is seeing a welcome screen enough?

It is important but incomplete. Also reconcile removable storage, activation state, and relevant account or device dashboards.

### Should I let the buyer sign in before payment?

Use a safe agreed process that confirms setup readiness without exposing the seller's credentials or surrendering custody prematurely.

### What if the device still asks for my account?

Stop the transfer and follow current manufacturer guidance for activation protection, account removal, and a repeat reset.
