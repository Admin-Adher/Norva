# Set a Review Date for Every Offline Content Batch

> **In short:** Give every offline batch a personal review date tied to its purpose: after a trip, before the next connection gap, or before a device change. On that date, reassess need, current eligibility, required tracks, progress, and storage, then retain, retest, or remove each item. This organisational date is not a promise about rights or technical expiry.

A review date solves a simple problem: local items can remain after the event that justified them. The date creates a deliberate decision without requiring constant storage monitoring.

## Distinguish review from expiry

Your review date is a reminder you set. It does not define how long an item is technically available and does not extend or replace the source's conditions or associated rights.

Offline access in Norva remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. An item may need rechecking before your chosen date, especially after an account, source, app, or device change.

Use neutral labels such as **review on**, **needed until**, or **retest before**. Avoid writing **guaranteed until**.

## Choose dates from real events

Useful triggers include:

- the day after a trip or disconnected weekend;
- one week before another planned gap;
- before a device migration, reset, or storage cleanup;
- after finishing a series batch;
- after a household profile change;
- after an app or system update that affects local behaviour.

For recurring preparation, combine dates with the [offline content rotation](/blog/build-offline-content-rotation/) so active, fallback, remove, and recheck states remain visible.

## Review each item, not only the total

On the date, inspect every item and ask:

1. Is there a named future session?
2. Is the intended profile or viewer still correct?
3. Is current offline eligibility visible?
4. Do required audio and subtitles remain available locally?
5. Has progress reconnected correctly?
6. Does observed storage still justify retention?
7. Should the item be retained, retested, or removed?

Do not assume a successful old test remains current. If the item is essential for another trip, repeat disconnected verification.

## Remove through the app

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. Use the app's own offline removal control rather than manipulating internal files. The [offline item removal guide](/blog/remove-offline-downloads/) provides a conservative sequence.

After removal, check the device storage view later rather than demanding instant exact agreement. Temporary data and system accounting can update on a different schedule.

## Assign ownership on shared devices

Record who can approve removal. A household review should not expose more viewing history than necessary, and one person should not delete another person's planned item without agreement.

The [offline storage handbook](/blog/offline-storage-management-handbook/) helps reconcile shared-device space with item-level decisions.

## Make the next batch smaller

At the end of the review, compare planned items with what was actually used. If several remained untouched, reduce the next batch or improve session estimates. The [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) helps adjust selection, time, battery, and fallback decisions.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Presenting a personal date as guaranteed availability.
- Reviewing only total storage rather than individual need.
- Keeping a fallback with no future scenario.
- Removing another profile's item without confirmation.
- Manually deleting encrypted app-managed files.
- Retaining an item without repeating an old test after material changes.
- Changing the date repeatedly without making a decision.

## Frequently asked questions

### How often should batches be reviewed?

Tie the date to the event that created the batch. Post-trip and pre-trip reviews are usually more meaningful than an arbitrary universal interval.

### Does the review date mean the item expires then?

No. It is your organisational checkpoint. Actual availability depends on current device, source, media, account, and rights conditions.

### Should finished items always be removed?

Usually they can be considered for removal, but confirm shared-device needs and whether another planned disconnected period justifies retention.
