# How to Identify Which Provider Bills Your Subscription

> **In short:** Start with subscription pages for the store accounts and payment providers your household uses. Match the plan label, renewal date, purchase account, receipt issuer, and redacted statement descriptor. Treat the media application's entitlement display as supporting evidence, not necessarily the billing source of truth. Do not start another subscription to test a theory. Once identified, record the provider and official management link without card data, passwords, tokens, or full receipts.

The billing provider matters because it controls renewal, cancellation, receipts, and transaction support. Norva's current terms state that subscriptions may be billed through a store or payment provider and should be managed through the original provider.

## Check active subscription screens first

Sign in to each likely official store or payment account and open its subscription-management area. Look for the current Norva plan, renewal or expiry date, and status. This is usually stronger evidence than a remembered setup path.

Use only accounts you are authorized to inspect. In a household, ask the purchaser to check privately instead of sharing credentials.

## Match the receipt issuer

Search purchase email safely for the plan name and likely provider. Confirm sender domain carefully and open the provider account directly rather than following a suspicious receipt link. A legitimate-looking email is not proof until it matches the official subscription page.

The [receipt-organization guide](/blog/organize-subscription-receipts/) shows how to retain issuer, date, amount, currency, and redacted reference without storing complete payment data.

## Use bank records cautiously

A transaction descriptor can support identification, but abbreviations may be confusing and an intermediary can appear instead of the application name. Mask account and card details before sharing any extract. Do not publish statements in a forum or support chat.

If the descriptor remains unclear, ask the financial institution through its official channel and avoid disputed-charge actions until the subscription is identified accurately.

Check whether the amount is recurring or a one-time purchase and whether a family organizer controls the store account. These distinctions can explain why the expected subscription is absent from one person's account page.

## Compare dates across systems

Match purchase date, trial end, renewal date, billing interval, and amount. Small tax or currency differences can occur, so document them rather than forcing an exact match. The strongest case combines provider status with a corresponding receipt or statement.

The [renewal-date guide](/blog/document-next-renewal-date/) prevents an estimate from being recorded as a provider-confirmed date.

## Do not repurchase to reveal the provider

Starting another trial or subscription can create duplicate billing and a second account. If no provider shows the plan, gather minimal evidence and contact Norva support and the likely providers.

The [duplicate-account guide](/blog/duplicate-account-created-next-steps/) describes safe recovery if another account was already created accidentally.

## Separate billing from entitlement

Norva can process entitlement status to decide access while the provider handles the payment instrument. A stale in-app status does not prove the provider failed to bill, and a bank charge does not prove the app received an updated entitlement.

Use the [subscription-status troubleshooting guide](/blog/subscription-status-not-updating/) if the billing provider is confirmed but access state remains wrong.

## Original evidence: billing provider evidence ladder

| Rank | Evidence | What it establishes | Caution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Official active-subscription screen | Provider and current status | Confirm purchase account |
| 2 | Official provider receipt | Issuer, plan, date, amount | Redact identifiers |
| 3 | Provider purchase history | Transaction association | Check account and currency |
| 4 | Redacted bank descriptor | Charged intermediary | Descriptor may be abbreviated |
| 5 | Norva entitlement display | Application access state | May lag billing status |
| 6 | Memory | Initial hypothesis | Never use alone |

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Checking only one household store account.
- Trusting an email without confirming the provider portal.
- Sharing complete receipts or statements.
- Treating entitlement status as billing proof.
- Creating another subscription as a test.
- Ignoring dates, currency, tax, or billing interval.
- Canceling through the wrong provider.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can Norva's app screen identify the provider?

It may show entitlement or provider information, but verify the billing source in the original provider's official subscription page.

### Is a bank descriptor enough?

It is supporting evidence, but abbreviations and intermediaries can mislead; pair it with provider-account or receipt evidence.

### What if no store account shows the subscription?

Check authorized household accounts and other providers, then contact official support with dates and redacted evidence rather than repurchasing.
