# How to Prepare a Phone or Tablet for a Long Journey

> **In short:** Prepare the entire device, not only the viewing list. Check storage, complete and test eligible offline items, update the app and operating system early, verify headphones and charging, review mobile-data controls, protect account access, and leave enough time to correct a failed check before departure.

A long journey concentrates several weak points: intermittent networks, limited charging, unfamiliar Wi-Fi, low storage, and little time for account recovery. A reliable setup treats them as one system.

## Define the journey before choosing media

Record the travel duration, expected connected segments, charging opportunities, and realistic viewing time. Then choose fewer priority items than the maximum the device could hold.

For each item, confirm the exact version and any essential audio or subtitle options. Offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. Use the [responsible offline-viewing checklist](/blog/offline-viewing-travel-checklist/) for the item-level preparation.

## Stabilise the device early

At least early enough to recover from a problem:

1. install current app and operating-system updates;
2. restart the device after updates;
3. confirm the intended account and profile;
4. verify that the screen lock works;
5. check account recovery details through official routes;
6. open the app once while connected;
7. avoid major configuration changes immediately before departure.

An update can change storage use, permissions, or sign-in state. Complete it before downloading the final set rather than during boarding.

## Build a realistic storage plan

Open the device storage view and record available capacity. Measure a representative eligible item rather than relying on a generic amount per hour. Keep working headroom for the operating system, temporary files, photos, and normal app activity.

If space is limited, remove finished offline items through the app first. Do not manually delete unknown app folders. The [offline storage budgeting worksheet](/blog/storage-for-offline-video/) shows how to measure real sizes on the device.

## Test disconnected playback and audio

After each priority item reports completion:

- enable airplane mode;
- confirm Wi-Fi has not been re-enabled;
- open the offline area;
- play more than the first frame;
- sample a later point;
- test required audio and subtitles;
- stop and reconnect deliberately.

Then pair or plug in the headphones you will actually carry. Test volume controls and any adapter. Do not assume a headset remembered by the device is charged or packed.

## Prepare power without promising runtime

Charge the phone or tablet and any permitted power accessory. Pack the correct cable and adapter. Reduce unnecessary screen brightness and background activity if comfortable, but do not publish a fixed battery-life promise: device age, temperature, display, radios, and workload change the result.

Run a short representative playback test and note the observed battery change. Extrapolate cautiously and keep a margin. Airline or transport rules can restrict certain batteries, so check the operator's current official policy for your journey.

## Control network fallback

Review whether the media app may use mobile data. Device-level low-data or data-saver controls can reduce background use, but effects vary by app. Test the current setting.

If public Wi-Fi is necessary, verify the venue's exact network name, use HTTPS, and avoid bypassing warnings. The [public Wi-Fi safety checklist](/blog/media-player-public-wifi-safety/) covers sign-in and session closure.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Updating the operating system on departure day.
- Filling all available storage.
- Testing downloads while connectivity remains active.
- Forgetting headphones, adapters, or charging cables.
- Assuming one episode proves the whole season is ready.
- Relying on a fixed battery estimate from another device.
- Leaving mobile-data fallback unreviewed.
- Depending on an unfamiliar public network for last-minute recovery.

Device and transport rules vary. Verify the current platform and carrier settings on the actual equipment.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I keep Wi-Fi on during the journey?

That depends on the journey and your connection plan. If you do not need it, disabling unused radios can prevent accidental connections. Verify any transport-specific requirements.

### How much free storage should remain?

There is no universal reserve. Measure planned items and keep a conservative working margin appropriate to the device's other uses.

### What is the most important final test?

Open each priority item with both Wi-Fi and mobile data unavailable, then verify the intended audio and subtitle options.
