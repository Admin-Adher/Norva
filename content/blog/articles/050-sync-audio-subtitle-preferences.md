# How Audio and Subtitle Preferences Follow You Between Devices

> **In short:** Norva can retain your preferred audio and subtitle languages across supported devices. The preference is an instruction about what to choose when available; it cannot create a track missing from the connected source, selected version, or media item. Compare the same profile and version when a choice behaves differently on another screen.

Language continuity reduces repetitive setup, but it is conditional. A profile can prefer French audio and English subtitles while one title offers only another combination. The preference remains meaningful even when a particular item cannot satisfy it.

## Distinguish preference from availability

Use two separate questions:

1. **What does this profile prefer?**
2. **What tracks does this media item actually provide?**

The first can follow the profile across supported devices. The second depends on the compatible source and media.

This distinction prevents three incorrect assumptions:

- a saved preference guarantees the track exists;
- every version has identical options;
- every episode in a season carries the same tracks.

For account setup, see [setting audio and subtitle language preferences](/blog/set-audio-subtitle-language-preferences/).

## Establish a controlled baseline

On Device A:

1. confirm the intended account and profile;
2. review the saved audio and subtitle preferences;
3. choose a familiar title whose options are known from its details;
4. open the available track controls;
5. note which options are selected.

On Device B:

1. use the same account and profile;
2. open the same title and version;
3. compare available tracks;
4. note the selected audio and subtitle options;
5. avoid changing them until the comparison is complete.

Do not infer a guaranteed sync time. Verify connectivity and observe the current state.

## Use the preference-versus-availability matrix

Choose three titles:

- one known to expose the preferred options;
- one with several language choices;
- one with limited or uncertain options.

The matrix produces evidence about your own source and profile. It does not claim that the chosen tracks exist for any particular title.

## When the audio preference is not applied

Check:

- same profile on both screens;
- same title, episode, and version;
- preferred track listed in the current media options;
- another manual selection was not made during this session;
- device can connect;
- source metadata has not changed.

Use [audio-language filters for browsing](/blog/audio-language-library-filters/) to narrow candidates, then confirm the actual track at title or playback level.

## When the subtitle preference is not applied

Check whether:

- the text track exists for this exact item;
- the selected version differs;
- the source uses a different language label;
- another profile is active;
- subtitles were manually turned off for the session;
- an episode has different options from the previous one.

The [subtitle-language filter workflow](/blog/subtitle-language-library-filters/) explains the candidate-to-confirmed sequence.

## Shared household considerations

A single profile has one current preference context. If household members need distinct defaults, separate profiles may reduce repeated switching. Norva includes up to two profiles; Norva Family includes up to five.

Those numbers describe profiles, not guaranteed device or simultaneous-playback limits. Check current plan terms on the official pricing page before making an account decision.

## A practical cross-screen routine

For a multilingual session:

1. choose the correct profile;
2. filter the catalogue by the essential language need;
3. open the exact version;
4. confirm available audio and subtitle tracks;
5. play and verify the selection;
6. move to another supported screen only after the first state is clear;
7. recheck the same version before changing preferences.

If playback moves between mobile and TV, use [the cross-screen handoff workflow](/blog/start-mobile-finish-tv/).

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating preference as proof of availability.
- Comparing different profiles.
- Comparing two versions with different track sets.
- Assuming all episodes share one language configuration.
- Changing several preferences during diagnosis.
- Leaving the device offline and expecting another screen's latest state.
- Inferring language from artwork, synopsis, or interface locale.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will Norva always select my preferred audio language?

The preference can be retained across supported devices, but the chosen source version and media item must provide that track. Confirm the actual options.

### Can subtitle and audio preferences be different languages?

They are separate choices. Whether a particular combination can be used depends on the audio and text tracks available for that media.

### Why does one episode behave differently from the previous one?

Track availability can vary by media item. Inspect that episode's options rather than assuming the whole season has identical language metadata.
