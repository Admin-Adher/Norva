# How to Sign In on a Temporary Accommodation TV More Safely

> **In short:** Use a temporary TV only with the owner's permission and when the model is supported, the Norva app came from the television's official app channel, and the sign-in destination is independently verified. Prefer an official device-authorization flow when the current app offers one, avoid exposing passwords to bystanders, record the session, and confirm the sign-out path before entering any account information.

A hotel or rental television is not under the traveller's long-term control. The next guest, host, property manager, device administrator, or reset process may encounter whatever the session leaves behind. Sometimes the safest decision is to use a personal device instead.

## Decide whether the television is suitable

Confirm that guests are allowed to use installed apps or install an app. Do not change owner settings, factory-reset the TV, attach storage, or add a personal system account without permission.

Check Norva's current supported-device information. Availability on TV does not mean every television model, operating system, region, or accommodation configuration is compatible. If the device is unsupported or heavily customized, stop rather than trying unrelated packages or websites.

## Verify the official app

Use only the TV's official app distribution channel and verify the publisher, app name, and current Norva documentation. Do not sideload software, accept an app sent by a stranger, or follow a search advertisement claiming to unlock compatibility.

If Norva is already installed, inspect its visible account state before proceeding. A prior guest may still be signed in. Do not explore their library; notify the accommodation and use another device.

## Verify every sign-in destination

If the current official app shows a QR code, short code, or web address, inspect the destination on your own trusted device before entering credentials. Follow the [official sign-in destination guide](/blog/verify-official-sign-in-destination/). A QR code is only a transport mechanism; it does not prove the page is genuine.

Prefer a device-authorization flow only when the official app currently offers it. Do not state or assume that Norva uses a particular code flow on every TV.

## Control the network and surroundings

Confirm the accommodation network with appropriate staff or host. Keep the television screen and your phone away from passers-by while codes or account identifiers are visible. Do not enter authorized-source credentials into an unfamiliar portal or support chat.

The [hotel check-in privacy plan](/blog/hotel-check-in-device-privacy-plan/) helps verify the network and room-device relationships. CISA travel guidance recommends protecting devices and using care with public wireless networks.

## Plan the exit before login

Find the current app's sign-out or account-removal control and read the expected steps without accidentally changing another session. Set a checkout reminder and identify a second way to review trusted devices from your own phone.

Use the [accommodation TV sign-out checklist](/blog/sign-out-accommodation-tv-checklist/) as a required companion. If no reliable sign-out path exists, do not create the session.

## Limit the session

Use the intended profile, avoid changing recovery settings, and do not save credentials in the TV browser. Keep the session to the planned stay. Record the TV's property or room label, app version if visible, login time, profile, and expected sign-out time without recording secrets.

Norva is a software media player and organizer. Connect only a compatible source you own or are authorized to use, and follow current Norva and source terms.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming every smart TV is supported.
- Installing software outside the official TV channel.
- Trusting a QR code without checking its destination.
- Entering credentials while another guest can see.
- Creating a session before finding sign-out.
- Factory-resetting accommodation equipment without permission.
- Leaving checkout responsibility undefined.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a hotel TV safer than public Wi-Fi on my phone?

They create different risks. Evaluate device control, app provenance, network, sign-in destination, physical privacy, and the ability to remove the session.

### Should I use the TV browser?

Avoid entering credentials in an unfamiliar shared browser. Use the official app and official authorization path when supported and verified.

### What if I cannot find sign-out?

Do not sign in. Use your own supported device and contact official support for current guidance.
