# How to Set a Free-Space Reserve for Offline Media

> **In short:** Set the reserve before preparing media. Start with current free storage, observe how much normal use and pending updates can consume, and add a buffer for temporary app and system activity. Measure representative offline items, then stop adding them before the projected free space crosses your boundary. Review the reserve after real trips instead of copying one universal percentage.

A reserve is the space you intentionally leave unused by the offline batch. Its job is to keep the device practical for everything else, not to maximise the number of local items.

## Record the current storage baseline

Open the operating system's storage view and note:

- total capacity;
- current available space;
- the media app's footprint;
- other large categories;
- pending system or app updates;
- photos, messages, or work files likely to grow during the trip.

Apple and Android provide storage controls, but category definitions and recommendations differ. Use the current guidance for the actual operating-system version.

## Observe normal variation

Check free space at the same time on several ordinary days before a trip. Record changes caused by photos, messages, app updates, documents, and temporary data. The largest normal change can inform a conservative reserve, but it is still an observation rather than a guarantee.

Android's official troubleshooting guidance notes that some phones can develop issues when less than ten percent of storage is free. Treat that as platform guidance for affected devices, not a universal target for every phone, tablet, or operating system.

## Add known upcoming needs

The reserve should cover more than historical variation. Consider:

- an operating-system update you plan to install;
- expected photos or videos during travel;
- offline maps or documents prepared elsewhere;
- work or school files;
- temporary transfer data;
- emergency headroom when removing items is inconvenient.

Do not count cloud capacity as free local storage. A file in cloud storage may still need device space when downloaded.

## Measure representative offline items

Complete one eligible item and compare storage before and after. Repeat for a different duration or quality option if your planned batch varies. The [offline video storage guide](/blog/storage-for-offline-video/) provides a detailed measurement table.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. Offline availability remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

## Set and enforce the boundary

Use this planning relationship:

`projected free space = current free space - measured planned items`

Only continue when projected free space remains above the reserve. Recheck after each transfer because observed sizes and temporary data can differ from expectations.

When the next item would cross the boundary, stop. Use [limited-storage prioritisation](/blog/prioritize-downloads-limited-storage/) to choose by essential need, session fit, tracks, and measured size rather than lowering the reserve impulsively.

## Review after the real event

Compare the projected result with actual free space, whether any transfer failed, and whether the device remained usable. Increase the reserve when the plan was too tight. Reduce it only after repeated evidence and without compromising expected device needs.

The [offline storage handbook](/blog/offline-storage-management-handbook/) connects this reserve to cleanup, category distinctions, and multiple devices.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Copying a universal percentage without observing the device.
- Counting cloud capacity as local space.
- Ignoring update, photo, or document growth.
- Using expected instead of completed item sizes.
- Lowering the reserve to fit one speculative item.
- Deleting system or app-managed files manually.
- Expecting storage totals to refresh immediately.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is ten percent always enough?

No. Android mentions that threshold for some phone issues, but devices, systems, workloads, and trip needs differ. Build a reserve from your own observations.

### Should the reserve be a percentage or a fixed amount?

Either can be a reminder, but the chosen boundary should come from actual device use and known upcoming needs rather than an arbitrary format.

### Can I change the reserve during preparation?

Only after reviewing new evidence. Do not shrink it simply because another item is appealing.
