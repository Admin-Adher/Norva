# How to Remove an Obsolete Source Without Rushing

> **In short:** Confirm why the source is obsolete, who owns it, whether external billing remains, and which users depend on it. Record a privacy-safe catalog, category, version, progress, favorites, device, and local-download baseline. Check whether other authorized sources provide needed items and whether cases require evidence. Remove only the Norva source configuration through current controls, verify the remaining catalog and profiles, then handle cancellation, credentials, and records separately with the external provider.

Removing a connection can affect visible catalog structure even when the external source account remains active. Preparation prevents an unused setting from becoming an unexpected data-loss investigation.

## Confirm the reason and owner

Record whether the source expired, was replaced, belongs to a departing household member, is no longer authorized, or simply remains unused. Confirm the external owner and account administrator.

If authorization ended, stop using the source while preparing its safe removal.

## Check external obligations

Review the source provider's access status, billing, receipts, cancellation route, retention, and recovery. Removing it from Norva does not cancel or delete the external account.

Do not discard provider evidence before external obligations are closed.

## Record a catalog baseline

Use the [catalog-baseline guide](/blog/baseline-before-second-source/) to capture source label, categories, approximate counts, filters, grouping, known duplicates, and a small sample. Mark items or versions that appear only through this source.

Avoid a full private catalog export.

## Record progress and favorites impact

The [progress-baseline guide](/blog/progress-baseline-after-source-change/) captures a tiny sample by profile, source, version, and position. Note favorites supplied only by this source without duplicating complete history.

Do not promise that progress will move to another source or version.

## Review devices and local media

Identify supported devices with the source configured and eligible offline downloads or cached artifacts. Delete local media through current controls where authorization or household policy requires it.

A local download is not proof that continued source access remains authorized.

## Close open investigations

Preserve timestamps, versions, category deltas, and masked samples required by an open support case. Removing the source can eliminate the cleanest reproduction path. Coordinate with support before removal when the case depends on it.

Exclude credentials and full private addresses.

## Remove one configuration

Freeze filters and other sources, record the time, and remove or disable only the obsolete source using current Norva controls. Do not change grouping, profile, application version, or another source simultaneously.

Verify the expected label disappears and no unrelated source was changed.

## Verify remaining state

Check categories, counts, sample items, alternate versions, favorites, progress, and playback from remaining authorized sources. Classify results as expected, unexpected, or unknown.

If the wrong source was removed, use only a documented authorized reconnection path; do not restore from a leaked note.

## Close the external lifecycle separately

Cancel, transfer, retain, or delete the external source account according to owner decisions and provider terms. Rotate credentials if access changed or a former administrator knew them. The [maintenance audit](/blog/source-connection-maintenance-audit/) confirms no obsolete endpoint or role remains.

Define a rollback window before removal. Record who can restore the authorized connection, which non-secret settings must be retained, and how the household will recognize unexpected impact. Follow current provider and Norva guidance rather than inventing a duration. During the comparison, avoid unrelated filter, profile, grouping, or source changes that would obscure the result. If removal affects more than the documented scope, stop further cleanup and compare the baseline. A rollback plan cannot guarantee recovery, but it preserves a clear decision point and responsible owner.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Removing before identifying the owner.
- Assuming Norva removal cancels billing.
- Exporting the complete catalog or history.
- Promising progress transfers to another version.
- Forgetting local downloads and shared devices.
- Removing while a support case needs reproduction.
- Changing several sources together.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does removing a source from Norva close its provider account?

No such assumption should be made. Norva configuration and the external source account have separate controls.

### Will favorites and progress move automatically?

Do not assume so. Item, source, version, and profile identity can differ; record a small baseline and verify.

### Should I delete the credentials immediately?

Remove unnecessary access promptly, but coordinate owner recovery, external obligations, and authorized rollback before destroying the only valid recovery path.
