# How to Pair a TV With Your Norva Account

> **In short:** Open the official Norva app on a supported TV and follow its current pairing instructions. Approve the temporary request only through Norva’s official account flow, confirm the intended account and profile, and review trusted devices afterwards. Treat any pairing code as sensitive and allow it to expire if the destination looks wrong.

**Draft verification notice:** an identified reviewer must reproduce the exact controls and redacted screens in the current TV app before publication.

## Why television pairing exists

Entering a long password with a directional remote is awkward and can expose it to nearby viewers. Pairing lets the TV display a temporary challenge that is approved through an account session on a trusted device.

Norva’s privacy policy lists pairing codes, device tokens, and trusted-device records among the data used to connect TV and phone to one account. The exact interface still requires live verification.

## Before you begin

Prepare a supported TV with the official Norva app, access to the intended account, a stable connection, and a trusted phone or browser if the on-screen instructions require one.

Do not pair a TV you do not control unless you know how and when the account will be removed. Keep the screen outside recordings or public view while a code is visible.

The [first-session guide](/blog/norva-getting-started/) provides the wider setup baseline.

## Step 1: open the official TV flow

Launch Norva on the TV and navigate to its current sign-in or pairing entry point. Confirm the branding and app identity.

**Observable result:** the TV presents an official sign-in choice or temporary pairing instruction.

## Step 2: read the complete instruction

Use only the destination shown by the official app, and verify that it belongs to Norva before authenticating.

If a temporary code appears:

- do not photograph or publish it;
- do not read it aloud in public;
- do not enter it on an unrelated site;
- allow it to expire when anything appears suspicious.

**Observable result:** you understand where the request must be approved.

## Step 3: authenticate deliberately

On the trusted device, open the official destination and sign in if required. Confirm the displayed account and profile before entering or approving the TV information.

A browser may already contain another household member’s session. Do not assume it is the correct one.

**Observable result:** the approval screen refers to the intended account and a TV request.

## Step 4: approve once

Enter or confirm the temporary information exactly as displayed. Submit once, then return to the TV.

If the code expires, restart from the TV. Repeatedly approving an old code does not diagnose the cause.

**Observable result:** the TV opens the account experience or presents a specific error.

## Step 5: verify the TV context

Check that:

1. the intended profile or profile selector appears;
2. expected source sections begin loading;
3. no private code remains on screen;
4. a known title can be located;
5. sign-out or account controls remain reachable.

Pairing confirms the account relationship. It does not prove that every source item or format will play.

## Step 6: review devices

Open current account controls and inspect the trusted-device list. Confirm the new TV appears in a recognisable way and no unknown device was added.

Use [How to Review and Remove Trusted Devices](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) after selling, returning, or losing control of a TV.

## Diagnose a failed pairing

Classify the visible symptom:

- No instruction: confirm connection and restart the official app.
- Code rejected: check transcription and expiration.
- Wrong account: sign out on the approving device and begin again.
- TV remains waiting: avoid repeated approvals; restart the TV flow.
- Paired but library empty: investigate source connection separately.

Record device type, app version, time, and a redacted error. Contact [Norva support](https://norva.tv/support) without sharing a live code.

## Pairing does not make an untrusted TV safe

The code reduces password entry; it does not protect an account from someone with continued physical access to the paired device. Sign out and remove the device when it leaves your control.

The remote-focused design context is explained in [Why TV Interfaces Need Different Design From Phone Apps](/blog/tv-interface-vs-phone-interface/).

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I reuse a pairing code?

Treat it as temporary and single-purpose. Start a new TV flow after expiration or failure.

### Should I send the code to support?

No. Share only a redacted error and necessary device context through the official route.

### Does pairing download media?

No. Pairing links the account. Offline access is a separate conditional feature.
