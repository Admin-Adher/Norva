# How to Review Favorites on a Larger Touch Screen

> **In short:** Review favourites one screenful at a time, under the correct profile, and place each item into one of four temporary decisions: keep, watch soon, verify, or remove. Confirm title and version before changing the list, and avoid starting playback merely to inspect an item. The tablet's larger grid helps comparison only when context stays stable.

A long favourites list stops being useful when saved items no longer represent genuine intent. A tablet can show more neighbouring cards than a phone, making patterns easier to see, but it also increases the chance of tapping the wrong overlaid control.

## Protect the profile context

Before opening favourites, confirm the active account and profile. Norva can preserve favourites, progress, history, and preferences under the same account on supported devices. That continuity means a tablet change may appear elsewhere, so treat removal as a real library decision.

On a guest or shared tablet, follow the [guest-viewing boundary](/blog/how-to-prepare-a-tablet-for-guest-viewing-without-mixing-profiles/) before editing anything.

## Define the four decisions

Use these temporary categories:

- **Keep:** still relevant, correctly identified, and worth retaining.
- **Watch soon:** a small set with a realistic near-term plan.
- **Verify:** identity, version, availability, or household ownership is unclear.
- **Remove:** no longer wanted by this profile and confirmed safe to remove.

These categories need not exist as product features. They are a review method kept in a note or worksheet.

## 1. Review one screenful

Do not scroll while deciding. Identify the first and last fully visible cards, then review only that group. Read the title and a secondary identity field such as year, series context, or source/version label when available.

W3C guidance on consistent identification supports presenting repeated controls consistently, but you must still observe which icon means favourite in the current interface.

## 2. Separate detail review from playback

Open a detail page when a card is ambiguous. Check the full title, description, duration, season or episode, and grouped variants that are actually visible. Return without pressing play if the goal is list maintenance.

If return loses the grid position, record the last stable card. The [complete tablet workflow](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-tablet-viewing-workflows/) explains how filters and scroll landmarks preserve context.

## 3. Limit “watch soon”

A watch-soon group should be small enough to represent a decision rather than another backlog. Choose a personal limit based on actual viewing plans; do not adopt an invented universal number.

Add a reason or occasion: weekday episode, family choice, travel candidate, or finish this season. Remove the temporary label once the occasion passes.

## 4. Verify before removal

Before touching the favourite control, compare the card or detail title with the worksheet row. Let any grid animation stop, then activate once. Confirm the visible state changed for the intended item.

W3C target-size principles reinforce the value of controls that do not demand excessive precision. If the favourite control overlaps another action or is difficult to target, use the detail screen or document the issue instead of repeated taps.

## 5. Close the review

Stop after a logical batch. Confirm the remaining list still belongs to the intended profile and that no item began playing. If the tablet is shared, sign out or hand off according to the household routine.

Use the [tablet workflow checklist](/blog/a-tablet-viewing-workflow-checklist/) when the review is part of a larger planning session.

Apply no removal from the first pass. Review the “remove” rows a second time, then make changes individually. This two-pass method reduces decisions based on a cropped title or momentary mood.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid editing under the wrong profile, removing from a moving grid, treating artwork as identity, starting playback to inspect metadata, and turning “watch soon” into a second unlimited list. Recommendations or availability from the connected source may change; a removed favourite is not proof that the underlying item disappeared.

The interface may not expose batch editing, custom lists, or every metadata field. Do not claim those features without verification.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I remove unavailable items?

Not automatically. Verify whether the unavailable state is temporary, source-specific, or tied to a variant. Use the “verify” bucket first.

### Can household members share one favourites list?

They can choose to, but separate profiles are more appropriate when distinct preferences are wanted and the plan provides them.

### How often should I review favourites?

Use an event trigger such as the end of a season, before travel, or when the list no longer supports decisions. No universal schedule is required.
