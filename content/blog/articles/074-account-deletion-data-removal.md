# How Account Deletion and Data Removal Should Work

> **In short:** Account deletion should cover the cloud account and associated personal data, subject to documented legal exceptions. It is not automatically the same as cancelling a store subscription, removing a trusted device, or deleting offline files from every device. A complete exit checks all four scopes and keeps a non-sensitive record of the request.

“Delete my account” sounds like one action, but a cross-device service has several data locations and relationships. Norva’s Privacy Policy explains that account and usage data is kept while the account is active and deleted or anonymised after deletion, except for limited records that must be retained for legal obligations.

This article explains the scopes; it is not legal advice and does not promise an exact completion time.

## Scope 1: Cloud account and associated data

Norva provides two verified routes:

- in the application: Settings → Account → Delete account;
- on the Web: the dedicated account-deletion page.

The request should address account identifiers and associated usage data described in the Privacy Policy. Keep the confirmation or request reference, but do not store passwords or source credentials in your notes.

The right to erasure under GDPR Article 17 has conditions and exceptions. Therefore, “all data disappears instantly with no exception” is not a safe claim.

## Scope 2: Subscription and billing relationship

Deleting an account and cancelling a recurring subscription are distinct concepts. Norva’s Terms state that subscriptions renew until cancelled through the store or provider used to subscribe, and that the provider’s terms and refund rules may also apply.

Before deletion:

1. identify the provider used for the subscription;
2. review its current cancellation control;
3. save a non-sensitive confirmation;
4. avoid assuming account deletion alone stops future billing.

Do not promise a refund or universal withdrawal outcome.

## Scope 3: Trusted devices and sessions

An account can have trusted-device records and pairing relationships. Review them where current controls are available, especially if the account is being deleted because of suspected unauthorised access.

The [account-security checklist](https://norva.tv/blog/secure-media-player-account/) helps secure credentials during the exit, while the [cross-device data map](https://norva.tv/blog/cross-device-media-player-data/) explains why device records exist.

## Scope 4: Local offline media

Norva’s policy states that offline media is encrypted and stored on the device, not uploaded to Norva. Local storage therefore needs a local removal step through supported application or device controls. Uninstalling or securely erasing a transferred device may also affect local files according to the platform and policy.

Do not assume a cloud deletion request can reach an offline, lost, or powered-off device. Read [how on-device encryption protects offline downloads](https://norva.tv/blog/on-device-encryption-offline-downloads/) for the limits of local protection.

## Use the four-scope deletion map

This map is the original evidence element for the article.

| Scope | Action | Evidence to retain |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cloud account | Submit verified deletion request | Confirmation or reference |
| Subscription | Cancel through original provider if required | Provider confirmation |
| Trusted devices | Review and sign out where available | Non-sensitive device list |
| Local files | Remove offline media and erase transferred devices | Device completion check |

Complete every relevant row. A blank row is an unresolved scope, not proof that no action is needed.

## Before you submit the request

- Save any account information you are legally entitled and practically able to retain.
- Record the subscription provider.
- Review devices you still control.
- Remove local offline media you no longer need.
- Ensure another household member is not relying on the same account without understanding the effect.
- Use only official Norva pages and support channels.

Never include source passwords or private links in a deletion email or public screenshot unless an official secure flow explicitly requires them.

## After the request

Keep the confirmation and check the service response. If legally required records are retained, the provider should describe the basis and scope through its policy or response. If the request remains unresolved, use the official contact details in the Privacy Policy.

Account deletion does not change the rights or terms of the media source that was connected. It removes or anonymises Norva account data according to Norva’s policy and applicable obligations.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming deletion and subscription cancellation are identical.
- Forgetting offline files on a device.
- Giving away a device before signing out or securely erasing it.
- Expecting instant, exception-free erasure.
- Deleting the account before recording the subscription provider.
- Sending credentials through an unverified support channel.

The current policy and account controls must be rechecked before publication because deletion workflows and legal requirements can change.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does account deletion automatically cancel billing?

Do not assume it does. Review and cancel through the store or payment provider used to subscribe, following its current terms.

### Is every record erased immediately?

The policy says personal data is deleted or anonymised after account deletion, except for limited records retained to meet legal obligations. It does not promise instant completion.

### What happens to offline media?

It is stored locally on the device according to Norva’s policy. Remove it through supported local controls and securely erase a device before transferring ownership.

### Where can I request deletion?

Use Settings → Account → Delete account in the app or Norva’s dedicated Web deletion page.
