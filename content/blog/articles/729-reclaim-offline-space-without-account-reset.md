# Reclaim Offline Space Without Resetting Your Account

> **In short:** Reclaim space by removing finished or unnecessary offline items through the app, one measured batch at a time. Record storage before and after, allow the device report to refresh, and keep account actions separate from local-storage cleanup. Do not begin by clearing all app data, reinstalling, or deleting the account; those actions are broader, less reversible, and may remove useful evidence.

An account reset is not a storage-management technique. Offline media, temporary data, settings, credentials, and synced account information occupy different layers, so the safest cleanup starts with the exact local item you no longer need.

## Confirm what is using space

Open Norva's offline area and the device storage settings. Record current free space, the app's reported footprint, and the finished items you can identify. The totals may differ because the operating system can include app code, cache, settings, and other local data.

Read the guide to [app data versus offline media](/blog/app-data-vs-offline-media-storage/) before using broad platform controls. Labels such as cache, offload, delete, clear storage, and uninstall do not have interchangeable consequences.

## Rank removable items

Place local items into four groups:

1. finished and no longer needed;
2. duplicated on another independently verified device;
3. outside the next planned viewing window;
4. required soon or retained as a justified fallback.

Start with the first group. If an item belongs to another household member, confirm its owner and profile before removal. The [offline download removal guide](/blog/remove-offline-downloads/) provides a focused item-level sequence.

## Remove one batch through the app

Select the exact item, read the action label and confirmation, and remove it using the app's supported control. Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and stored on the device. Do not browse for or manipulate app-managed encrypted files directly.

After removal:

- confirm the item no longer appears available offline;
- wait for the operating system's storage display to refresh;
- record the new free-space reading;
- verify that another required offline item still works;
- stop when the chosen reserve has been restored.

Small batches preserve cause and effect. If several categories change at once, you cannot know what reclaimed the space.

## Keep account state out of the cleanup

Signing out, resetting an account, deleting account data, clearing all application storage, or reinstalling can affect credentials, preferences, local media, or other state. Norva's privacy policy says downloaded media is removed when the application is uninstalled. That makes reinstallation a destructive step, not a harmless refresh.

If the measured app footprint still looks unexplained after item-level removal, use the [unexpected storage diagnostic](/blog/find-unexpected-offline-storage-use/) and current device guidance. Capture evidence before considering a broader action.

## Rebuild only what the plan requires

Once the reserve is restored, do not immediately refill it. Recalculate the remaining budget, identify the next viewing window, and prepare only eligible items with a named owner and review date. This prevents cleanup from becoming a repeating emergency.

Add the device, app version, profile, and expected reserve. If the storage change is much smaller or larger than expected, label it unexplained rather than inventing a cause.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Deleting the account to solve a device-space problem.
- Clearing all app storage to remove one item.
- Reinstalling before recording the offline library.
- Manipulating encrypted local files outside supported controls.
- Removing another person's batch without checking ownership.
- Judging success before the storage report refreshes.
- Refilling the restored reserve immediately.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will removing a local item delete my account?

Item-level offline removal and account deletion are different actions. Read the exact confirmation presented by the current app before proceeding.

### Why did the displayed space not change immediately?

Operating-system storage reports can update on their own schedule. Wait, refresh the view, and compare a second reading before escalating.

### Is reinstalling a safe cleanup shortcut?

No. Norva's privacy policy says uninstalling removes downloaded media. Record your state and use targeted controls before considering a reinstall for a separately diagnosed issue.
