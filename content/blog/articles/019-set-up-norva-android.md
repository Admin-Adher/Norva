# How to Set Up Norva on an Android Phone or Tablet

> **In short:** Follow Norva’s official site to its verified app destination, confirm the publisher identity, and install only on a supported Android device. Sign in, connect one compatible source you are authorised to use, and test one known item plus one account-level feature before configuring offline or data options.

**Draft verification notice:** the official listing URL, supported Android versions, exact labels, and screenshots must be verified by an identified human.

## Verify the app before installation

Do not select an app solely because its name or icon looks familiar. Begin at [norva.tv](https://norva.tv/) and follow the current official destination.

Check the app name, publisher identity, linked privacy policy, device requirements, and consistency with Norva’s site. Avoid unverified packages or instructions that ask you to disable platform protections.

Google’s Play Protect documentation explains that Android can scan apps and warn about potentially harmful software. That protection does not replace verifying publisher identity.

## Prepare the device

Use a supported device you control. Install current security updates within the manufacturer’s support, confirm sufficient storage, and connect through a trusted network.

Prepare your account, one authorised source, and one reference item. On a shared Android user profile, understand who can open the app and view account data.

## Step 1: install and review prompts

Install from the verified official listing and open the app from the launcher. Read permission prompts in context.

Grant only permissions explained by the feature you intend to use. Denying a permission may limit a feature, but broad access should not be accepted without understanding it.

**Observable result:** the official Norva account flow opens without an unexpected installer or overlay.

## Step 2: sign in deliberately

Authenticate through Norva and confirm the intended profile. Norva’s privacy policy says account information is used to create and secure the account and that supported devices can share progress and preferences.

**Observable result:** the expected account context appears.

## Step 3: connect one source

Use the current source controls and follow [How to Connect a Compatible Media Source to Norva](/blog/connect-compatible-media-source-norva/).

Keep private settings out of screenshots, public support posts, and untrusted clipboard-history tools.

**Observable result:** catalogue loading begins or a specific error appears.

## Step 4: check mobile browsing

Locate the reference item and inspect:

- title and identity;
- category;
- source-supplied description or artwork;
- available audio or subtitle labels;
- touch targets and scrolling;
- account profile.

Do not expect the layout to match TV geometry. Meaning should remain coherent while controls suit touch.

## Step 5: test one playback path

Open the item, choose only available options, and begin briefly. Check audio, video, controls, and return behaviour.

If it fails, preserve item identity, test one other known item, record device and app version, and avoid clearing all app data before collecting evidence.

## Step 6: verify one account state

Save one favourite or progress point. Confirm it remains after returning to the library. If another supported screen is ready, compare the same account state there.

Use the [first-session guide](/blog/norva-getting-started/) for a controlled baseline. The planned [travel preparation guide](/blog/phone-tablet-long-journey-setup/) covers offline planning.

## Delay large offline actions

Before downloading anything, confirm that device, source, and associated rights permit offline access, that storage is sufficient, and that you understand removal controls.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device rather than uploaded to Norva. It does not promise that every item can be downloaded.

## Secure the device after setup

Use appropriate screen locking, keep platform security active, and review trusted devices. Before selling or giving away the device, sign out, remove account access, and follow the manufacturer’s secure-reset instructions.

Account deletion and local app removal are different actions.

## Limitations

The fact baseline does not include an official listing URL or supported-version matrix, so publication is blocked pending verification.

Hardware, source, network, media format, and rights can affect playback and offline access. One phone test does not prove universal Android or tablet support.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I install any app that uses the Norva name?

No. Follow Norva’s official site and verify publisher and privacy links.

### Does sign-in automatically download media?

No. Sign-in establishes account context. Offline access is separate and conditional.

### Why add only one source initially?

It keeps category, metadata, and error diagnosis tied to a known origin.
