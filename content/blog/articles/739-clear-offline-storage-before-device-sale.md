# Clear Offline Storage Before Selling a Device

> **In short:** Before selling or giving away a device, record the offline library and required tracks, complete the device maker's supported backup or transfer process, and verify the replacement device. Then review Norva and authorized-source access, remove the old device where appropriate, and follow the manufacturer's full sign-out and erase instructions. Deleting individual offline items alone does not prepare the entire device for transfer.

A phone or tablet contains account sessions, messages, photos, authentication data, saved networks, and other information beyond local media. The sale plan must cover the whole device while preserving only the records the owner still needs.

## Stop adding new local items

Once the transfer date is set, freeze the old device's offline plan. Record current items, versions, audio, subtitles, profiles, progress context, and verification dates using the [pre-device-change inventory](/blog/record-offline-library-before-device-change/).

Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and stored on the device. The inventory helps rebuild eligible items later; it is not an export of the encrypted media.

## Back up or transfer supported data

Follow the current device maker's instructions for backup or direct transfer. Confirm completion and review which categories the process supports. Do not promise that app-managed offline media will migrate simply because account data or settings do.

Keep the old device until the new one has passed basic checks. If no replacement device exists, preserve the non-sensitive inventory in a secure location that will remain accessible after erasure.

## Verify the replacement device

On the supported replacement device, confirm:

- operating system and app are current enough for the intended setup;
- the correct Norva account and profile are available;
- the compatible authorized media source reconnects through supported controls;
- progress and preferences appear as expected after synchronization;
- required audio and subtitles can be selected;
- any newly prepared eligible item passes without connectivity.

Local media must be verified on the new device. A successful sign-in does not prove that offline files transferred.

## Review access before erasing

Use the [trusted-device review](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) to decide whether the old device should be removed from account lists. Review [authorized-source credentials](/blog/protect-media-source-credentials/) and rotate them if exposure or shared access creates a reason, but do not record those credentials in the handoff checklist.

Sign out of services or disable device-protection features in the sequence required by the manufacturer. Platform instructions may differ by model, account type, carrier, and operating system.

## Erase the whole device through official controls

Removing offline items inside Norva can reclaim local space, but it does not remove email, photos, saved sessions, or other apps. Use the manufacturer-supported erase or factory-reset process after backup, transfer, and account checks are complete.

Follow current instructions for SIMs, eSIMs, paired devices, and activation or device-lock features. When the erase finishes, verify the device returns to its initial setup screen before handoff. Do not sign back into personal accounts for the buyer.

## Keep a minimal completion record

Record the date, device identifier fragment, official process followed, backup or transfer confirmation, trusted-device review, erase completion, and recipient handoff. Avoid storing serial numbers with account credentials in the same place.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Deleting offline items and calling the device ready for sale.
- Erasing before verifying the replacement or backup.
- Assuming local media transferred with account state.
- Forgetting trusted-device and authorized-source access.
- Following instructions for the wrong platform version.
- Leaving personal SIM or paired-device state unreviewed.
- Signing back in after the final erase.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is uninstalling Norva enough before a sale?

No. Norva says uninstalling removes downloaded media, but other personal data on the device remains. Follow the maker's whole-device transfer and erase process.

### Should I remove each offline item first?

You may clean up the app inventory, but that is not a substitute for the official device erase. Preserve the needed record before either action.

### When can I hand over the device?

After required transfer checks, account reviews, official erase, and confirmation that the device shows its initial setup state.
