# How to Transfer Household Account Administration Responsibly

> **In short:** Map every responsibility before the handoff: Norva sign-in and recovery, subscription provider, receipts, compatible source, profiles, supported devices, privacy reviews, and support cases. Check whether each service supports an email, owner, organizer, or payment change; do not assume one account can be transferred. Create independent credentials instead of sharing passwords. Verify the administrator's access and recovery, then remove the former administrator only after billing, source, device, and evidence checks succeed.

Administration is a set of duties, not one universal owner switch. The Norva account, source, store, email, and device platform can each follow different rules.

## Define the reason and deadline

Record whether the handoff follows a household move, billing change, long-term absence, separation, accessibility need, or routine responsibility rotation. Set a deadline and identify the current and future administrator.

For sensitive household circumstances, prioritize personal safety and seek appropriate professional guidance rather than using a generic checklist alone.

## Inventory every system

List the Norva account, recovery email, billing provider, purchase account, connected source, shared televisions, personal devices, profiles, receipts, reminders, and support cases. The [annual account audit](/blog/annual-account-subscription-audit/) provides a reusable inventory.

Do not put passwords, recovery codes, source credentials, or card details in the register.

## Check what can actually change

Review current official controls for email update, display name, plan, provider, store family administration, source ownership, and device access. Norva support should clarify current options when a contact or account owner field cannot be changed.

The [email-update preflight](/blog/prepare-account-email-update/) prevents an accidental duplicate during a supported email change.

## Avoid credential sharing

Do not hand over a password manager vault, shared password, one-time code, or recovery code as the transfer method. Where a service supports role delegation or family controls, use them. Otherwise, change the account contact and credentials through the official path with the current owner present.

After any credential change, review active sessions and devices.

## Transfer billing responsibility separately

The original provider controls subscription billing. A Norva email change may not move a store purchase. Decide whether the existing provider account remains responsible or whether a controlled provider change is needed.

Preserve redacted cancellation and purchase evidence with the person legally or practically responsible for it. Do not promise that a subscription itself can transfer.

## Confirm source authorization

The future administrator must remain authorized to manage the connected source. If the source belongs only to the departing person, disconnect it and establish a new compatible authorized source through its proper owner.

Never copy source credentials into the handoff document.

## Verify recovery and devices

Test sign-in and recovery without revealing secrets to observers. Confirm expected profiles, entitlement, source connection, and device inventory. Remove old personal devices and local downloads through current controls.

If access fails, use the [account-lockout support packet](/blog/account-lockout-support-packet/) rather than bypassing verification.

## Close the handoff

Have both parties confirm responsibilities, current provider status, next renewal, source owner, device access, and storage location for redacted evidence. Record the completion date and schedule a short follow-up audit.

During that follow-up, verify that notices reach the intended inbox, the billing owner can open provider records, and the former administrator no longer receives account or source alerts. Correct residual access through each official service rather than sharing a workaround.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating the handoff as password sharing.
- Assuming one email change moves billing and source ownership.
- Losing receipts before provider status is verified.
- Leaving a departing person's device signed in.
- Transferring a source without authorization.
- Creating a duplicate account without support guidance.
- Assuming all services support ownership transfer.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I simply give the new administrator my password?

No. Use supported role, contact, credential, or provider changes and maintain independent recovery rather than sharing reusable secrets.

### Does changing the Norva email transfer billing?

Not necessarily. The original store or payment provider controls its own purchase account and subscription.

### When is the handoff complete?

After sign-in, recovery, billing, source authorization, profiles, devices, evidence, and former-access removal are all verified.
