# Paused or Abandoned? Decide What Belongs in the Resume Row

> **In short:** A paused title has a real owner, a reason to return, and a trigger such as a date, companion, device, or required version becoming available. An abandoned title has no remaining intention to continue. When memory or ownership is unclear, classify the entry as unresolved and review it later instead of deciding from age or percentage.

Older does not mean abandoned, and recent does not mean active. A deliberate pause can last months; a title started yesterday can already be rejected. The distinction is intention plus a realistic return path.

## Use five decision questions

1. **Who owns the session?** Confirm the profile or viewer.
2. **Do they still want to continue?** Ask for a direct yes, no, or unknown.
3. **What will trigger the return?** Name a date, event, person, device, or access condition.
4. **Is the correct version ready?** Check episode, edition, language, subtitles, source, and supported screen.
5. **When will the decision be reviewed?** Set an event or date for uncertain pauses.

A “yes” without a trigger is aspiration, not a plan. A “no” with confirmed ownership is an abandonment decision. Unknown ownership or version moves the card into investigation.

## Recognise a deliberate pause

Useful return statements are concrete:

- “Continue season 2 when the shared viewer returns.”
- “Resume on the supported TV after confirming the subtitle version.”
- “Finish during the next authorised offline trip if availability remains.”

The statement connects intent to conditions. Languages, subtitles, offline access, and availability depend on the compatible source, media, device, and associated rights, so do not promise that the trigger will automatically be met.

Place active pauses in the third tier of [the resume priority ladder](/blog/prioritize-active-resume-items/) rather than letting them crowd out the next session.

## Identify abandonment without judgement

Abandonment simply means the future action has ended. Reasons may include lost interest, a wrong version, changed availability, or a group decision. You do not need to preserve a detailed reason.

Before changing the row, verify exact identity and profile. Apply the available action to one non-sensitive entry only when its current effect is understood. Reopen the row and confirm the intended card changed. Do not assume history, favorites, or other profiles share the same state.

For a crowded queue, use [the fast four-lane triage](/blog/triage-long-continue-watching-row/) to separate clear abandonment from unresolved entries.

## Handle unknowns with an expiry question

When no one remembers, write the missing question rather than a guessed answer: “Which version was this?”, “Was this a shared session?”, or “Did the viewer intend to return?” Give the question a review event.

An expiry does not have to mean automatic removal. It means the household will decide again. At review, choose continue, release, or preserve because a condition is still pending.

DCMI metadata terms can supply the minimum identity record—title, identifier, relation, format, and language—without collecting personal commentary.

## Original evidence: decision card

Apply the five questions to six older entries. Record only owner status, return answer, trigger, readiness, and review event. Ask the relevant viewer to confirm their own cards. Note how many classifications change after confirmation.

The exercise demonstrates whether age-based guesses were reliable in that household. It does not establish a universal abandonment window or claim anything about the product’s ranking behavior.

Feed unresolved decisions into [the weekly resume-row routine](/blog/weekly-resume-row-review/) rather than reopening them every day.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Applying a fixed number of inactive days to every title.
- Treating high progress as proof of return intent.
- Removing another viewer’s pause.
- Keeping a title indefinitely without a trigger.
- Recording sensitive reasons in a shared sheet.
- Assuming a current row control has broader or narrower effects without testing.

The framework clarifies intent; it cannot repair incorrect metadata, unavailable media, or unsupported versions.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is an unfinished title a failure?

No. Abandonment is a valid decision when the viewer no longer wants to spend time on it.

### How long can a deliberate pause last?

As long as the return trigger remains meaningful. Review the trigger rather than applying a universal age limit.

### What if household members disagree?

Separate personal and shared intent. One viewer can abandon a title while another keeps their own active state.
