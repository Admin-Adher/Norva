# Close an Account Without Losing Cancellation Evidence

> **In short:** Before closing, identify the billing provider and save redacted proof outside the email or account being retired: provider, plan, cancellation date, resulting status, access-through date, and case reference. Cancel renewal through that provider and verify it. Then preserve lawful account records you need, disconnect the authorized source, sign out devices, remove local media, and submit the Norva closure action separately. Keep confirmations until both billing and account outcomes are verified.

Account deletion and subscription cancellation solve different problems. Completing them in the wrong order can remove easy access to receipts, notices, or support history.

## Identify dependencies before closure

List the Norva email, recovery inbox, original provider, purchase account, connected source, profiles, supported devices, local downloads, and open support cases. The [media-account lifecycle handbook](/blog/media-account-lifecycle-handbook/) provides the complete map.

Do not close an email inbox that still receives provider or deletion confirmation.

## Preserve minimal billing proof

Keep provider name, plan label, purchase and cancellation dates, exact status, access-through date, amount and currency where needed, redacted confirmation, and official help link. The [receipt-organization guide](/blog/organize-subscription-receipts/) excludes card data and reusable transaction secrets.

Store the record in protected storage controlled by the account owner, not only inside the account being closed.

## Cancel with the original provider

Follow the provider's current official flow and verify the resulting status. Norva's current terms direct subscription management to the original provider. The [cancellation guide](/blog/cancel-subscription-original-provider/) explains how to distinguish canceled renewal from access ending immediately.

Do not assume deletion, sign-out, or uninstall changes provider billing.

## Preserve needed account records

Before requesting deletion, review whether lawful profile state, support correspondence, settings, or proof should be exported or recorded. Use only current export controls and retain no more personal data than necessary.

Do not copy source credentials, session tokens, or other people's profile details into the archive.

## Handle source, devices, and local data

Disconnect the compatible source through current controls and review its own account separately. Sign out or revoke supported devices when available. Delete eligible offline downloads and application data from devices you control, then verify shared or sold devices are reset appropriately.

Closing Norva does not delete a connected source account or store history.

## Submit and document account closure

Use the current Norva account-deletion control or official support path. Record request date, case reference, confirmation channel, and exact outcome. Norva's current privacy notice describes deletion or anonymisation, subject to stated limited records; do not convert that into an invented deadline.

Keep the recovery inbox available until confirmations and any required verification arrive.

## Perform two follow-up checks

First confirm the provider shows no unintended renewal. Separately confirm the Norva account closure result through the official channel. If one remains unresolved, use the appropriate provider rather than repeating both actions.

After completion, apply a retention period appropriate to the evidence and applicable obligations instead of storing it forever by default.

If a confirmation can only be retrieved from the retiring inbox, export the minimal message before deactivation and test that the protected copy opens. Keep sender, date, subject, and result; remove unrelated conversation history and tracking links.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Closing the recovery inbox first.
- Treating account deletion as subscription cancellation.
- Keeping full card or receipt data unnecessarily.
- Forgetting a connected source or shared device.
- Assuming uninstall deletes cloud records.
- Promising exact deletion timing.
- Discarding proof before both outcomes are verified.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I cancel or close first?

Preserve evidence, cancel through the original provider, verify status, then follow the separate Norva closure path unless official guidance for your case says otherwise.

### What cancellation proof should I keep?

Keep provider, action date, exact status, access-through date, and a redacted reference without payment credentials or full transaction data.

### Does Norva closure delete my source account?

No such assumption should be made. The connected source is a separate service with its own controls and records.
