# How to Choose Titles for an Offline Weekend

> **In short:** Choose offline weekend titles from real viewing windows, not from the size of your catalogue. Include a useful mix of durations and moods, confirm everyone’s preferences, then check each exact item for eligibility, storage cost, required audio or subtitles, and disconnected playback. Keep one modest fallback instead of building an oversized queue.

A weekend can contain less viewing time than it appears to. Travel, meals, social plans, sleep, and shared-device use compete with the same hours. A selective batch is easier to finish, verify, and remove afterwards.

## Map the weekend into viewing windows

List likely sessions rather than total time away. For example, you might have one short journey, one quiet evening, and an uncertain return trip. Label each window as short, medium, or long without forcing an exact duration before plans are settled.

Use the [offline viewing-hours method](/blog/estimate-offline-viewing-hours/) when you need a more detailed calculation. Its purpose is to establish a ceiling, not a target that must be filled.

## Build a balanced candidate list

Start with more candidates than final selections, then reduce the list. Useful dimensions include:

- duration that fits one known session;
- whether the item needs uninterrupted attention;
- interest from the person or people using the device;
- variety in tone or format;
- whether continuing a series requires earlier episodes;
- required language, subtitle, or accessibility options.

Avoid choosing only long items or only one mood. One short alternative can rescue a changed schedule without creating a large storage burden.

## Apply the non-negotiable checks

Open the exact title, episode, and version. Confirm that an offline action is available now. In Norva, offline access depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. A poster in the catalogue, a favourite marker, or previous connected playback does not establish offline eligibility.

For every candidate that remains:

1. record its observed local size after completion;
2. confirm adequate device storage remains;
3. check the required audio and subtitle options;
4. test a section with connectivity disabled;
5. assign a date for review or removal.

If storage is tight, use the [limited-storage prioritisation guide](/blog/prioritize-downloads-limited-storage/) instead of deleting unrelated data impulsively.

## Choose the final batch by role

A compact weekend batch can use four roles:

| Role | Purpose | Selection test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Anchor | Main planned session | Fits the longest dependable window |
| Short option | Brief or interrupted period | Can be completed comfortably |
| Shared option | Joint viewing | Agreed by everyone involved |
| Fallback | Schedule or mood changes | Small enough to justify its space |

One item can fill two roles. The table is not a required count; it is a way to spot a batch made entirely of similar choices.

## Original evidence: weekend selection scorecard

Score candidates with words rather than invented precision:

| Candidate | Window fit | Interest | Eligible now | Tracks verified | Storage impact | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Strong / Weak | Shared / Individual | Yes / No | Pass / Recheck | Low / Medium / High | Keep / Drop |
|  | Strong / Weak | Shared / Individual | Yes / No | Pass / Recheck | Low / Medium / High | Keep / Drop |

Reject any candidate that fails an essential requirement, regardless of its other scores. This creates a traceable decision without claiming that one title is objectively better than another.

## Verify before the weekend begins

Run the [night-before offline check](/blog/night-before-offline-readiness-check/) after the batch is complete. Disconnect the device, reopen each essential item, sample more than the opening moment, and test required tracks. Reconnect only after recording the result.

The broader [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) covers battery, reconnection, and cleanup when the weekend is part of a longer disconnected period.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Selecting by artwork rather than a realistic session.
- Treating the weekend’s full duration as viewing time.
- Downloading several nearly identical alternatives.
- Ignoring other people’s preferences on a shared device.
- Assuming availability from a catalogue listing.
- Forgetting that individual episodes can expose different tracks.
- Filling the device so completely that ordinary use becomes difficult.
- Keeping the batch long after the weekend.

Plans change. The objective is a small resilient batch, not perfect prediction.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many titles should I choose?

There is no universal count. Choose enough to cover realistic sessions plus one modest fallback, then stop when the batch no longer serves a distinct window.

### Should I finish a series season first?

Only if those episodes fit the weekend and are individually eligible. Verify episode order and required tracks rather than assuming the whole season behaves identically.

### Can two people prepare separate choices on one device?

Yes, but confirm the active profile, storage budget, and ownership of each choice. A shared batch should make removal decisions clear afterwards.
