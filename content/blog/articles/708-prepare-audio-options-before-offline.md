# Prepare Audio Options Before Going Offline

> **In short:** Open the exact item and version, identify the required audio track, and record its label before preparing the offline copy. After completion, disable connectivity, reopen the item, select that track, and play long enough to confirm it. Account preferences can guide selection, but offline availability depends on the source, media, device, rights, and included local tracks.

Audio is easy to overlook because the preferred language may work automatically while connected. That prior success does not prove that the same track is present in a local copy or in another version of the item.

## Define the actual requirement

Write down what matters before opening the track menu:

- required spoken language;
- original or dubbed presentation preference;
- any descriptive audio need;
- acceptable fallback language;
- intended profile and listener;
- headphones, speakers, or accessibility equipment to be used.

Avoid labels such as “default” in your notes. Record the visible language or track description because defaults can vary by profile, item, or version.

Norva can retain language and subtitle preferences when the options are available. The guide to [setting account language preferences](/blog/set-audio-subtitle-language-preferences/) explains that connected preference layer.

## Inspect the exact item and version

If several versions are grouped, open the one intended for offline use. Start connected and inspect the available audio menu. Select the required track and play a short section to make sure the label corresponds to the expected sound.

Do not assume that another episode, film version, or quality option carries the same tracks. Languages and subtitles depend on the source and media. Offline access also remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

## Prepare the local copy

Complete the offline action while connected. Keep enough device storage free and wait for the app to report completion. If a quality or version choice is involved, verify that the completed item matches the one whose audio menu you inspected.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. That local state is separate from the account preference stored for cross-device use.

## Test without connectivity

Disable Wi-Fi and mobile data, then close and reopen the app. Find the item through the offline area and:

1. start playback;
2. open the audio menu;
3. select the required label;
4. listen through dialogue and another representative moment;
5. stop and reopen to see whether the intended choice remains available;
6. record the outcome.

If the required track is absent, the item is not ready for that listener. Reconnect and inspect whether another eligible version contains it. Do not treat a different track as equivalent without the listener's agreement.

Pair this check with [offline subtitle preparation](/blog/prepare-subtitles-before-offline/) when captions or translation are also essential.

## Plan a respectful fallback

A fallback should be decided before the connection disappears. It may be another verified item, another audio language the listener understands, or a non-viewing activity. Do not rely on finding a new track after departure.

Add the result to the [night-before readiness check](/blog/night-before-offline-readiness-check/) so audio is reviewed alongside storage, battery, account state, and accessories.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming a saved preference guarantees local track inclusion.
- Checking one version and downloading another.
- Inferring a whole season's audio from one episode.
- Testing while the device remains connected.
- Recording “default” instead of the actual track label.
- Ignoring descriptive audio or other accessibility needs.
- Treating a language badge as proof without listening.

Track names can be incomplete or inconsistent in source metadata. When the label and sound disagree, document the mismatch and use Norva support only after collecting the exact item, version, device, and observed result.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will Norva always remember my audio choice offline?

Norva can retain preferences when options are available, but verify the exact local item. Do not assume a connected preference guarantees an offline track.

### Can different episodes have different audio options?

Yes, availability depends on each media item and source data. Check every essential episode independently.

### What if the track label is unclear?

Play a representative section while connected, then repeat offline. Record what you heard and the displayed label rather than guessing from abbreviations.
