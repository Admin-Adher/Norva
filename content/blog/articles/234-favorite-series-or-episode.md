# Should You Favorite the Series or an Individual Episode?

> **In short:** Favorite the whole series when you want to follow, revisit, or keep the work discoverable over time. Favorite one episode when you need a precise reference, provided the interface truly supports episode-level favorites. Do not use either scope as a substitute for watched progress, and do not assume that a heart shown at one level automatically applies to another.

The right favorite is determined by the action you want later. “I like this” is too broad to choose scope. “I want to return to this show when a new season appears” and “I want to replay this specific interview episode” point to different entities.

Write the desired future action as a verb: follow, resume, compare, replay, recommend, or research. Then choose the narrowest entity that still enables it.

## Choose a series favorite for an ongoing relationship

A series-level favorite is usually appropriate when you want to:

- keep the show easy to rediscover;
- return when more episodes become available;
- compare seasons or variants later;
- retain interest after finishing the current season;
- group the work with related shows.

The series favorite should point to the series record, not whichever episode happened to be open. That distinction matters when metadata follows a Series → Season → Episode hierarchy, as reflected in audiovisual identification systems.

For a finished show that remains valuable, combine the favorite with [the completed-series organization method](/blog/organize-completed-series/) rather than leaving it in an active queue.

## Choose an episode favorite for a specific reference

An episode-level favorite fits a concrete target such as:

- a memorable standalone story;
- a tutorial, performance, or interview to revisit;
- an episode to share or discuss later;
- a particular version that needs comparison;
- a reference whose season context must remain visible.

Record series, season, episode number, title, and version. A title alone may be reused. If the current interface does not offer a verified episode-level favorite, keep a private note or list rather than pretending a series favorite will reopen the exact episode.

## Keep favorites separate from progress

A favorite expresses future retrieval or preference. Progress records viewing state. Completion means the viewer reached a defined endpoint. These concepts can coexist:

- favorite series, never started;
- favorite series, completed;
- favorite episode, partially watched;
- completed episode, not favorited.

Do not automatically mark a favorite as watched, or remove a favorite when playback completes. Norva may retain favorites and progress across supported devices under the same account, but each state still serves a different purpose and current product behavior should be verified.

## Check whether the favorite survives context changes

Test a harmless item:

1. save it at the intended scope;
2. leave the detail page;
3. find it again from the favorites surface;
4. check what opens;
5. repeat on another supported device if cross-device use matters;
6. remove the test favorite.

The result tells you whether the favorite targets a series, episode, or current version. Visible labels should make that purpose clear, a principle reinforced by W3C labeling guidance.

## Avoid favorite clutter

Add a cleanup trigger to the card. Examples include “remove after recommendation is sent,” “review at season completion,” or “keep permanently as a collection reference.” Without a trigger, episode favorites can become an unsearchable second history.

If you save a pilot only to decide whether to continue, use [the clean pilot-sampling workflow](/blog/sample-pilot-episodes-cleanly/). If the favorite represents a broader fictional universe, [map spin-offs and companion series](/blog/map-spin-offs-and-companion-series/) instead of attaching everything to one title.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Favoriting the open episode when the intent concerns the whole show.
- Expecting a series favorite to preserve a precise episode version.
- Using favorites as completion markers.
- Assuming favorite scope from icon placement alone.
- Keeping temporary episode references forever.
- Sharing a favorite without enough identity context.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can both the series and an episode be favorited?

Yes, if the interface supports both and each has a distinct future purpose. Record that purpose to avoid redundant clutter.

### Should a favorite disappear after completion?

Only if completion is your explicit cleanup trigger. Preference and completion are separate states.

### What if only series favorites are supported?

Use the series favorite for discovery and keep the episode identity in a private external note.
