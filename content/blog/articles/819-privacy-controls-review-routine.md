# Build a Routine for Reviewing Account Privacy Controls

> **In short:** Review privacy controls across the account, profiles, paired devices, connected source, application store, operating system, notifications, diagnostics, and recovery settings. Run the review on a schedule that fits your household and after meaningful events such as a new device, lost device, source change, plan change, major update, or account closure. Record dates and outcomes, not credentials, and verify sensitive actions through official interfaces and notices.

Privacy controls are distributed. A single application screen cannot normally manage records held by a connected source, device platform, store, or payment provider. A routine prevents those layers from being forgotten.

## Choose a schedule and event triggers

A quarterly or twice-yearly review can work for a stable household, but the right frequency depends on device turnover and risk. Event-triggered reviews often matter more: new television, shared tablet, lost phone, password reset, source replacement, subscription change, household member departure, or policy update.

Use the [media-player privacy basics guide](/blog/media-player-privacy-basics/) to understand which categories and actors belong in the checklist.

## Review account and recovery first

Confirm the sign-in email, unique password, recovery channel, active sessions or trusted devices when the product exposes them, and multi-factor authentication where supported. Do not claim a setting exists until the current application or official documentation confirms it.

Store recovery codes or backup information securely and never paste them into the review log.

## Review profiles and usage state

List current profiles, owners, age-appropriate settings where offered, favorites, history, progress, preferences, and household access. Remove obsolete profiles only after understanding whether their state can be exported, merged, deleted, or lost.

Profile removal, sign-out, and account closure are separate actions.

## Review devices and local artifacts

Confirm which devices are still used, which are shared, whether screen locks and updates are active, and whether local downloads remain necessary. Remove or revoke devices through official controls when available, then verify the result.

The [viewing-device security audit](/blog/viewing-device-security-audit/) provides a deeper device-specific checklist for televisions, phones, tablets, and browsers.

## Review source and store separately

Confirm that each connected source remains compatible, authorized, expected, and secured under its own account controls. Then review the original subscription provider for plan, renewal, cancellation path, payment method, and receipts.

Do not place source credentials, tokens, or transaction identifiers in the routine log. A simple "verified through official portal" entry is enough.

## Review platform privacy settings

Operating systems may expose application permissions, analytics sharing, notification previews, background activity, storage use, and device backups. These controls differ by platform and version. Compare current official platform guidance rather than applying one device's menu to another.

CISA recommends core account practices such as strong unique passwords, multi-factor authentication, recognizing phishing, and timely updates. Apply them where supported.

## Compare the policy and product

Check the current privacy-policy date, named provider categories, data categories, purposes, retention language, transfer statement, security claims, and contact channel. The [policy-change audit](/blog/audit-privacy-policy-changes/) provides a structured diff.

Use the [retention-language guide](/blog/data-retention-period-questions/) whenever closure or deletion wording changes.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Reviewing only application settings.
- Recording passwords, codes, tokens, or receipts in a checklist.
- Assuming uninstall deletes cloud or store records.
- Removing a profile before preserving needed state.
- Claiming a control exists on every platform.
- Ignoring policy and processor changes.
- Completing an action without verifying the outcome.

## Frequently asked questions

### How often should I run this review?

Choose a practical schedule and repeat it after important device, source, household, subscription, security, or policy events.

### Should the log contain account details?

Keep only minimal evidence such as date, owner, action, and result; never store passwords, tokens, recovery codes, or complete receipts.

### Can one setting control every provider?

Usually not. Account operator, source, store, device platform, and payment provider can maintain separate controls and records.
