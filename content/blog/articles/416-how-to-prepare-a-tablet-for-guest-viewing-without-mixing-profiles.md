# How to Prepare a Tablet for Guest Viewing Without Mixing Profiles

> **In short:** Use a separate profile only if one is actually available in the current Norva plan, verify it before handing over the tablet, and define what the guest may change. Protect account credentials, avoid unnecessary local items, and end with a sign-out or profile handoff plus a reopen test. Do not invent a guest mode that the product does not show.

Guest viewing should protect both people: the guest should not see private history or account details, and the account owner should not inherit the guest's progress, favourites, or preferences. The preparation depends on whether the tablet is trusted and whether a spare profile exists.

## Check the current account structure

Norva currently describes up to two profiles for Norva and up to five for Norva Family. Verify current plan details on the pricing page before publication or use. A profile allowance is not a device limit, simultaneous-playback promise, parental control, or dedicated guest feature.

List the profiles visible in the account. Do not rename, delete, or repurpose another household member's profile without agreement.

## Choose one of three safe routes

### Available separate profile

Use an unused or explicitly shared profile if the account owner approves. Give it a neutral name that does not expose personal information. Confirm that it contains no private favourites or history the guest should not see.

### No separate profile, trusted guest

If the owner agrees, the guest may browse under an existing shared context, but both people should understand that progress, history, favourites, and preferences may mix. Keep the session planning-only when that risk is unacceptable.

### No safe account context

Do not open the account. Offer another authorised viewing arrangement rather than sharing credentials or creating an undocumented workaround.

The [shared-tablet account routine](/blog/a-shared-tablet-sign-in-and-sign-out-routine/) explains the difference between device unlock, account sign-in, and profile selection.

## 1. Prepare the tablet itself

Update only through the device's normal process, check that the screen lock works, and remove private notification previews if the owner chooses. Do not disable accessibility, safety, or care alerts merely to create a cleaner screen.

Place one verified Norva shortcut in a clear location. Remove passwords, recovery codes, private notes, and unrelated open tabs from view.

## 2. Enter the approved profile

The account owner should sign in privately, then select the guest-approved profile. Before handing over the tablet, show the guest how to identify the profile and how to pause, return, and stop.

Do not reveal account credentials. If the device offers credential saving, follow the owner's policy and avoid saving secrets on a borrowed or temporary device.

## 3. Define allowed changes

Agree whether the guest may:

- start or resume items;
- add or remove favourites;
- change language or subtitle preferences;
- create eligible local offline items;
- switch profiles;
- sign out.

Available languages and subtitles depend on the source and media. Offline access also depends on device, source, and associated rights; eligible local items may persist on the tablet after viewing.

For browsing saved choices, the [tablet favourites review workflow](/blog/how-to-review-favorites-on-a-larger-touch-screen/) helps distinguish viewing from library maintenance.

## 4. Keep the session within the boundary

Ask the guest to stay in the approved profile. If an account, payment, source, or privacy screen appears unexpectedly, pause and return the tablet to the owner. The guest should not troubleshoot credentials or change plan settings.

Norva is software for organising and playing a compatible media source the account owner is authorised to use. A guest session does not transfer rights to unrelated media or sources.

## 5. Close and verify

At the end:

1. pause and note the final item position;
2. remove temporary eligible local items if agreed;
3. restore language or subtitle preferences when they were meant to be temporary;
4. return to profile selection or sign out, according to the plan;
5. close the app or browser session;
6. reopen once to confirm the intended privacy boundary.

The [complete tablet viewing workflow](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-tablet-viewing-workflows/) adds power, audio, and interruption checks.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid sharing passwords, assuming a guest feature exists, using another person's profile without permission, treating a profile as a security boundary, or forgetting local items. A guest can still reach account areas if the product exposes them, so supervision or a different arrangement may be appropriate.

Device controls and Norva features can change. Review the privacy policy, terms, and current plan information rather than relying on an old household routine.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Norva have a dedicated guest profile?

Do not claim one without a current verified product source. Use only profiles visibly available under the account and plan.

### Can a guest change audio or subtitles?

Only if the owner agrees. Preferences may persist, and available tracks depend on the source and media.

### Must the owner sign out after every guest?

Sign out when the device or next user is not trusted. On a trusted household tablet, a deliberate profile handoff may be acceptable.
