# Protect Local Media Data After a Device Is Lost

> **In short:** Protect personal safety first, then use the platform's official locate, mark-lost, lock, or erase process from a trusted device. Separate eligible Norva offline media from screenshots, downloads, browser files, removable storage, and synced account state because protections differ. Secure recovery, review Norva and each authorized source independently, and track every remote action as requested, pending, or confirmed. Local encryption is conditional, not a complete loss response.

“Media data” can refer to very different assets. A downloaded eligible title, a poster screenshot, a browser export, a personal local file, and viewing progress may not live in the same place or follow the same protection and deletion rules.

## Put physical safety first

Do not enter an unsafe location or confront a suspected thief. Contact venue staff, transport operators, a repair carrier, or local authorities as appropriate. Preserve the last known time, location, device model, and serial reference without publishing them.

Use the [lost-device response card](/blog/lost-travel-device-response-card/) to assign owners for the platform, carrier, recovery email, Norva, and sources.

## Use official platform loss tools

From a known-good device, follow current Apple, Android, or manufacturer instructions for locating, marking lost, locking, or erasing. Availability depends on prior setup, connectivity, model, account state, and platform.

Record what the service reports. A remote erase queued for an offline device is pending, not complete. Do not remove the device from an account if current platform guidance says that would weaken location or activation protections.

## Build a local-data inventory by category

Record categories, not content:

- eligible Norva offline media;
- other application or authorized-source downloads;
- screenshots, screen recordings, and support evidence;
- browser downloads and exported files;
- personal local media or documents;
- removable memory cards or attached storage;
- notification previews and cached account details.

Do not assume all categories inherit the strongest protection on the device.

## Interpret Norva offline protection conditionally

Eligible offline media may be encrypted and stored locally when the device, source, media, and rights allow. Confirm current Norva privacy and support information for the exact environment. That statement does not prove every local file is encrypted or that the physical device has received a remote lock.

The [offline-library cleanup guide](/blog/post-trip-offline-library-cleanup/) helps reduce unnecessary local retention before the next incident.

## Separate local data from synchronized state

Progress, history, favorites, and preferences may synchronize across supported devices. Synced account state is not the same as a file stored on the lost device, and deleting a local item does not necessarily delete an account record.

Use official Norva controls or support to understand current behavior. Avoid broad deletion during an active incident until the household distinguishes the desired local, account, and source outcomes.

## Secure accounts and sources independently

Follow the [phone-loss account guide](/blog/secure-media-account-after-phone-loss/) for recovery email, mobile number, password exposure, sessions, and trusted devices. Change a credential when it was exposed or reused, not merely because the device is missing.

Review each compatible authorized source at its own official destination. A Norva password or session action does not erase a source application or revoke that source's sessions automatically.

## Handle removable storage and backups

If the device held removable storage, include its format, encryption state, and data categories in the incident. Platform remote erase may not cover every removable medium; verify manufacturer documentation.

Check backups without restoring them onto an untrusted replacement. Retain only the evidence and data justified by household, source, legal, and privacy needs.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Risking personal safety to retrieve the device.
- Treating queued erase as completed erase.
- Assuming every local file uses Norva's conditional offline protection.
- Confusing synchronized state with local storage.
- Forgetting removable media and screenshots.
- Changing Norva while ignoring source sessions.
- Removing a platform device before reading current loss guidance.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are Norva offline titles protected on a lost device?

Eligible media may be locally encrypted when device, source, media, and rights allow; verify current documentation and still use the full platform loss response.

### Does remote erase remove synchronized history?

A device erase and account-level synchronized state are separate. Check current Norva and platform behavior before requesting additional deletion.

### Should I change every source password?

Only when exposure or reuse is plausible, but review every source's devices and sessions independently through its official destination.
