# A Safe Reconnection Routine After Travel

> **In short:** Return on a trusted network, verify device date and time, and record each profile's offline progress before connecting anything. Choose the device with the authoritative session, reconnect it first, and capture the resulting Norva account state and alerts. Bring remaining devices online one at a time, investigate conflicts without deleting evidence, review trusted access, and postpone local-media cleanup until progress and preferences are confirmed.

Reconnection is a state transition, not merely turning Wi-Fi on. Several devices may hold different local progress, changed time zones, expired travel settings, or temporary sessions. Connecting all of them together makes the result harder to explain.

## Secure the return network

Use a home or other trusted network whose administrative access, encryption, updates, and connected devices are maintained. The FTC recommends securing the router, changing default administrative credentials, keeping software current, and using appropriate wireless encryption.

Do not make a crowded airport or hotel network the default return environment just because the trip is ending.

## Freeze and record offline state

Keep all travel devices disconnected. For each one, record:

- device and app version;
- account and profile;
- item and last local playback position;
- local date and time of the session;
- required audio or subtitles;
- any error, pending action, or unusual storage state;
- whether the device was shared or temporarily signed in elsewhere.

Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and device-local, while progress, history, favorites, and preferences can synchronize across supported devices.

## Verify date and time

Check the device's date, time, and time zone against a trusted reference. Automatic settings depend on platform and configuration, so correct errors through current device guidance before interpreting timestamps.

The [time-zone viewing plan](/blog/handle-time-zone-changes-in-viewing-plan/) preserves origin and destination records. Do not alter the clock merely to influence app behavior.

## Select the authoritative device

Choose the device whose session should define the next progress state. This is usually the most recently completed intended session, but the household must decide from the ledger rather than assuming the newest displayed timestamp is correct.

Follow the [first-reconnection plan](/blog/plan-first-reconnection-after-offline/): connect that device, open Norva, wait for the current app state, and record what appears. Do not immediately seek, replay, favorite, or remove an item because those actions add new changes.

## Reconnect remaining devices one at a time

For each device, compare its offline record with the now-observed account state. Connect, observe, and document before moving to the next. If progress conflicts or a prompt appears, preserve both observations and contact support when the intended outcome is unclear.

Do not promise a universal conflict-resolution rule. Sync behavior can depend on timing, version, connection, profile, and product implementation.

## Review security and travel settings

Run the [post-trip trusted-device review](/blog/trusted-device-review-after-trip/). Confirm temporary TV sessions ended, shared networks are no longer needed, roaming and metered settings match home use, and no unexpected account alert or device appears.

If an account may have been exposed, use a trusted official destination, protect the recovery email, rotate the affected credential, and review related sessions. Do not send credentials to support.

## Delay storage cleanup

Keep local items until the desired progress and preferences are confirmed. Then follow the [post-trip offline cleanup](/blog/post-trip-offline-library-cleanup/) using item-level controls. Removing the evidence too early can make a progress or track dispute harder to diagnose.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Reconnecting every device simultaneously.
- Trusting an incorrect time-zone timestamp.
- Changing progress before capturing the first state.
- Deleting local media before sync is understood.
- Ignoring temporary TV or public-network sessions.
- Assuming one universal conflict rule.
- Sending passwords or source credentials to support.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which device reconnects first?

The household should choose the device with the intended authoritative session after comparing offline records, not merely the highest displayed time.

### How long should I wait before connecting the next device?

Long enough to observe and record a stable current account state. Product timing can vary, so avoid a fixed universal interval.

### When can I remove offline items?

After desired progress, history, favorites, preferences, and any conflict are understood and the cleanup owner approves the action.
