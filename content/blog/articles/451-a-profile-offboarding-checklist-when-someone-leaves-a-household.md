# A Profile Offboarding Checklist When Someone Leaves a Household

> **In short:** Treat profile offboarding and account-access offboarding as separate tasks. Inventory the departing person's profile state, devices, eligible local media, and account access; agree what should be retained; then use only verified Norva and device controls. Do not delete a profile, clear history, or reset devices until ownership, consequences, and consent are understood.

A departure can involve valuable progress and favourites as well as privacy concerns. A checklist protects both the departing member and the people who continue using the account.

## Establish decision ownership

Identify the account holder, the departing profile owner, and any shared-profile users. Agree who may approve changes. A profile is an organisational context, not independent account ownership.

Review Norva's current terms and privacy policy before handling account data. Do not interpret this workflow as legal advice for ownership disputes.

## Inventory the profile state

Record only what is needed:

- profile label and purpose;
- current favourites;
- active series or film progress;
- language and subtitle preferences;
- supported devices where the account remains signed in;
- eligible local media prepared for that profile;
- whether the profile is shared with anyone staying.

Avoid screenshots that expose private history unnecessarily. The [household profile guide](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-household-media-profiles/) explains which states profiles organise.

## Separate profile state from account access

Changing or removing a profile may not sign the account out on a device. Likewise, signing out a device may not remove the profile from the account.

For access, follow the broader [account offboarding routine](/blog/an-account-offboarding-routine-when-someone-moves-out/). Do not infer a remote session-management feature unless the current product visibly provides one.

## Decide retain, remove, or defer

Classify each state:

- **retain:** needed by a remaining shared context;
- **remove:** belongs only to the departing person and removal is understood;
- **defer:** consequence or ownership is unclear.

A deferred decision is safer than irreversible cleanup based on assumption. Do not attempt to transfer progress between profiles unless a verified control or support instruction exists.

## Review local media and devices

Offline access depends on device, source, and associated rights. Eligible items are stored locally on the device. Review those items on devices being returned or retained, using documented app controls.

Do not browse file-system folders or erase another person's device without permission. Device reset belongs to the device owner's official process.

## Use verified management controls

Before activating removal, read the visible consequence and preserve required state. If Norva does not clearly explain what profile deletion changes, contact support.

Avoid renaming the profile to “deleted” as a substitute for offboarding; that can retain private state and confuse household ownership.

## Confirm the result

After the agreed change:

- verify remaining profiles still belong to their owners;
- check that shared favourites and progress were not unintentionally altered;
- confirm intended devices have the correct sign-in state;
- remove temporary notes containing private data;
- record who approved the final decision.

Use [the profile quality checklist](/blog/a-household-profile-quality-checklist/) to review the remaining structure.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid deleting before inventory, treating profile removal as account sign-out, clearing shared history without consent, and assuming progress can be transferred.

Available controls and plan terms can change. This checklist documents a household process; it cannot resolve legal ownership, guarantee data recovery, or replace Norva support.

## Set a short verification window

Choose a follow-up date to confirm the profile decision, device access, and source responsibility still match the offboarding record. This catches a missed shared device without retaining a former member's activity longer than necessary.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should the profile be deleted immediately?

No. First identify ownership, shared state, device access, and the verified consequences of removal.

### Can favourites be moved to another profile?

Do not assume a transfer feature. Preserve a list when appropriate and use only verified controls.

### Does removing a profile sign out every device?

Do not infer that. Review device and account sessions separately through current controls.
