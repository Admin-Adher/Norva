# How to Keep Specials Clear Without Breaking Episode Order

> **In short:** Keep a special as a distinct special record with its own title, release date, parent series or season, and source identifiers. Preserve the regular episode sequence unchanged. Add release-order and, when verified, story-order placement as separate viewing cues with confidence and spoiler notes. If placement is disputed, show alternatives rather than forcing the special into a regular episode number.

Specials can be pilots, holiday programs, recaps, bonus episodes, web extras, or other supplemental works. Their role varies, so “Season 0” or a large number alone does not explain when or whether to watch them.

Keep blank or unknown values explicit. A precise invented episode number is worse than an honest unresolved placement.

## Preserve identity and parentage

EIDR's public hierarchy separates series, seasons, episodes, edits, and manifestations. Preserve a special as a distinct child or related asset rather than pretending every child follows the same numbered pattern.

The FIAF manual also supports hierarchical series and episode description. Link the special to its parent without altering the identity of surrounding regular episodes.

## Separate facts from viewing cues

Facts include title, release date, parent, identifier, and any official number. Viewing cues are recommendations such as “watch after episode 6” or “optional before season 2.” Store them separately.

This distinction allows several routes:

- **Release route:** place by original release date.
- **Story route:** place by verified narrative chronology.
- **Completion route:** collect nonessential extras after the season.
- **Selective route:** include only specials required for the viewer's goal.

No route should rewrite the canonical numbering.

## Add a spoiler boundary

For each cue, state the latest regular episode that should be completed first. Avoid describing the event that causes the spoiler. “After S1E6” is safer than a placement note that reveals a character outcome.

When resuming after a long break, [the series recovery card](/blog/resume-series-after-long-break/) should check for relevant specials between the last completed and next regular episode.

## Handle split seasons separately

A special released during a midseason break does not automatically belong to the first or second part. Use [the split-season mapping workflow](/blog/handle-split-seasons/) to preserve parent season and release windows, then attach the special's cue to the appropriate boundary.

## Verify the playable version

Record current source, runtime, audio, subtitles, and availability. A library can place a special correctly while still pointing to an unusable version.

Norva may organize series variants and compatible authorized sources, but source metadata can label specials inconsistently. Verify identity and placement against authoritative evidence before creating a route.

## Test the interface route

From the regular episode list:

1. Confirm regular numbers remain continuous.
2. Open the special through a clearly labeled section or cue.
3. Return to the prior episode without losing context.
4. Verify “continue” does not skip or force an optional special unexpectedly.
5. Confirm alternate placement remains discoverable.

For anthology programs, [the anthology series workflow](/blog/organize-anthology-series/) may determine that episodes are not narratively dependent; special placement should then emphasize identity and release context rather than invented continuity.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Renumbering regular episodes to insert a special.
- Treating “Season 0” as a complete explanation.
- Mixing release order and story order in one field.
- Writing spoiler details into the placement label.
- Forcing a disputed special into one precise slot.
- Ignoring source-version and track readiness.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should every special appear in the main episode list?

Not necessarily. It must remain discoverable and related, while the presentation can use a separate section or optional cue.

### Is release order always the best placement?

It is the easiest factual route to verify, but a story or completion route may better serve a declared goal.

### What if two sources number the special differently?

Preserve authoritative identifiers and aliases, document both schemes, and avoid rewriting the regular sequence to match one source menu.
