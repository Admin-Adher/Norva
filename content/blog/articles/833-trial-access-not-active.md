# Trial Access Not Active? Build a Support-Ready Check

> **In short:** Reopen the official offer and confirm that the trial applies to the account and purchase channel. Verify the original provider shows a trial or entitlement, then match the correct Norva account, selected plan, device, application version, network, and automatic clock. Record exact messages and timestamps. Do not start another trial, create another account, or send full receipts. If one official refresh does not resolve access, contact Norva with redacted evidence.

Norva currently advertises a seven-day trial, but eligibility, purchase-channel state, and final confirmation must be checked for the individual account. A landing-page description alone does not prove activation.

## Preserve the live offer context

Record the official URL, retrieval time, displayed plan, trial duration, payment requirement, price after trial, tax wording, and provider. The [trial-start checklist](/blog/seven-day-trial-start-checklist/) supplies the full pre-enrollment record.

Do not rely on an old screenshot if the purchase screen shows different terms.

## Confirm provider acceptance

Open the original provider's official subscription page. Check whether the trial, subscription, pending action, or error appears and which purchase account is signed in. The exact status wording matters.

Use the [billing-provider guide](/blog/identify-subscription-billing-provider/) if the household is unsure which store or payment account was used.

## Match the intended Norva account

Confirm the masked Norva email, profile set, and source configuration. An accidental second account may show no entitlement even when the provider purchase exists elsewhere.

Do not change email addresses or delete either account during diagnosis. Map the accounts first.

## Check device and application state

Verify supported device status, current application and operating-system versions, stable network, adequate storage, and automatic date and time. Restart once after recording evidence. If testing Web, compare with a supported clean browser session without disabling security controls.

Do not install unofficial packages, bypass certificate warnings, or expose credentials to a remote helper.

## Attempt one documented recovery action

If current Norva or provider guidance offers entitlement refresh, restore, or sign-in recovery, perform it once and record the time and outcome. Do not repeat enrollment or purchase.

The [subscription-status guide](/blog/subscription-status-not-updating/) explains how billing and entitlement can temporarily disagree.

## Distinguish access from source readiness

A trial can be active while a compatible source remains unconfigured or unreachable. Conversely, a catalog might appear while paid entitlement is not correctly reflected. Record which screen or feature is blocked and the exact message.

Norva does not provide media; the user connects a compatible source they own or are authorized to use. Do not describe a source error as proof that the trial is inactive.

## Build the support packet

Include masked accounts, offer and confirmation timestamps, selected plan, provider status, device model, system and app versions, network type, exact message, screenshot with sensitive fields removed, actions attempted, and desired outcome. Exclude passwords, codes, tokens, source credentials, card numbers, and full receipts.

The [account-lockout support packet](/blog/account-lockout-support-packet/) provides a compatible redaction checklist.

## Separate rejection from delayed display

Record whether the provider rejected enrollment, accepted it but shows a pending state, or confirms an active trial that Norva does not yet display. Those scenarios need different owners and evidence. Do not describe them all as an activation delay. If the provider rejected the transaction, resolve that provider state before expecting Norva entitlement to appear.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming every account is eligible for a trial.
- Creating another account or purchase.
- Confusing a source error with trial entitlement.
- Repeating restore actions without a trace.
- Sending full receipts or card data.
- Installing unofficial software to test access.
- Guessing a universal activation delay.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does the advertised trial guarantee eligibility for every account?

No. Verify the live offer, purchase channel, provider confirmation, and account-specific conditions before drawing that conclusion.

### Should I start the trial again?

No. Re-enrollment can create duplicate accounts or billing; preserve evidence and use the official recovery or support path.

### What screenshot is safe to send?

Show the exact status and timestamp after removing account, transaction, payment, token, source, and other sensitive details.
