# Secure a New Norva Account From the Start

> **In short:** Create the account only from the official Norva site or application, use a unique password stored in a reputable password manager, and review recovery options through the current account flow. Enable an additional factor only if the official flow offers it. Keep Norva credentials separate from source secrets, control shared-device sessions, and record safeguards without copying passwords, codes, tokens, or private addresses.

Account security is easiest to improve before habits spread across screens. The goal is not to claim perfect protection. It is to reduce predictable risks, preserve recovery paths, and keep the Norva account boundary separate from the compatible media source you are authorized to use.

## Begin from an official destination

Navigate directly to the official Norva domain or use the verified application already installed on a supported device. Check the domain before entering an email address or password. Be cautious with search advertisements, shortened links, unexpected messages, and pages that ask for source details before you understand why.

The [account preparation guide](/blog/prepare-before-creating-norva-account/) helps collect non-sensitive decisions before registration.

## Create a unique managed password

Use a password that is not reused for email, the media source, payment, or another service. A reputable password manager can generate and store a long unique value, reducing the temptation to create a memorable variation of an old password.

Do not place the password in a household note, browser screenshot, support ticket, or onboarding ledger. The security record needs only a confirmation that a unique managed password exists.

## Review additional verification cautiously

If the current Norva account or authentication-provider flow visibly offers an additional verification factor, evaluate and enable it from the official controls. This article does not assume that a particular factor is available. Store recovery codes, if any are issued, outside ordinary screenshots and chat history.

Never approve an unexpected prompt simply because it arrives while you are troubleshooting.

## Protect the recovery path

Use an email account you control and secure that mailbox with its own unique password. Review recovery information only through current official settings. Do not invent an answer, phone requirement, or recovery procedure that is not displayed.

If account access later fails, the [lockout support packet](/blog/account-lockout-support-packet/) shows how to document the problem without exposing secrets.

## Separate Norva and source credentials

Norva is a software player and organizer; the connected source remains a separate authorization boundary. Do not reuse the same password between them. Do not send source credentials to another household member through an unprotected note merely because both people use the same profile.

When entry fails, use the [credential-error guide](/blog/credential-entry-error-without-exposure/) rather than copying the secret into evidence.

## Control shared screens

Before signing in on a shared browser, mobile device, or TV, decide who owns the session and who may change account, source, or profile settings. Avoid saving passwords on an unmanaged device. Sign out when the screen leaves your control and remove temporary screenshots or clipboard contents.

Record the device category and session owner, not a serial number or household location.

## Recognize phishing and impersonation

Treat urgency, threats, unexpected payment requests, and requests for passwords or verification codes as warning signs. Open the official site separately instead of following the message link. Support evidence should describe the request and time without forwarding an active secret.

## Understand the published privacy boundary

Norva states that traffic to its cloud is protected in transit by HTTPS and that passwords are stored in hashed form by its authentication provider. These statements do not mean every device, source, local network, or copied note is protected end to end. Read the current privacy policy for the actual data practices and controls.

## Schedule a short review

After onboarding, confirm the official email, password-manager entry, recovery path, shared sessions, and source-secret separation. Repeat the review after a suspected exposure, household administrator change, or account recovery. If a source credential may have been exposed, follow the source provider's official rotation process.

## Common security mistakes

- Reusing an email or source password.
- Saving a secret in a screenshot or support message.
- Approving an unexpected verification prompt.
- Assuming HTTPS protects copied notes or the local device.
- Leaving an account signed in on an unmanaged screen.
- Recording recovery answers or codes in the audit log.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Norva definitely offer an additional verification factor?

This article does not make that claim. Use the option only if it appears in the current official account or authentication flow.

### Can one household password be shared by everyone?

A shared secret increases exposure and weakens accountability. Prefer controlled account administration and purposeful profiles without distributing credentials casually.

### What should I send support after a security problem?

Send a redacted timeline, affected account identifier as requested through the official channel, device category, and observed messages. Never send passwords, codes, tokens, or source secrets.
