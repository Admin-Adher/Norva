# Should a Title Stay in Favorites After You Watch It?

> **In short:** Completion does not automatically remove or justify a favorite. Verify the exact title and viewer completed it, then restate the future action. Keep it for rewatch, reference, recommendation, or discovery; move it when the remaining task belongs elsewhere; remove it when no future action remains. Preserve history and progress separately.

Before watching, a favorite may mean “consider this.” After watching, the viewer has new evidence. The list should reflect that change without treating completion as preference.

Do not start the decision until completion belongs to the correct viewer and version.

## Revisit the original intent

Use [the favorite admission card](/blog/decide-what-to-favorite/) to recover the original purpose. Common pre-watch reasons include trial, recommendation, anticipated comparison, or long-term interest. Ask whether viewing fulfilled, disproved, or transformed that purpose.

## Keep the favorite for a durable action

Keeping makes sense when the title now supports:

- a genuine rewatch intention;
- a stable personal reference;
- recommendation to a defined audience;
- future comparison with another version or related work;
- discovery of meaningfully similar titles;
- continued following of a series.

“I liked it” can be enough when the list is explicitly a record of durable preferences. State that list purpose rather than inventing a task.

## Move the item when another list owns the task

Examples:

- remove a completed item from next-up while keeping it in favorites;
- move a title needing discussion to a temporary reference note;
- move a related sequel into next-up without keeping the completed work urgent;
- add it to a themed shortlist for an upcoming occasion.

[The favorite-versus-next-up model](/blog/long-term-favorites-vs-next-up/) keeps these transitions independent.

## Remove when future action ends

Removal is appropriate when the original decision is complete and no durable purpose remains. Confirm the control removes only favorite state. It should not be assumed to erase or preserve history, progress, or source data without testing current product behavior.

After removal, leave the page and verify the title no longer appears in favorites while completion remains correctly recorded.

## Treat version-specific outcomes carefully

A viewer may value the work but not the watched version, or may want to compare another language or edit. Record the exact version. DCMI metadata terms distinguish identifier, format, language, relation, and type; these fields prevent a work-level opinion from being attached accidentally to every version.

## Use favorites as a discovery seed

When the post-watch action is “find something related,” follow [the favorites discovery workflow](/blog/start-discovery-from-favorites/). Keep the seed favorite stable while recommendations are evaluated separately. Do not automatically favorite every candidate.

## Respect personal scope

One household member’s completion or opinion should not change another’s favorite. Verify profile or viewer scope before removal. Norva may retain favorites and progress across supported devices under the same account; current isolation and synchronization should be confirmed.

## Make the action understandable

W3C label guidance supports controls that expose purpose and state. “Remove from favorites” should not be represented as “delete title.” When completion triggers a prompt, offer keep, move, and remove with consequences.

Before confirming removal, state which object changes: this version, the underlying work, or the viewer's saved reference. Then reopen the destination list to verify that any promised move actually occurred.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Removing favorites automatically at completion.
- Keeping every watched title as a preference record.
- Confusing completion with recommendation value.
- Ignoring work versus version scope.
- Changing another viewer’s favorite.
- Removing next-up and favorite state together.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can a disliked title remain a favorite?

Yes, if it serves a clear reference or comparison purpose. Label the list intent so favorite does not imply endorsement.

### Should a series favorite remain after one season?

Review the series-level future action separately from the completed season.

### What if I might rewatch someday?

Set a concrete review trigger. Vague possibility alone may not justify permanent placement in a tightly curated list.
