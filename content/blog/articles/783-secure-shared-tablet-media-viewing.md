# How to Secure a Shared Tablet for Media Viewing

> **In short:** Update the exact tablet and official media app, use a supported device lock, and separate users or guests when the platform genuinely provides that feature. Give viewers only the Norva profile and source access they need, hide sensitive lock-screen previews, minimize app permissions, and protect downloads. Keep recovery email and password-manager access outside ordinary viewing, define handoff and offboarding rules, and verify every control in current manufacturer documentation.

A shared tablet sits between a personal phone and a household television. It is portable enough to be lost, yet familiar enough that people may leave it unlocked with administrative email, browser sessions, and source applications available.

## Identify users and physical exposure

List who uses the tablet, where it travels, whether guests handle it, and who administers it. Do not place names or a home address in the device label. Decide whether it is a dedicated viewing device or also holds email, finance, work, or personal messages.

The more mixed the use, the stronger the need for separation and a shorter automatic lock interval. Use the [screen-lock timeout guide](/blog/screen-lock-timeout-viewing-device/) to balance exposure, accessibility, and viewing interruptions.

## Use platform separation where it exists

Some Android tablets support multiple users or a guest. Availability and behavior vary by model, manufacturer, management state, and version. Apple offers different controls depending on device and context; do not assume a consumer tablet supports a separate guest user.

Use the [guest-mode evaluation guide](/blog/guest-mode-media-viewing-where-available/) to decide whether a documented user, guest, app-locking, or guided-access feature actually reduces exposure. Test what remains visible across users before relying on it.

## Separate viewing from administration

Ordinary viewers should not need the recovery inbox, password-manager master access, platform-owner password, or private credentials for compatible authorized sources. Configure the media application through an authorized administrator, then provide the least access needed for approved viewing.

Do not send credentials in household chat. Use approved password-manager sharing only for administrators who legitimately manage the boundary.

## Apply locks and update support

Use a supported PIN, password, or biometric-backed lock under current platform guidance. Keep the operating system and media app updated separately. Record whether the exact model still receives manufacturer security updates.

Avoid easy-to-observe unlock patterns in a shared room. Biometrics can improve convenience but still require a secure fallback credential and clear household consent.

## Minimize permissions and notifications

Audit camera, microphone, photos, nearby devices, local network, notifications, and background access. Keep only permissions required for features the household uses. Follow the [media-app permission audit](/blog/audit-media-app-permissions/) and retest after revocation.

Hide sensitive notification content on the lock screen or disable unnecessary media alerts. Also review email, message, and password-manager previews; the media app is not the only source of exposure.

## Handle downloads and local files

Eligible offline media may be encrypted locally when device, source, media, and rights allow. Verify current Norva guidance for this exact tablet. Separate that conditional product behavior from photos, browser downloads, screenshots, and source-app files that may follow different rules.

Give guests no reason to browse file managers or galleries. Clean up temporary screenshots and verify that a completed download does not reveal another profile's history.

## Define handoff, travel, and offboarding

Before handing over the tablet, switch to the intended user context, close administrative apps, clear temporary casting, and lock it. After guest use, end the guest or temporary session using platform instructions and verify the administrator context remains secure.

Before travel, record the device in the household response card. When retiring or repairing it, remove media access and follow the manufacturer's exact preparation and reset process.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming every tablet offers a true guest user.
- Sharing the administrator unlock code broadly.
- Leaving email or password-manager previews visible.
- Mixing source credentials with ordinary profile access.
- Granting camera or microphone access without a used feature.
- Treating local media encryption as protection for all files.
- Ending guest use without verifying the resulting state.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a separate Norva profile the same as a device user?

No. A media profile organizes application experience; platform user separation controls broader device access when supported.

### Should a shared tablet have no lock for convenience?

No lock leaves portable account and local data exposed. Choose an effective method and timeout that also respects household accessibility needs.

### Can guests use offline downloads?

Availability depends on the exact device, source, media, rights, and current product behavior. Verify access and avoid exposing another user's local data.
