# An Account Offboarding Routine When Someone Moves Out

> **In short:** Inventory the departing member's devices, signed-in sessions, profiles, source responsibilities, eligible local media, and account recovery roles. Agree what belongs to whom, then use verified account, source, app, and device controls one layer at a time. Profile deletion is not account offboarding, and sign-out is not source-credential recovery. Verify the final boundary without exposing private history.

A move changes physical custody and household trust. The routine should protect the departing member's personal state while preventing unintended continued access to the shared account.

Set a completion owner and an agreed communication channel before changing access. This prevents the departing member, source steward, and device custodian from each making overlapping changes without knowing which state is current.

## Name the parties and scope

Identify the account holder, departing member, source owners, device custodians, and owners of any shared profiles. Record agreement and unresolved ownership.

This workflow is not legal advice. Disputed property, billing, or access rights require appropriate review.

## Inventory access layers

List:

- personal and shared devices;
- Norva sign-in state;
- profile ownership;
- source steward roles;
- saved credentials or recovery access;
- eligible local media;
- browser sessions on shared computers;
- shared-screen notification exposure.

Use [the trusted-device inventory](/blog/build-a-trusted-device-inventory-for-the-household/) as the device baseline.

## Preserve profile state separately

The departing member may want a record of favourites or progress where current controls and consent allow it. Do not promise profile transfer or export.

Follow [the profile offboarding checklist](/blog/a-profile-offboarding-checklist-when-someone-leaves-a-household/) before removal or renaming.

## Reassign source responsibility

If the departing person owns or controls a connected source, re-establish household authorisation before continued use. Do not copy or retain their credentials without permission.

If authorisation ends, pause active sessions and use the verified disconnect process.

## Remove account access through verified controls

Sign out household sessions on devices leaving the trusted group. Use current account or authentication-provider controls for password and recovery changes when legitimately required.

Do not claim Norva offers remote session revocation unless the interface or support verifies it. Contact support if a device cannot be reached or the session list is unclear.

## Review local media

Eligible offline items are stored locally when device, source, and rights allow. Remove shared-account local items from departing or retained devices as agreed, using documented controls.

Do not inspect or erase unrelated personal files.

## Verify the boundary

After changes:

1. reopen retained household devices and confirm intended access;
2. verify devices leaving no longer expose the shared account where possible and authorised;
3. confirm source responsibility;
4. review payment and contact information through the account holder;
5. update the inventory and agreement;
6. delete temporary notes with private data.

## Communicate completion

Tell affected household members which access paths changed and which remain. Do not disclose the departing person's private history or reason for leaving.

The [governance pillar](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) provides role reassignment guidance.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid deleting the profile first, assuming device removal changes source credentials, retaining a source without authorisation, and factory-resetting another person's device without consent.

Available account and device controls vary. This routine cannot guarantee recovery, settle ownership, or replace legal, source-provider, or Norva support guidance.

## Verify from a non-destructive view

After the official removal, check the household's trusted-device inventory and account access list without attempting to sign in as the former member. Confirm remaining source stewards, shared-device state, and recovery contacts; preserve only the completion record needed by the household.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is profile deletion enough?

No. Review account sign-in, devices, source roles, credentials, and local media separately.

### Should every password be changed?

Use legitimate risk-based and official recovery guidance. Do not rotate secrets indiscriminately or share replacements.

### What if a departing device is unreachable?

Preserve the inventory, use verified account controls if available, and contact support. Do not claim remote removal succeeded without evidence.
