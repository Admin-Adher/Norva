# How to End a Guest Handoff Cleanly on a Shared Device

> **In short:** Pause and record the guest's final item position, remove only temporary local items the guest created, restore settings that were meant to be temporary, and sign out when the device or next user is not trusted. Close the app or browser, reopen once, and verify that the guest account, history, and credentials are no longer exposed.

A clean ending protects the guest's account and the device owner's viewing context. It should not erase another person's history, reset the whole app, or rely on closing a window as proof of sign-out.

## Agree on the exit state

Before the session ends, decide:

- whether progress should remain with the guest profile;
- whether any favourite change was intentional;
- which language or subtitle settings were temporary;
- whether eligible local items were created;
- whether the guest account must be signed out;
- what the next user should see.

A profile is an organisational context, not a security boundary from anyone who can access the same signed-in account.

## Pause and capture the final state

Record item or episode, version, approximate position, audio, and subtitles. This allows the guest to resume later without leaving the shared screen open.

The [cross-device handoff state guide](/blog/a-state-by-state-guide-to-cross-device-viewing-handoff/) provides the full identity card.

## Remove temporary local items

Offline access depends on device, source, and associated rights. Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored locally rather than uploaded to Norva.

Remove only items created for this guest session, using the visible documented control. Do not browse app folders or delete another user's files. If ownership is uncertain, leave the item and ask the device owner.

## Restore temporary preferences

Return notification, orientation, audio output, language, and subtitle settings that both parties agreed were session-only. Do not override the guest's profile preferences if those changes were intentional and belong to that profile.

Use [the shared-screen privacy workflow](/blog/how-to-resume-on-a-shared-screen-without-exposing-private-history/) to review notification previews and account visibility.

## Sign out through the account control

Switching profiles is not signing out. Use the verified Norva sign-out route when the guest account should leave the device. Do not save credentials, recovery codes, or source access details on the shared screen.

If the visible sign-out path is unclear, contact support rather than deleting application data.

## Close and reopen once

After sign-out, close the current app or compatible browser session. Reopen the verified Norva route and check:

- no guest email or private profile is exposed;
- no automatic resume begins;
- saved credentials are not offered unexpectedly;
- the next user sees the agreed neutral state.

For a tablet, the [shared-tablet sign-out routine](/blog/a-shared-tablet-sign-in-and-sign-out-routine/) adds device-lock and home-screen checks.

## If the guest account remains visible

Do not continue browsing account screens. Capture only the minimum non-sensitive state, retry the documented sign-out once, and close the app. Contact Norva support if the session persists.

Never share passwords, source credentials, private URLs, payment data, or unredacted history in a support attachment.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid confusing profile switch with sign-out, deleting all app data, leaving local media, changing another profile's preferences, and skipping the reopen test.

Browser storage, operating systems, authentication providers, and device management differ. This routine reduces retained session exposure but cannot control a device owner with administrative access.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should the guest remove their profile?

Not automatically. A profile may belong to the account and household. Sign out the guest account or follow the agreed account routine.

### Is closing the app enough?

Do not assume so. Reopen once and verify the intended session boundary.

### Can the owner clear all history afterward?

Only through verified controls and with respect for whose data it is. Do not erase unrelated viewing activity.
