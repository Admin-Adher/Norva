# A Recurring Audit for Offline Storage

> **In short:** Audit offline storage on a regular schedule and after material events such as a trip, update, device change, failed preparation, or unexplained storage shift. Reconcile the app inventory with device storage, confirm every batch has an owner and review date, justify duplicates, test essential items without connectivity, restore the protected reserve, and carry unresolved differences into a named follow-up action.

Offline storage drifts because plans change. A fallback becomes unnecessary, another viewer adds a batch, an update changes the app footprint, or a return-trip cleanup never happens. A recurring audit converts that drift into visible decisions.

## Choose calendar and event triggers

Use a cadence that matches how often the household prepares offline media. A monthly review may suit frequent use, while a pre-trip and post-trip cycle may suit occasional use. Add event-triggered audits after:

- a system or app update;
- a failed or interrupted preparation;
- a device migration, loss, or sale;
- a large unexplained storage change;
- the end of a shared viewing plan;
- a change in accessibility or track requirements.

The audit should happen early enough to fix problems before the next disconnected window.

## Reconcile the two storage views

Record device free space and the operating system's app footprint. Then list completed offline items in Norva. Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and device-local, while the platform app total may also include code, settings, cache, metadata, and temporary data.

Use the [offline storage handbook](/blog/offline-storage-management-handbook/) for the baseline method. If a change cannot be traced to known items or other device activity, open an exception using the [unexpected storage diagnostic](/blog/find-unexpected-offline-storage-use/).

## Review every batch as a decision

For each batch, confirm:

- owner and intended profile;
- planned viewing window;
- item version, audio, and subtitles;
- preparation and last test dates;
- retain, recheck, replace, or remove status;
- [expiry or review date](/blog/set-offline-content-expiry-review/);
- cleanup owner.

Items without a current purpose should not remain merely because preparation took time. Remove them through the app's supported control after checking ownership.

## Audit duplicates and device roles

When several supported devices are involved, compare the same item across inventories. A duplicate is justified only when it serves another viewer, another journey, an accessibility configuration, or a credible fallback.

The [multi-device allocation guide](/blog/divide-offline-storage-across-devices/) helps preserve essential-use devices and avoid copying the full library everywhere. Verify the retained copy before removing another.

## Test what matters, not everything equally

Prioritize essential and recently changed items. Confirm profile, version, audio, and subtitles; disable connectivity; and sample more than one playback position. Rotate the remaining sample across audit cycles so the whole library receives attention over time.

Current offline eligibility still depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. A former pass is historical evidence, not a permanent guarantee.

## Restore the reserve and close exceptions

Compare free space with the device-specific reserve. Remove finished items in small measured batches and let the platform report refresh. Do not clear all app data merely to make the scorecard green.

Every failed check needs an owner, next action, and due date. If it requires support, preserve versions, exact messages, non-sensitive steps, and before-and-after readings.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Auditing only when storage is already full.
- Counting visible artwork as offline verification.
- Removing another viewer's items without ownership checks.
- Keeping duplicates without a documented role.
- Treating a previous test as permanent eligibility.
- Closing unexplained changes without evidence.
- Scheduling no next audit.

## Frequently asked questions

### How often should the audit run?

Match it to usage and risk. Use both a recurring calendar and event triggers such as trips, updates, failures, and device changes.

### Must every item be played during each audit?

Test essential and changed items first, then rotate a sample of lower-priority items across cycles. Do not claim untested items passed.

### What should happen to an unexplained difference?

Assign an owner, preserve the evidence, change one factor at a time, and carry the exception forward until it is resolved or safely accepted.
