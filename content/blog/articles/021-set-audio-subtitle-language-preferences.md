# How to Set Preferred Audio and Subtitle Languages

> **In short:** Open Norva’s current language settings, select your preferred audio and subtitle languages, and save the choice. Then test media that is known to contain those tracks. Preferences can guide selection, but they cannot create a language or subtitle track that the source and media do not provide.

**Draft verification notice:** the exact settings path, labels, fallback behaviour, and screenshots must be verified by an identified human before publication.

## Separate preference from availability

A preference is an account choice. Availability is a property of the source and media.

If your preferred audio is French but an item contains only English audio, the player must use an available option or ask for a choice. The exact fallback must be observed rather than assumed.

Language badges can also be incomplete when source metadata is incomplete. Read [What Audio and Subtitle Language Badges Mean](/blog/audio-subtitle-language-badges/) before treating a label as definitive.

## Prepare three reference items

Choose authorised media with source-confirmed tracks:

1. one item containing the preferred audio and subtitle languages;
2. one missing the preferred audio but containing another track;
3. one missing the preferred subtitle language.

Record title identity, device, source-supplied track list, and date. Do not infer a track from the spoken language alone; inspect the available options.

## Step 1: open current language settings

Navigate to Norva’s current account or playback preference area. Interface wording can differ by device or version.

Identify separate controls for audio and subtitles if present. Do not assume that selecting an interface language changes media tracks.

**Observable result:** the current preference choices are visible and distinguish audio from subtitles.

## Step 2: choose a preferred audio language

Select one language from the available preference list and save once.

Open the first reference item and inspect its active audio track. Confirm that the chosen track actually exists in the media.

**Observable result:** the preference is saved and either selects the matching available track or produces a documented alternative.

## Step 3: choose a subtitle preference

Select the preferred subtitle language or documented subtitle behaviour. A subtitle language and subtitle enabled state may be separate choices.

Open the first reference item, confirm the track exists, and inspect which subtitle is active.

**Observable result:** the saved choice has a clear, repeatable outcome on media containing the track.

## Step 4: observe fallback honestly

Use the second and third reference items. Record what Norva does when the preferred option is absent:

| Item | Preferred track present? | Selected result | User prompt? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Known full match | Yes |  |  |
| Audio missing | No |  |  |
| Subtitle missing | No |  |  |

Do not describe this as universal until repeated on supported devices and verified against current product documentation.

## Step 5: check another supported screen

Norva states that language and subtitle preferences can be associated with the account on supported devices when those options exist.

On another supported device, confirm the same account and profile, open one reference item, and record the visible preference. Device layouts may differ even when the account choice is consistent.

## Use filters as discovery aids

An audio-language filter can narrow the library when source metadata identifies tracks consistently. A subtitle-language filter does the same for subtitle information.

See [How Audio-Language Filters Improve Browsing](/blog/audio-language-library-filters/) and [How Subtitle-Language Filters Save Time](/blog/subtitle-language-library-filters/). Filters do not independently inspect or create tracks unless the product explicitly documents that behaviour.

## Diagnose a wrong selection

Check in order:

1. Is the intended track actually present?
2. Is its language label correct?
3. Is the account and profile correct?
4. Is the saved preference visible?
5. Is a title-specific choice overriding it?
6. Does the same result occur on another known item?
7. Does another supported device show the same account choice?

Preserve a redacted screenshot of the track list and item identity before resetting preferences.

## Limitations

Track names, languages, and subtitles depend on the source and media. A source may label a track incorrectly or omit metadata. Device playback capabilities can also differ.

This draft does not claim a tested fallback order. Publication is blocked until current product behaviour is recorded.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does a language preference translate audio?

No. It selects among tracks that the media makes available.

### Is subtitle language the same as interface language?

No. Interface language affects menus; subtitle preference concerns media tracks where available.

### Why does one title ignore the preference?

The preferred track may be absent or mislabelled, or a title-specific choice may apply. Inspect its actual track list first.
