# How to Move a Session From TV to Tablet

> **In short:** Pause on the TV, record profile, item or episode, version, position, audio, and subtitles, then leave the TV state unchanged. On the supported tablet, verify the same account and profile before opening media. Match identity and position, test touch controls and audio output, and resume once. The input method changes from remote to touch, so verify it locally.

A TV-to-tablet handoff often happens when the shared screen is needed by someone else. The viewing state may follow the account, but posture, notifications, orientation, speakers, and control reach belong to the tablet.

## Capture the TV state with the remote

Pause once and record:

- account and profile;
- full title or series, season, and episode;
- version or source label;
- approximate position;
- audio track;
- subtitle track or off state.

The Norva TV experience is designed around remote navigation. Use visible controls and avoid pressing Back repeatedly, which can leave the detail context or app.

## Leave the TV in a known state

Keep the source paused until the tablet is verified. If another household member needs the TV immediately, close or switch through the agreed household routine only after the state card is complete.

Do not infer that both devices may play simultaneously from profile capacity. Current plan, source, rights, and terms govern the intended use.

## Prepare the tablet target

Place the tablet in a stable handheld, propped, or docked position. Check power, network or eligible local state, orientation, and interruptions. The [long-session tablet preparation guide](/blog/how-to-prepare-a-tablet-for-a-long-viewing-session/) is useful when the move is not temporary.

Open the verified Norva route on the supported tablet and confirm the account and profile.

## Match media state

Find the item independently. For episodic media, enter the series and season before selecting the episode. Match the version, then compare progress.

The [cross-device state model](/blog/a-state-by-state-guide-to-cross-device-viewing-handoff/) explains why item identity and version come before timeline adjustment.

## Verify touch controls

Let the tablet layout settle, then test reveal controls, pause, return, and one harmless library action. If a keyboard is connected, verify visible focus before relying on it. W3C focus-visible guidance describes the need for perceivable keyboard focus, but the current interface must be tested.

Do not assume a TV remote's directional order maps to the tablet grid.

## Verify audio and subtitles

Lower the tablet level and confirm the intended output. A TV speaker route does not transfer to tablet speakers or headphones. Follow [the tablet speaker-to-headphone routine](/blog/how-to-switch-safely-between-speakers-and-headphones-on-a-tablet/) if changing outputs.

Select only audio and subtitle tracks available on the target version.

## Resume and release the TV

Resume once on the tablet. Confirm the scene, episode, version, audio, and subtitles. Then close or leave the TV paused according to the household agreement.

Do not use the TV as a live comparison screen longer than needed.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid closing the TV before recording state, choosing the tablet's first continuation card, forgetting profile identity, testing tablet audio at the TV's level, and assuming remote navigation translates to touch.

Device support, source access, network state, and media tracks can differ. This workflow does not guarantee identical layouts or concurrent playback.

## Frequently asked questions

### Must I sign out of the TV?

That depends on whether the TV is trusted and who uses it next. Follow the household's account routine.

### Can I rotate the tablet after resuming?

Yes when supported, but verify item, position, and controls after reflow rather than assuming the layout stays identical.

### What if the tablet shows an older position?

Stay paused and check profile, episode, version, and target freshness before seeking.
