# End a Media Account Session Safely on a Shared Browser

> **In short:** Sign out through Norva's official account control, return to an unauthenticated screen, and close every private or guest window. Check whether the browser offered to save the password, remove any saved entry and site data where you are permitted, inspect downloads and screenshots, and review account sessions from your own trusted device. If credentials may remain exposed, change them through the official destination.

A closed tab can leave a live cookie, saved password, downloaded file, autofill entry, or browser profile behind. Private or guest browsing reduces some local traces, but it does not make an untrusted computer safe for sensitive sign-in.

## Prefer not to sign in

Use a personal supported device whenever possible. Google advises signing into sensitive sites only on trusted devices even when Chrome Guest mode is available. A public or borrowed computer may be managed, monitored, infected, or configured in ways the visitor cannot inspect.

If the session is unavoidable, use the browser's current guest or private mode, avoid syncing a personal browser profile, and verify the [official Norva sign-in destination](/blog/verify-official-sign-in-destination/).

## Sign out at the account layer

Use the official Norva page's sign-out control. Do not rely on closing the tab, browser, or computer. After sign-out, navigate back or reopen the site within the same session and confirm that the personal library and profile are no longer visible.

If the browser shows another user's account, do not explore it. Close the session and notify the device owner or official administrator.

## Inspect browser saving and autofill

Check whether the browser displayed a save-password prompt or stored the credential. Remove the entry only through the current browser's password controls and only when you are authorized to manage that profile.

Do not delete another person's stored passwords or clear all browser data without permission. If you cannot verify whether your password was saved, treat exposure as possible and rotate it from your trusted device.

## Clear the session data you own

Close all private or guest windows. Google documents that Guest mode removes its browsing history, cookies, and site data on exit, while also warning that device owners, networks, and websites may still observe activity.

For a regular shared profile, remove Norva site cookies, cache, history, form entries, and saved credentials where current browser controls and permissions allow. Check the downloads folder for account exports or screenshots; deleting download history alone may not delete the actual file.

## Review remotely from a trusted device

Open the known Norva destination on your own device and inspect current session or trusted-device controls documented by official support. Use the [post-trip trusted-device review](/blog/trusted-device-review-after-trip/) after travel.

Do not claim remote sign-out occurred unless the current account interface confirms it. If the feature is unavailable, contact support and record the limitation.

## Decide whether to rotate credentials

Change the password when it may have been saved, observed, entered on an unverified page, reused elsewhere, or left in an unclosed session. Use the [credential exposure response plan](/blog/credential-exposure-incident-plan/), protect the recovery email, and review related accounts.

Do not rotate through the same shared browser. Use a trusted updated device and a unique value from the password manager.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Closing the tab without signing out.
- Signing a personal browser profile into the shared device.
- Assuming private mode defeats device monitoring.
- Deleting another user's browser data without permission.
- Removing download history but leaving the file.
- Rotating the password on the same untrusted computer.
- Claiming remote revocation without confirmation.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is private browsing enough?

No. It reduces selected local traces but does not make the device, network, or destination trusted. Explicitly sign out and review the account remotely.

### Should I clear the entire browser?

Only within your authority and current browser guidance. Prefer targeted account and site cleanup, and do not erase another person's data.

### What if I cannot tell whether the password was saved?

Treat exposure as possible. Change the password from a trusted device, secure recovery, and review sessions and devices.
