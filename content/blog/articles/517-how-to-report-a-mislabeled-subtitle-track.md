# How to Report a Mislabeled Subtitle Track

> **In short:** Capture the exact displayed label, subtitle state, source, item, version, device, and app or browser version. Verify cue language or role in two suitable scenes, compare one known track or version, and state the smallest correction supported by evidence. Redact credentials, source addresses, private history, account email, and unrelated profiles; do not attach media or full subtitle files.

A mislabeled track is visible and selectable, but its language or role label conflicts with controlled cue evidence. A missing entry, timing issue, or unreadable character problem should be reported separately.

Write the packet shortly after testing so exact labels and timestamps remain available. If another person owns the source, profile, or device, obtain consent before including that context and let the authorised owner complete any identity check required by support.

## Define the mismatch

Choose one category:

- displayed language conflicts with repeated cue language;
- full, forced, captions, or signs-and-songs role conflicts with coverage;
- two distinct tracks have indistinguishable labels;
- regional or script distinction is incorrect when authoritative evidence supports it;
- label is truncated or presented ambiguously in one interface.

Avoid “wrong subtitles” as the only description.

## Preserve the untouched selector

Record exact spelling, capitalization, punctuation, order, role text, selected marker, and state. Add source, item, episode, version, account or anonymised profile, device, app or browser version, connectivity, and local time zone.

A privacy-safe screenshot can preserve the display where authorised.

## Verify cue behavior

Use two scenes relevant to the claim. For language, choose ordinary dialogue cues. For role, compare ordinary dialogue with a foreign passage, sign, song, or non-speech event as appropriate.

Describe cue behavior briefly. Do not copy substantial text or attach timed-text resources.

## Add one controlled comparison

Compare a clearly labelled track in the same item, the same candidate on another authorised version, or a neighboring episode. Keep device, profile, state, and scene stable where possible.

Use [the language-label guide](/blog/how-to-read-subtitle-language-labels-without-guessing/) to avoid inventing region or role. Use [the forced-versus-full guide](/blog/forced-or-full-subtitles-how-the-tracks-differ/) for coverage claims.

## Original evidence: report packet

1. **Displayed label:** exact text and state.
2. **Selected context:** source, item, version, device.
3. **Verified cues:** two timestamps and short behavior descriptions.
4. **Expected distinction:** minimal language or role correction supported by evidence.
5. **Reproduction:** numbered steps.
6. **Comparison:** one relevant known context.
7. **Privacy review:** secrets and unrelated data removed.

When naming conventions are unknown, ask for a truthful distinction rather than prescribing exact replacement text.

## Handle duplicate labels

If two entries behave differently, use [the duplicate-label inspection guide](/blog/why-duplicate-subtitle-labels-need-closer-inspection/). Record both cue sets. Do not call one duplicate merely because the label matches.

## Route the report carefully

Subtitle metadata may originate with the connected source while the player presents supported fields. The packet helps the source owner and Norva support determine the appropriate route. Do not blame one layer without evidence.

Use the official support channel and retain the case reference. Avoid parallel reports unless instructed.

## Protect content and privacy

Do not send credentials, source addresses, payment data, account email, private history, unrelated profile names, media clips, or full subtitle files. A short cue description and selector screenshot are normally more relevant.

## Verify a later correction

Reopen the same item/version, record the new label, and sample the same scenes. Close the report only for that tested context; do not promise other versions or episodes are corrected.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid reporting after one bilingual cue, confusing role with language, prescribing unsupported metadata, and assigning a source or player cause without evidence.

The packet improves reproducibility but cannot guarantee correction or expose the internal metadata owner.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I attach the subtitle file?

No. Describe the behavior and follow official support instructions without distributing content.

### What if the label is broad but not technically false?

Report only when the missing distinction creates a reproducible usability problem and provide the controlled comparison.

### Can I edit the label myself?

Use only authorised, documented source controls. Do not alter evidence before the issue is understood.
