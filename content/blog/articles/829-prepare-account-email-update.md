# Prepare Safely Before Updating an Account Email

> **In short:** Secure and confirm access to both the old and new inboxes before changing anything. Record the existing account, profiles, original subscription provider, renewal evidence, devices, and recovery route without saving secrets. Use only the authenticated account setting or official Norva support path. Do not register the new email as a separate account. Afterward, verify sign-in, notices, profiles, entitlement, source connection, and device sessions, then secure or retire the old inbox.

An email can be an account identifier, notice destination, and recovery channel. A rushed change can create lockout, missed billing notices, or an accidental duplicate account.

## Secure both inboxes

Update weak or reused email passwords, enable multi-factor authentication where supported, and confirm recovery methods. Do not begin if the old inbox is compromised; use the provider's and Norva's official recovery or support channels first.

CISA recommends strong unique passwords, multi-factor authentication, phishing awareness, and timely updates as core account practices.

## Inventory what depends on the email

Record the Norva account's current profiles, device list, original billing provider, plan status, source connection, and support history. The old email may also be the sign-in for a store or source, but those accounts are separate and will not necessarily change together.

The [media-account lifecycle handbook](/blog/media-account-lifecycle-handbook/) helps separate Norva, source, device, and provider controls.

## Preserve minimal billing evidence

Keep the provider name, renewal date, plan label, and redacted confirmation reference in a secure record. Do not copy card data or full receipts into an email-change note.

If billing is tied to a store account using the old email, changing the Norva email may not update that store. Verify the provider separately.

## Use the existing-account change path

Look for a current email-update control while authenticated to the existing account. If none is documented, contact Norva through the official support link. Avoid signing up again with the new address; that can create a second empty account with different profiles and entitlement.

The [duplicate-account recovery guide](/blog/duplicate-account-created-next-steps/) applies if a second account already exists.

## Verify messages carefully

An email change may require confirmation through one or both inboxes, but exact steps depend on current implementation. Inspect sender domain and open the official site directly if a message appears suspicious. Never send a one-time code or password to a person claiming to help.

Record confirmation time, not the code or link token.

## Test the changed account

Sign out in a controlled session and sign back in using the documented new identifier. Confirm profiles, progress, favorites, entitlement, source connection, and current devices. Do not reset or delete the old account record until continuity is verified.

After success, follow the [post-email-change security checklist](/blog/secure-account-after-email-change/) to review sessions, devices, recovery, and notices.

## Prepare a support packet if needed

If the change fails, provide account creation date, masked old and new emails, approximate change time, device and application version, provider name, error wording, and redacted billing evidence. Exclude passwords, codes, tokens, source credentials, and payment data.

The [account-lockout support packet](/blog/account-lockout-support-packet/) gives a reusable evidence format.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Beginning without access to the old inbox.
- Creating a second account with the new email.
- Assuming the source or store email changes too.
- Deleting evidence before verifying entitlement.
- Sending confirmation codes to support.
- Clicking an unverified email-change link.
- Claiming an exact workflow not published currently.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will changing my Norva email change my store account?

Do not assume so. The store or payment provider has separate account controls and should be verified independently.

### Should I create a new account with the new address?

No, unless official Norva guidance explicitly requires it. Use the existing-account change or support path to avoid duplication.

### When can I retire the old inbox?

After sign-in, notices, recovery, profiles, entitlement, and provider records are verified and no separate account still depends on it.
