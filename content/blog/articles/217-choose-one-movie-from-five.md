# A Decision Method for Choosing One Movie From Five

> **In short:** First remove any movie that fails a true must-have. Give all remaining candidates the same readiness check and one-sentence case. Choose a bye rule before judging them, compare two pairs on the strongest current preference, then compare the two winners and the bye candidate. Recheck runtime, source, cut, and tracks for the final winner; do not reopen discovery unless every candidate fails.

Five is small enough to decide and large enough to create circular debate. A bracket works when it compares equally prepared candidates and refuses new criteria halfway through.

## Run the constraint gate

Write the session must-haves: time, audience, current availability, and required tracks. Test each movie.

If a candidate fails, remove it with the reason. Do not keep it in the bracket because its poster or reputation is appealing.

Use [the runtime shortlist workflow](/blog/build-runtime-based-movie-shortlist/) when time is the uncertain constraint.

## Normalize the candidate cards

Give every eligible movie the same fields:

- exact work and cut;
- runtime;
- familiar or new;
- strongest current fit;
- main uncertainty;
- one-person or group support;
- readiness status.

W3C label guidance stresses that controls need clear purpose. Candidate cards need the same clarity: “good” is not a criterion, while “fits the available 100 minutes” is.

## Choose the bye rule in advance

With five candidates, one can enter the final round directly. Pick the rule before considering which title benefits:

- strongest verified fit with the main constraint;
- rotation among group members' first choices;
- least familiar candidate when discovery is the declared goal;
- familiar anchor when risk must be low;
- random assignment if all five are equally acceptable.

Write the rule and the bye candidate. Do not change it after the first pair result.

## Compare two pairs

Pair the remaining four randomly or through a declared contrast. Ask one shared question for both pairs, such as:

- Which better fits current energy?
- Which adds more useful novelty?
- Which has stronger group support?
- Which is more ready with fewer unknowns?

Record one sentence for the winner. Do not use runtime in one pair and category in another unless the method explicitly weights both.

## Run the final three

Compare the two pair winners and bye candidate. Restate the session's strongest preference, then select the movie that best satisfies it after all must-haves.

If the group cannot agree, use the declared tie-break: rotating chooser, majority vote, or random selection among the fully acceptable finalists. W3C notification guidance reinforces the value of a clear outcome and recovery path; state the winner and why the process is closed.

For a broader initial pool, [the 12-to-5-to-3 movie-night funnel](/blog/plan-personal-movie-night/) should happen before this final method.

## Perform the launch check

Before playback, verify:

- correct title and cut;
- current source availability;
- runtime still fits;
- audio and subtitle tracks;
- no stale progress or wrong version;
- playback route is ready.

If the winner fails, choose the runner-up from the final rather than restarting discovery.

## Review the method

Afterward, ask whether the winner fit the session, not whether everyone rated it highest. If the bracket repeatedly favors familiarity or one person's preferences, change the next bye rule before seeing candidates.

Use [the familiar-versus-new grid](/blog/balance-familiar-and-new-movies/) when novelty balance is the recurring conflict.

Norva may present movies, versions, favorites, and compatible authorized sources in one interface, but version readiness and current metadata remain source-dependent. Verify the winner directly.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Including a candidate that fails a must-have.
- Giving unequal information to different movies.
- Choosing the bye after evaluating pair results.
- Changing criteria in each comparison.
- Reopening search when one finalist fails.
- Treating the bracket as a universal quality ranking.

## Frequently asked questions

### What if fewer than five pass the gate?

Continue with the eligible set. The method can become a four-, three-, or two-candidate comparison.

### Should the highest-rated movie get the bye?

Only if rating is the declared main preference and the rating source is meaningful to the group.

### Can the bracket be random?

Pairing and the final tie-break can be random when every candidate is acceptable and everyone agrees to that rule.
