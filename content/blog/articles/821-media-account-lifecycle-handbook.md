# The Complete Media Account Lifecycle Handbook

> **In short:** Manage a media account as a sequence: verify the service and terms, choose a plan from recurring profile needs, create and secure the account, connect only a compatible source you own or are authorized to use, organize profiles and supported devices, monitor trial and billing dates, review privacy controls, preserve needed records, and close the account, store subscription, source connection, device sessions, and local data as separate actions.

Norva describes itself as software that organizes and plays media from a compatible source selected by the user; it does not supply the media. That boundary should guide every lifecycle stage.

## Prepare before creating the account

Read the current landing page, terms, privacy policy, support information, plan details, trial conditions, and supported-device information. Confirm that you have a secure email account, password manager, compatible authorized source, and a device that meets current requirements.

The [pre-account checklist](/blog/prepare-before-creating-norva-account/) turns those checks into a short evidence record without storing credentials.

## Choose from recurring needs

Count the people who need persistent personal profiles, not occasional visitors. Current Norva material states that the Norva plan supports up to two profiles and Norva Family supports up to five. It currently presents the complete software experience in both plans, but those claims and pricing must be rechecked at decision time.

Use the [plan-by-profile guide](/blog/choose-plan-by-profile-needs/) and [Norva-versus-Family framework](/blog/norva-vs-norva-family-decision-framework/). Do not infer simultaneous-stream or device limits when they are not published.

## Create and secure the account

Use the official Norva site or application, a unique password, and a protected email account. Enable stronger authentication if the current product offers it. Keep recovery material outside shared notes and never send a password, source credential, pairing token, or recovery code through informal support channels.

Record the creation date, current terms date, selected plan, and original subscription provider, but not secrets.

## Connect an authorized source carefully

Norva requires the user to connect a compatible source they own or are authorized to access. Confirm the source's own terms, security settings, and account owner before entering details into the official secure flow.

Treat Norva and the source as separate accounts. A password or access change on one does not automatically change the other.

## Organize profiles and devices

Create only the recurring profiles the household needs. Use non-sensitive names and keep personal preferences, history, progress, and favorites scoped appropriately. Maintain a simple inventory of supported televisions, phones, tablets, and browsers associated with the account.

When a device changes owner, sign out, remove account access through available controls, delete local media, and verify factory-reset behavior where relevant.

## Start the trial deliberately

Norva's current landing page advertises a seven-day free trial, requires a payment method, and says the selected plan becomes paid unless canceled before the trial ends. It also describes a reminder before renewal and notes that taxes may apply. Recheck the live purchase screen and original-provider terms before starting.

The [seven-day trial checklist](/blog/seven-day-trial-start-checklist/) records dates, provider, price, tax, renewal, and cancellation evidence.

## Maintain the account

Review profiles, devices, source authorization, store status, recovery controls, application permissions, notification previews, downloads, and privacy notices on a practical schedule and after material events.

Use the [privacy-control routine](/blog/privacy-controls-review-routine/) after a new device, lost device, source change, plan change, major update, or household change.

## Close every layer deliberately

Before closure, preserve lawful records or profile state you need, remove local downloads, disconnect sources, sign out devices, and follow Norva's account-deletion control. Separately cancel the subscription through the original store or payment provider and confirm the status.

Do not assume uninstall closes the account, cancels billing, deletes the source account, or removes store records.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating application, source, device, and store as one account.
- Starting a trial without recording the exact end and renewal conditions.
- Choosing a plan from temporary guests rather than recurring profiles.
- Storing credentials in a lifecycle spreadsheet.
- Assuming uninstall cancels billing.
- Inventing device or simultaneous-use limits.
- Closing the account before preserving needed lawful evidence.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Norva supply media with the account?

No. Norva's current terms describe player and organizer software; users connect a compatible source they own or are authorized to use.

### Can deleting the Norva account cancel a store subscription?

Do not assume so. Follow cancellation controls from the original subscription provider and verify the resulting status separately.

### When should I review the account?

Use a practical schedule and repeat the review after device, source, household, subscription, security, or policy changes.
