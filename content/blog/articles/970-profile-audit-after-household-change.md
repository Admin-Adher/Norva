# Audit Media Profiles After a Household Change

> **In short:** Trigger an immediate audit when someone joins, leaves, or changes administrative responsibility. Reconfirm the Norva account, billing, and authorized-source owners; list expected shared screens; review each profile's recurring purpose and privacy-safe name; and decide retain, rename, hand off, or retire. Do not inspect detailed viewing history or assume profile state can transfer. Follow current official controls and assign every unresolved access or ownership issue.

A household change affects more than profile labels. It can alter who controls the account, pays the subscription, administers the connected source, owns a shared device, and expects preferences or progress. Handle these boundaries separately so one profile action does not conceal an unresolved access problem.

## Define the change and urgency

Record the event category, effective date, authorized remaining administrator, and immediate risks. A lost device, departure under conflict, or suspected credential exposure needs prompt security action; an ordinary new shared context may allow planned onboarding.

Use the [maintenance handbook](/blog/media-app-maintenance-audit-handbook/) as the event trigger, not the next quarterly date.

## Reassign account and billing roles

Confirm who controls the account email, unique password, recovery path, and subscription with the original provider. Account administration and billing may belong to different people. Follow the [household account transfer guide](/blog/transfer-household-account-administration/) rather than sharing credentials informally.

Record the provider and owner, never payment details or recovery codes.

## Reconfirm source authorization

Identify who owns or is authorized to administer the compatible source and whether the changed household may continue using it. A Norva profile does not grant source rights. Use the [secure source handoff](/blog/household-admin-source-handoff/) for connection details.

If authorization ends, stop affected access through the proper source and account processes before reorganizing profiles.

## Inventory expected screens

List personal mobile, shared TV, and browser categories still expected. Mark devices leaving the household, remaining shared screens, and unknown sessions. Use official controls and support without assuming a complete device dashboard.

When credentials changed, run the post-password access audit separately.

## Review profile purpose

For each profile, ask whether its recurring personal or shared context remains valid. Avoid opening detailed history or favorites merely to identify the owner. The [quarterly profile audit](/blog/quarterly-profile-hygiene-audit/) provides purpose and duplication criteria.

Use retain, rename, new-owner discussion, retirement-plan, or unresolved.

## Protect names and privacy

Remove full names, emails, birth dates, locations, health or belief details, account identifiers, and source information from visible labels. The [private profile naming guide](/blog/name-norva-profiles-clearly/) helps create a neutral replacement.

Do not rename a departed person's profile in a way that reveals why they left.

## Preserve preferences carefully

Ask the continuing profile user which language or subtitle preferences matter. Test one known item where the source supplies the required tracks. Do not infer preferences from private history, and do not promise every source item contains them.

Record preference intent separately from item availability.

## Plan retirement before deletion

For a profile no longer needed, record owner authorization, unresolved progress or favorite concerns, current support guidance, and a cooling-off date. Do not assume profile history can be exported, merged, or transferred. Make destructive changes only through current official controls with an understood consequence.

Afterward, verify remaining profile names and one shared-screen handoff.

## Review plan capacity

Current official information describes up to two profiles for Norva and up to five for Norva Family. Recount recurring contexts after the household change and consult current pricing. Capacity does not define device count, simultaneous playback, or parental controls.

Avoid changing plans solely because a temporary guest arrives.

## Minimize and close evidence

Keep role names, neutral profile codes, decisions, action owners, and completion dates. Delete temporary screenshots and never retain passwords, source credentials, private viewing history, or unnecessary details about the household change.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Sharing an account password to simplify handoff.
- Treating a profile as source authorization.
- Inspecting detailed history to infer ownership.
- Assuming state can be merged or transferred.
- Forgetting billing and shared screens.
- Keeping sensitive change details in the audit.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should a departing member's profile be deleted immediately?

Not automatically. Address access and security promptly, then use an authorized retirement plan and current support guidance for profile state.

### Can a profile be transferred to another person?

Do not assume a transfer feature or that private state should move. Define the new recurring context and follow documented controls.

### Does a smaller household always need a different plan?

No. Count stable profile contexts and review current pricing and terms; do not infer plan need from household size alone.
