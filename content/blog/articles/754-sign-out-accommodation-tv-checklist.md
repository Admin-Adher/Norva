# The Accommodation TV Sign-Out Checklist

> **In short:** Sign out through the official Norva app, return to the account chooser or unauthenticated screen, and reopen the app to confirm your profile is absent. Remove intentional casting or pairing relationships from your own devices, review trusted-device or session controls where currently available, and record checkout evidence. Do not factory-reset accommodation equipment without permission, and change passwords only when exposure is suspected.

Closing playback is not the same as ending an account session. A temporary television can retain a profile, authorization, pairing, browser page, or account chooser after the traveller turns off the screen.

## Start before packing the remote

Run the checklist while you still have room access, a working network, and enough time to contact support. Find the session receipt created during [safer temporary-TV sign-in](/blog/sign-in-temporary-accommodation-tv/) and confirm the property or room, app, profile, and expected sign-out owner.

Do not rely on the accommodation's future reset routine. The traveller should close the session they created.

## Sign out through the official app

Use the current Norva app's account, profile, or settings controls and read each confirmation. Interface wording can change, so follow what the official app presents rather than an old screenshot.

After sign-out:

1. return to the app's unauthenticated or account-choice state;
2. close the app;
3. reopen it;
4. confirm the personal profile and library are not visible;
5. ensure no resume card exposes private viewing history.

If the app still shows account data, stop and use Norva's official support path. Do not browse unrelated owner settings or another guest's account.

## Remove pairings from personal devices

If the phone or tablet was intentionally paired for casting, remote control, or audio, remove or forget that relationship using current controls on the personal device. Also check whether a QR-code authorization page remains open in the browser and close the completed session.

Do not factory-reset the TV, delete accommodation apps, or remove property network settings without permission. Those actions belong to the owner.

## Review trusted access remotely

From a trusted personal device, use the [trusted-device review](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) and any current Norva session controls documented by official support. Remove the temporary television when it appears and when the control is appropriate.

Do not invent a remote-revocation feature if the current account interface does not expose one. Record "not available" and contact support rather than claiming the local sign-out was remotely confirmed.

## Decide whether credential rotation is needed

Routine use of a verified official app does not automatically require a password change. Rotate a credential when it may have been exposed, entered into an unverified page, seen by another person, reused elsewhere, or left in a session that cannot be closed.

Use a trusted device and official destination. Changing one password does not necessarily secure email, platform, or authorized-source accounts, so review each affected service separately.

## Finish the room and network check

Inspect the television, bedside area, outlets, adapters, cables, and personal devices. Remove saved accommodation Wi-Fi from personal devices when that matches the travel plan, and confirm no screenshots containing codes or account details remain unnecessarily.

Repeat a broader [trusted-device review after travel](/blog/trusted-device-review-after-trip/) on the home network. This catches a temporary session that did not appear immediately.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Turning off the TV without signing out.
- Assuming the property will reset every session.
- Removing accommodation-owned apps or settings.
- Forgetting casting or remote-control pairings.
- Claiming remote revocation without seeing it.
- Changing only the Norva password after wider exposure.
- Waiting until after checkout to inspect the screen.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is closing the app enough?

No. Use the app's sign-out control and reopen it to confirm your personal account state is absent.

### Should I factory-reset the television?

Not without the owner's permission and current accommodation instructions. Remove only the session and pairings you created.

### What if sign-out fails?

Document the exact state, use a trusted personal device to review available account controls, contact Norva support, and rotate exposed credentials when appropriate.
