# Progress Reset After an Item Identity Change? What to Check

> **In short:** Confirm the active profile before comparing progress. Pair the earlier and current observations for a tiny sample, including source label, title code, year, media type, season, episode, duration, visible version, saved position or completion, device, application version, and timestamp. Determine whether the same logical item now appears under another identity or group. Avoid editing history, and send support only redacted sample evidence.

A familiar title does not prove the same progress identity. A source refresh can coincide with another version, episode, duration, or source card becoming visible, but the interface evidence must be compared before any cause is claimed.

Keep the paired evidence unchanged until support can review the exact comparison.

## Confirm the profile first

Verify the same Norva account and active profile used for the earlier viewing. Record any profile switch, sign-out, household role change, or device reassignment. Different profiles can carry different progress and history context.

The [source-change progress baseline](/blog/progress-baseline-after-source-change/) explains how to record this before planned changes.

## Choose a minimal sample

Use one affected item and, if needed, one unaffected control. For series, include season and episode. Assign privacy-safe sample codes and avoid exporting the complete household history. Record why the earlier position is known: displayed time, percentage, completion marker, or dated screenshot.

## Pair visible item identity

Compare source label, title, year, media type, season, episode, duration, resolution or edition cue, audio or subtitle cue, artwork, and grouping. Record every visible difference. Do not inspect hidden identifiers or assume title matching determines progress.

If versions separated after a refresh, use the [grouped-version comparison](/blog/grouped-versions-split-after-refresh/).

## Record progress without false precision

Use the format actually shown: time, percentage, progress bar, completed state, or absent marker. Do not convert an approximate bar into exact seconds. Include timestamps and whether playback occurred between observations.

Norva's current privacy notice should remain the source of truth for how progress-related usage data is described.

## Rebuild the source-change timeline

Record the last known correct progress, source update or refresh, visible sync state, first new item identity, and first reset observation. Include device and timezone. Do not claim the refresh caused the reset solely because it occurred first.

The [import and sync handbook](/blog/catalog-import-sync-troubleshooting-handbook/) keeps source, sync, device, and identity layers separate.

## Compare one other supported device

Use the same account, profile, sample, source selection, filters, and grouping on another trusted device. Record both application versions and close timestamps. Different progress on one device is a cross-screen observation, not proof of a local or server fault.

## Look for another visible identity

Search and browse for the earlier source version or grouped card. The prior progress may still appear on another visible entry. Do not open multiple versions and advance playback just to test them; that changes the state being investigated.

## Protect history during recovery

Do not mark the item completed, reset progress, delete history, remove the source, merge cards, or bulk-edit favorites. Preserve the original and current pairs. If support recommends a reversible action, record the before and after result.

## Classify carefully

Use profile mismatch, different episode, different source or version, duration change, grouping change, device-specific difference, prior identity found, progress absent on all matching identities, or unknown. Do not promise automatic transfer or recovery between identities.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Comparing progress across different profiles.
- Matching by title or artwork alone.
- Ignoring episode, duration, source, and version changes.
- Advancing playback while testing multiple identities.
- Deleting history before preserving paired evidence.
- Sharing the household's complete viewing history.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will progress move automatically to another version?

Do not assume it will. Compare visible identities and current Norva guidance, then ask support about the specific redacted sample.

### Does the same title mean the same item?

No. Source, year, media type, episode, duration, version, and grouping cues may distinguish visible identities.

### How much history should I send support?

Send only the affected sample pair, relevant timestamps, profile context, versions, and one control item when needed—not complete history.
