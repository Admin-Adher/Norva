# The Complete Offline Viewing Planning Handbook

> **In short:** A dependable offline viewing plan starts with realistic viewing time, then selects only eligible items that fit measured device storage and battery capacity. Complete transfers early, test every essential item without connectivity, verify required audio and subtitles, and schedule reconnection and cleanup. Availability always depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

Offline viewing is most reliable when it is treated as a small project rather than a last-minute download queue. The goal is not to fill a device. It is to prepare a tested set of items that matches a specific disconnected period and leaves the device usable.

## Define the disconnected situation first

Write down where connectivity may be absent or unreliable, how long that period could last, which device you will use, and whether anyone else needs that device. Separate total journey time from genuine viewing opportunities. Transfers, meals, work, sleep, and charging restrictions can reduce the usable window.

Then identify non-negotiable needs: a particular language, subtitles, headphones, a specific profile, or an accessibility setting. These requirements should influence selection before any transfer begins. The guide to [estimating offline viewing hours](/blog/estimate-offline-viewing-hours/) provides a time-first worksheet.

## Make five planning decisions

### 1. Choose a realistic batch

Select a small variety of items that suit the available sessions. A short episode can fit a brief wait; a film may need an uninterrupted block. The [offline weekend selection method](/blog/choose-titles-for-weekend-offline/) helps balance duration, mood, and shared preferences without overpacking.

### 2. Confirm item-level eligibility

A title appearing in a catalogue does not prove that an offline copy is available. Check the exact item and version in the current app. With Norva, offline access exists only when the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights allow it. Record “not available” rather than assuming a later retry will change the result.

### 3. Budget local storage

Use the device storage screen and measured sizes from representative completed items. Do not rely on a universal size-per-hour estimate: duration, quality, encoding, and included tracks can differ. Preserve working space for the operating system, apps, photos, and temporary data. For a wider cleanup strategy, use the [offline storage management handbook](/blog/offline-storage-management-handbook/).

### 4. Budget power

Measure battery use during a representative local playback session on the actual device. Screen brightness, battery condition, audio output, temperature, and other activity all affect runtime. Build a reserve rather than treating one observation as a guarantee. The [offline battery budget](/blog/offline-viewing-battery-budget/) turns that observation into a trip plan.

### 5. Set a removal and reconnection plan

Decide when finished items will be removed and when the device will reconnect. Reconnection can allow account state such as playback progress to update. If several devices were used while disconnected, verify the intended profile and progress before continuing elsewhere.

## Prepare and verify the batch

Complete transfers on a trusted, stable connection with enough time to investigate failures. Keep the device powered when practical. Once the app reports completion, disconnect deliberately: enable airplane mode or disable both Wi-Fi and mobile data, then reopen the app and play more than the first moment of each essential item.

Check the required audio track and subtitle option during that test. Offline track availability can differ from what was visible while connected. Finish with the [night-before readiness check](/blog/night-before-offline-readiness-check/) so account, storage, power, accessories, and the prepared batch are reviewed together.

## Common mistakes and limits

- Treating every catalogue item as eligible for offline use.
- Preparing more hours than the trip can realistically accommodate.
- Filling nearly all available storage.
- Testing while one network connection remains active.
- Forgetting language, subtitle, or accessibility needs.
- Assuming app-managed local items will move automatically to another device.
- Keeping old batches indefinitely.
- Treating local availability as ownership or a change in associated rights.

Device settings, source behaviour, account state, and rights can change. Repeat the critical checks after an app update, device change, or long gap between trips.

## Frequently asked questions

### How early should I prepare?

Begin early enough to finish transfers, diagnose failures, and repeat a disconnected test. The right lead time depends on batch size, connection conditions, and available storage; there is no universal number of hours.

### Should I prepare more than I expect to watch?

A small alternative can be useful, but a large unused batch adds storage pressure and verification work. Prioritise realistic sessions and one modest fallback.

### Does completed mean verified offline?

Not by itself. The useful proof is opening and sampling the exact item with connectivity disabled, including any audio or subtitle option you depend on.
