# Secure a Media Account After Losing a Phone

> **In short:** Protect personal safety first, then use the phone platform's current official locate, mark-lost, lock, or erase guidance from a known-good device. Secure the recovery email and mobile number, review Norva account access, and handle every compatible authorized source separately. Change exposed or reused credentials, contact official support when needed, and record remote actions as requested, pending, or confirmed rather than assuming they succeeded.

A missing phone combines a physical-device incident with several account boundaries. It may hold an unlocked email session, password manager, Norva access, source applications, notifications, downloaded media, and a phone number used for recovery. One remote command does not automatically settle every boundary.

## Protect people before accounts

Do not confront a suspected thief or enter an unsafe location to recover a device. Contact local authorities or venue staff when appropriate. If the phone is merely misplaced, call it only when doing so does not reveal sensitive information or create a safety risk.

Use the [lost travel device response card](/blog/lost-travel-device-response-card/) to collect time, last known location, device identity, and safe contact routes without recording passwords or recovery codes.

## Open the platform's official loss tools

From a trusted device and connection, follow current Apple or Android guidance for locating, marking as lost, securing, or erasing the phone. Availability and behavior depend on the platform, prior configuration, connectivity, and account state.

Keep evidence of what the official service reports. Mark each command **requested**, **pending**, or **confirmed**. An offline phone may not immediately receive a remote action, and account removal does not prove that local data was erased.

Do not remove the device from the platform account prematurely if current official guidance says that doing so affects location, lock, or protection features. The platform's latest instructions control the sequence.

## Secure recovery email and the phone number

Review the recovery inbox from a known-good session. Check devices, sessions, recovery methods, forwarding, filters, and unfamiliar changes using the email provider's official controls. If its password was exposed or reused, replace it with a unique value.

Contact the mobile carrier through a known official destination to report the loss and ask about the appropriate line or SIM protections. Do not give a caller a one-time code merely because they claim to represent the carrier.

## Review the Norva account separately

Open Norva through a known official route. Review any currently available account or device controls documented by Norva. If the phone was unlocked, a password was exposed, or the same password was reused elsewhere, follow the [credential-exposure incident plan](/blog/credential-exposure-incident-plan/) and create a unique replacement.

Use the [trusted-device review guide](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) to classify known, retired, temporary, and unknown access. Do not assume that changing a password closes every session unless current official documentation confirms it.

## Protect authorized sources independently

Norva and each compatible source the account owner is authorized to use are distinct. Review source sessions, devices, credentials, and recovery at that source's official destination. Never send a source password or access code to Norva support.

Check for password reuse across Norva, sources, email, device platform, and the password manager. Every reused account is independently exposed and needs its own unique replacement.

## Account for offline media and local data

Eligible offline media may be stored encrypted on the device when the device, source, media, and rights allow. That product behavior is only one layer; it does not replace the platform lock, account review, carrier action, or remote erase decision.

Follow the [offline-media device-loss guide](/blog/protect-offline-media-after-device-loss/) to distinguish locally stored media, synced account state, and source access. Verify current Norva product behavior in official documentation before relying on it for a specific device.

## Preserve evidence and close deliberately

Record the loss time, platform reports, carrier case, security alerts, account changes, support case, and confirmation status. Keep secrets, live links, codes, and unredacted screenshots out of the ledger.

Closure means the physical-device risk has been handled as far as possible, recovery is secure, relevant credentials are unique, unknown access is addressed, sources were reviewed independently, and remote actions reached a known state.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Risking personal safety to retrieve the phone.
- Using a link from an unsolicited loss alert.
- Removing the platform device before checking current official guidance.
- Assuming a queued erase already completed.
- Securing Norva while leaving recovery email exposed.
- Forgetting authorized sources and carrier controls.
- Treating encrypted offline media as the entire response.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I erase the phone immediately?

Follow the platform's current official sequence. The right choice depends on safety, location features, connectivity, prior configuration, and the sensitivity of accessible data.

### Does changing my Norva password secure every source?

No. Review and secure each authorized source, recovery provider, and device-platform account through its own official destination.

### Is a remote erase request proof that data is gone?

No. Record it as requested or pending until the platform reports a confirmed state, while continuing the separate account-response steps.
