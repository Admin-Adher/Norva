# How to Prioritize Offline Items When Storage Is Limited

> **In short:** Protect a working free-space reserve before selecting media. Reject anything ineligible or missing essential audio or subtitles, then rank remaining items by real session need, likelihood of use, natural fit, and measured local size. Add items one at a time until the next choice would cross the reserve. Keep only one high-value fallback.

Limited storage creates a decision problem, not merely a cleanup problem. The smallest files are not automatically the best choices, and the most wanted title is not useful if it cannot be verified offline.

## Establish the boundary first

Open the device storage view and record current free space. Decide how much must remain available for the operating system, app activity, photos, messages, updates, and normal use. There is no universal reserve that fits every device.

Apple and Android provide storage settings and recommendations, but labels and accounting can vary. Use the [free-space reserve method](/blog/set-free-space-reserve-offline-media/) to choose and document a device-specific boundary.

Do not begin by deleting personal files. Review finished offline items, other app downloads, and clearly unused local data through their proper controls.

## Reject candidates that cannot satisfy the session

Before comparing size, remove any candidate that fails one essential condition:

- no current offline eligibility for the exact item and version;
- required audio or subtitles are absent or unverified;
- runtime does not fit a realistic session;
- disconnected playback test fails;
- the item belongs to the wrong profile or series position;
- associated rights do not permit the intended use.

Norva offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. A candidate does not earn priority simply because it appears in the catalogue.

## Rank the remaining items by value per session

Use plain categories rather than a false numerical score:

1. **Essential:** directly serves a dependable viewing window or accessibility need.
2. **Useful:** likely to be watched and fills a distinct duration or mood.
3. **Fallback:** covers one credible change the first two categories cannot.
4. **Speculative:** has no named session or duplicates another choice.

Drop speculative candidates first. The [offline content rotation](/blog/build-offline-content-rotation/) prevents old fallbacks from accumulating between trips.

## Compare measured storage cost

Among candidates in the same value category, compare actual completed sizes when available. Do not assume every hour or episode uses the same space. Quality, encoding, tracks, duration, and source packaging can differ.

If two equally useful items fit the same window, the smaller observed item may be the better storage choice. If a larger item is the only one that meets an essential subtitle need, size alone should not displace it.

Use the [viewing-hours estimate](/blog/estimate-offline-viewing-hours/) to avoid budgeting space for time that is unlikely to exist.

## Add items incrementally

After each completed transfer:

1. recheck free device storage;
2. verify the item offline;
3. update its measured cost;
4. compare projected free space with the reserve;
5. stop before the next item would cross the boundary.

This incremental method accounts for differences between expected and observed storage. It also catches temporary data or another app consuming space during preparation.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Choosing only by smallest size.
- Applying one generic size-per-hour estimate.
- Filling storage to the displayed limit.
- Keeping several fallbacks for the same purpose.
- Deleting app folders manually.
- Ignoring subtitle, audio, profile, or episode-order needs.
- Trusting a queued item instead of a completed disconnected test.

Storage reporting may take time to refresh. Treat measurements as a planning range and verify the final state on the device.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I lower quality to fit more items?

Only when a lower option is available, visually acceptable on the intended device, and still contains required tracks. Compare actual results rather than assuming a fixed saving.

### Is the shortest item always the safest choice?

No. It must still be eligible, wanted, correctly ordered, and usable with the required audio or subtitles.

### What if the reserve leaves room for no media?

Protect essential device functions first. Use a different supported device, reduce the plan, or rely on a non-media fallback rather than forcing storage below your boundary.
