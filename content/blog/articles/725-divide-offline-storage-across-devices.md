# How to Divide Offline Storage Across Several Devices

> **In short:** Give each device a clear role based on its viewer, journey, screen, battery, available storage, accessibility needs, and essential non-media tasks. Protect a separate free-space reserve on every device, place each item where it will actually be used, and duplicate only a justified critical fallback. Verify every local copy independently; device-local media should not be assumed to sync or transfer.

Several devices create flexibility, but copying the same batch everywhere multiplies transfer time, storage use, verification, and cleanup. Allocation works better when every device solves a distinct problem.

## Inventory the devices separately

For each supported phone or tablet, record:

- intended viewer and Norva profile;
- current free storage and chosen reserve;
- observed battery condition and charging access;
- screen and audio suitability;
- accessibility needs;
- other essential uses such as navigation or communication;
- who will carry and maintain it.

Do not infer one device's capacity from another, even when nominal storage is similar. Operating-system use, other apps, battery age, and local files differ.

## Assign a journey role

Useful roles include:

- **primary viewing device:** best suited to the main planned sessions;
- **short-session device:** convenient for a brief window;
- **shared device:** serves agreed household choices;
- **essential-use device:** preserves battery and space for travel functions;
- **fallback device:** carries one independently verified alternative.

One device can hold more than one role, but conflicting priorities should be visible. The [shared-device offline plan](/blog/shared-device-offline-viewing-plan/) helps when several viewers use one screen.

## Allocate by actual need

Match items to the viewer and session. A long film belongs on the device planned for an uninterrupted window, while a short episode may suit the device carried during a transfer. Essential subtitles, descriptive audio, headphones, and screen readability can outweigh raw storage capacity.

Do not split item counts equally. Compare measured sizes and apply a [device-specific free-space reserve](/blog/set-free-space-reserve-offline-media/) to each screen.

## Limit duplication

Duplicate an item only when it protects a credible failure or supports two independent viewers. Every duplicate must still be eligible for that exact device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. Account progress and preferences can sync across supported devices, but that does not mean a local media copy transfers between them.

If a device is being replaced, use the [pre-migration offline inventory](/blog/record-offline-library-before-device-change/) rather than treating another device's batch as a backup.

## Prepare and test every device independently

For each assigned item:

1. confirm the account and profile;
2. open the exact item and version;
3. check current eligibility;
4. complete preparation;
5. verify required audio and subtitles;
6. disable connectivity;
7. sample at least two positions;
8. record the result and cleanup owner.

Do not mark Device B ready because Device A passed. Local storage, app state, tracks, and rights conditions must be observed on each one.

## Plan progress and reconnection order

Avoid changing the same item's progress on several devices under one profile while disconnected. If two independent sessions are expected, record which progress should be preserved and which device reconnects first.

After the trip, verify account state before removing local copies. The [offline storage handbook](/blog/offline-storage-management-handbook/) provides the safe cleanup sequence.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Duplicating the full batch on every device.
- Treating profile limits as device or simultaneous-use limits.
- Assuming local media syncs with account progress.
- Applying one reserve or battery estimate to all devices.
- Ignoring accessibility and audio-output differences.
- Letting the essential-use device consume its protected battery.
- Reconnecting conflicting progress without a sequence.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should the largest device hold everything?

No. Consider viewer, session, battery, accessibility, reliability, and essential tasks, not storage capacity alone.

### Is one duplicate ever useful?

Yes when it covers a credible device or schedule failure and is independently eligible and verified. Document why it earns the extra space.

### Do I need the same profile on every device?

Use the profile belonging to the intended viewer. Confirm account and profile context before preparation and playback rather than relying on defaults.
