# Norva vs. a Built-In TV Player: Which Workflow Fits You?

> **In short:** A built-in TV player may be sufficient for a simple, single-screen task that it already handles well. Norva is designed for organising a compatible authorised source and retaining progress, favourites, history, and preferences across supported web, mobile, and TV devices. Test both on the same TV; neither is universally better.

“Built-in player” covers many different products and firmware versions. Avoid general claims about all TVs. Compare the exact player already on your television with the current Norva experience.

## Compare the workflow, not the logo

Use criteria that affect daily viewing:

| Criterion | Built-in player | Norva |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source support | Verify in TV manual and settings | Requires a compatible authorised source |
| Catalogue organisation | Observe on the actual TV | Designed to organise the connected catalogue |
| Remote navigation | Test complete tasks | TV navigation designed for a remote |
| Cross-device progress | Verify current TV ecosystem | Retained across supported Norva devices |
| Profiles | Verify on actual player | Up to 2 or 5 by Norva plan |
| Languages and subtitles | Depends on media and player | Preference retained; tracks depend on media/source |
| Updates and support | TV manufacturer lifecycle | Norva product and supported-platform lifecycle |

The table identifies questions. It is not a performance result.

## When a built-in player may fit

Keep the built-in workflow in consideration when:

- viewing happens only on that television;
- it already reaches the intended authorised media source;
- its remote controls are clear for the household;
- cross-device state and separate profiles are unnecessary;
- the required media formats and tracks work in a representative test;
- the TV manufacturer still provides the needed support.

Read the current TV manual rather than assuming a port, app, or format works because a similar model did.

## When Norva may fit

Norva is worth evaluating when the household values:

- one organised experience across supported web, mobile, and TV devices;
- retained playback progress, history, favourites, and preferences;
- profile separation;
- grouped media versions;
- search and browsing beyond one screen;
- remote-oriented TV navigation;
- conditional eligible offline access on mobile.

Norva does not include media access. The user provides a compatible source they own or are legally authorised to use. Read [what Norva includes and requires](/blog/what-norva-includes/) before treating the comparison as complete.

## Run the same-task comparison

Choose three known items and perform identical tasks:

1. launch the player from the TV home screen;
2. find one item by browsing;
3. find another by search;
4. open details and select a version if available;
5. start, pause, seek, and resume;
6. change audio or subtitles on a known compatible item;
7. press Back from playback and details;
8. return to the same item later;
9. repeat the progress check on another device if supported.

Count remote actions only when both routes begin and end at equivalent states. The [TV-versus-phone interface guide](/blog/tv-interface-vs-phone-interface/) explains why good controls can differ across inputs.

## Evaluate continuity separately

A TV-only player may score well for immediate playback while offering no relevant cross-device workflow for your needs. Norva's value should be tested by beginning on one supported device and resuming the same profile, item, and version on another.

Use the [cross-device media player explainer](/blog/cross-device-media-player-explained/) to define the state being compared. Do not assume immediate synchronisation; observe the actual checkpoint.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Generalising from one TV brand or firmware version.
- Comparing different sources or media versions.
- Counting clicks from unequal starting points.
- Assuming profile counts are device limits.
- Ignoring TV software support and updates.
- Treating visual polish as proof of playback compatibility.
- Forgetting that missing tracks can originate in the media itself.

Re-test after major TV, app, or source changes.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Norva replace the TV's operating system?

No. Norva is a media player and organiser used within the supported TV environment. The TV operating system and hardware remain part of the workflow.

### Is a built-in player always faster?

No universal claim is justified. Measure launch, navigation, and playback on the actual TV under comparable conditions.

### Which option is better for several screens?

Norva is designed for continuity across supported web, mobile, and TV devices. Verify the exact workflow and source during the trial.
