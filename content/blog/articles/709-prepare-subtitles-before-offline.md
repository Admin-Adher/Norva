# Prepare Subtitle Options Before Going Offline

> **In short:** Select the exact item and version, identify the subtitle track you need, and verify its label and readability while connected. After the offline copy completes, disable Wi-Fi and mobile data, reopen it, and test several captioned moments. Subtitle availability depends on the source and media; a saved preference or connected track list does not prove offline inclusion.

For someone who depends on captions or translated subtitles, their absence is not a minor inconvenience. Treat subtitles as an essential readiness requirement and test them with the same care as the video itself.

## Describe the subtitle need precisely

Record the intended language and type of support. A viewer may need translated dialogue, same-language captions, speaker identification, or other accessibility information. Do not assume every track with the same language label provides the same experience.

Also note the viewing conditions: screen size, expected brightness, distance, and whether the device's text styling controls are important. The general guide to [selecting video subtitles](/blog/select-video-subtitles/) covers connected playback choices.

## Check the exact media version

Open the item and version you intend to prepare. Inspect the subtitle menu while connected, select the required track, and observe several scenes that actually contain dialogue. A menu label alone cannot reveal timing, completeness, or readability.

Languages and subtitles available in Norva depend on the source and media. If episodes or versions differ, verify each essential one rather than extrapolating from a neighbour.

## Complete the offline preparation

Start the offline action only when the exact item is eligible. Availability remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. Wait for a completed state and preserve enough local storage for the device to operate normally.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. The local copy must therefore be tested independently from the connected catalogue view.

## Test subtitle usability offline

Disable Wi-Fi and mobile data, close the item, and reopen it through the offline area. Then:

1. select the intended subtitle label;
2. play one dialogue-heavy moment;
3. seek to another captioned section;
4. check timing against speech and visible action;
5. check legibility against both bright and dark scenes;
6. confirm the track remains available after reopening;
7. record the result.

If the track is missing or unusable, reconnect and inspect another eligible version. Do not rely on a last-minute connected search. Pair the process with [offline audio verification](/blog/prepare-audio-options-before-offline/) when both spoken language and captions matter.

## Check display settings without overpromising

If the device or app exposes subtitle styling, test the actual controls. Increase size or contrast only to the degree available in the current setup. Do not promise that every source-supplied track supports every styling option.

The W3C's media accessibility requirements describe why captions, readable presentation, and user control matter, but they do not prove implementation in a specific app or item. Your disconnected test remains decisive.

Add the outcome to the [night-before readiness check](/blog/night-before-offline-readiness-check/) so it is not lost among storage and battery tasks. The [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) provides the broader workflow.

Record the device, profile, and test date. “Recheck” means the item should not be relied upon for an essential accessible session.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating a subtitle language badge as proof of usability.
- Testing a silent opening with no captioned dialogue.
- Checking one episode and assuming a season matches.
- Downloading a different version from the one inspected.
- Assuming connected preferences guarantee local inclusion.
- Ignoring timing, clipping, contrast, or screen size.
- Leaving connectivity active during the test.

Source metadata can be incomplete or inconsistent. Document the exact label and observed behaviour before seeking support.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are subtitles always included with an offline item?

Do not assume so. Inclusion depends on the exact source, media, version, device workflow, and rights. Test the local copy before leaving connectivity.

### Is one captioned scene enough?

Use at least two representative moments. One can reveal basic presence; another can expose timing, readability, or incomplete local data.

### What if the correct language label shows the wrong text?

Record the mismatch, item, version, profile, and device. Try another eligible version while connected or choose a verified fallback.
