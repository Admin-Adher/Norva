# Build a Repeatable Audio Quality Listening Protocol

> **In short:** Define one question, authorised sources, matched excerpts, fixed playback route, output, processing, room, and safe level method before listening. Conceal labels and alternate order where practical, keep trials short, collect observations before preference, repeat only planned differences, add rests and a stopping rule, and report unknowns. Do not claim laboratory rigor from a household test.

Repeatability comes from controlling context and documenting decisions, not listening more loudly or for longer.

## Pre-register the question

Write the exact comparison and decision: identify an artifact, choose a compatible track, compare dynamics, or locate an output limit. List properties that must match and known differences that prevent causal claims.

Choose a stopping rule before hearing results.

## Verify sources and rights

Use only tracks listeners own or are authorised to use. Record version, edit, mix, language, codec, bitrate context, channels, sample format, loudness, and dynamics when verified. Mark unknowns.

Do not share clips, credentials, addresses, tokens, or viewing history.

## Select short excerpts

Include the dimensions relevant to the question: dialogue, transients, sustained tones, ambience, stereo or spatial cues, bass, silence, dense mixtures, and dynamic changes. Store timecodes, not copyrighted transcripts.

Use several excerpts so one instrument or mix does not determine the conclusion.

## Fix the listening chain

Record device, software, decoder, track, route, processing, receiver, speakers or headphones, placement or fit, room noise, seat, and gain stages. Verify the physical output before each session.

Do not change route or picture scene during a trial.

## Match loudness safely

Use valid programme-loudness evidence when available or careful safe perceived matching. Lower level before switching outputs. Never raise level to expose artifacts, and stop with discomfort, fatigue, or unexpected peaks.

Record method and every gain value without treating slider numbers as acoustic level.

## Original evidence: protocol sheet

| Field | Pre-registered value |
|---|---|
| Question and decision | Narrow statement |
| Versions and unknowns | Metadata and limits |
| Excerpts | Timecodes and dimensions |
| Route/output/room | Fixed context |
| Level method | Safe procedure |
| Order schedule | A/B and B/A |
| Repeats/rest/stopping | Planned limits |
| Notes | Observation, preference, confidence |

Do not edit the question after seeing the first result without starting a new exploratory round.

## Conceal and randomise where practical

Hide filenames and quality labels, have another person switch, and balance starting order. Ask listeners to describe the sound before choosing. Keep notes independent until each round ends.

[The responsible audio comparison guide](/blog/how-to-compare-two-audio-versions-responsibly/) supplies a multidimensional scorecard.

## Use rests and limited repeats

Short breaks reduce fatigue and adaptation. Repeat only a preselected meaningful difference, reverse order, and stop at the planned count. If the result fails to recur, record uncertainty.

Do not train listeners toward an expected artifact during the scored round.

## Separate observation and preference

"A cymbal warbles at 01:14" is an observation; "I prefer B" is a preference. Record confidence and task impact. [The artifact guide](/blog/how-to-recognize-audio-compression-artifacts/) provides neutral vocabulary.

## Interpret limits honestly

ITU-R BS.1116 and BS.1534 specify controlled methods for particular assessment purposes. A household protocol lacks calibrated rooms, trained panels, and statistical design unless those are genuinely provided. State scope and avoid universal conclusions.

## Report and restore

Include protocol, versions, unknowns, excerpts, path, level method, order, listener codes, observations, preferences, repeats, fatigue, and limitations. Restore volume, route, processing, and track. Use [the audio checklist](/blog/an-audio-quality-literacy-checklist/) before sharing.

Current Norva audio controls require official verification. Norva organises and plays compatible authorised sources; it does not certify their mixes.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid visible labels, unmatched loudness, unlimited replay, leading language, unsafe volume, or changing the question after results. Informal tests support decisions, not universal codec rankings.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long should a listening session last?

No universal duration fits every task. Use short excerpts, planned trials, regular rests, and stop with fatigue.

### Must every listener agree?

No. Preserve individual observations, preferences, confidence, and context.

### Is random order enough to make a test scientific?

No. Source matching, level, environment, design, analysis, and qualified methods also matter.
