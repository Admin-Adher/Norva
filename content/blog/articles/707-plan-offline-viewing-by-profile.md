# Plan Offline Viewing Around Household Profiles

> **In short:** Coordinate household offline viewing by assigning every item to an intended profile and viewer before downloading. Record the device, required audio or subtitles, storage share, and who may remove the item. Keep progress on the correct profile, avoid simultaneous conflicting sessions, and verify the intended state after reconnection. Do not assume local storage is automatically separated by profile.

Profiles help keep progress, history, favourites, and preferences personal, but a shared device still has one physical storage pool. A household plan needs both account context and device-level ownership.

## Confirm the profile arrangement

List who will use the device and which profile each person should open. Norva's current plans describe profile capacity: Norva includes up to two profiles and Norva Family up to five. Profile count is not a device limit or a promise about simultaneous use.

If the household still relies on one shared profile, review why [individual household profiles matter](/blog/individual-household-media-profiles/) before preparing disconnected sessions. Separating viewers can reduce mixed progress, but every person still needs to confirm the active profile.

## Assign ownership before transfers

For every planned item, record:

- intended profile and viewer;
- exact title, episode, and version;
- planned session;
- required audio or subtitle option;
- observed local size;
- person authorised to remove it;
- expected reconnection order.

Ownership here means responsibility for the local plan, not ownership of media rights. The connected source and associated rights still govern eligibility.

## Share physical storage deliberately

Avoid an equal split unless it matches actual needs. One person may have a long disconnected period while another needs only one short episode. Start with real session windows, then allocate a conservative amount of space.

Do not assume the operating system exposes separate local media totals for each profile. Check the current app and device behaviour. The [shared-device offline plan](/blog/shared-device-offline-viewing-plan/) provides a device-first allocation method, while [dividing offline storage across devices](/blog/divide-offline-storage-across-devices/) covers households with more than one supported screen.

## Protect progress context

Before playback, confirm the profile again. While disconnected, avoid switching profiles or replaying the same episode under another person's profile unless the consequences are understood.

After reconnecting:

1. keep the original profile active;
2. allow the app time to connect;
3. verify the expected progress on that device;
4. then compare another supported device;
5. resolve any conflict before starting a new session.

Norva can sync progress, history, favourites, and preferences on supported devices. Reconnection is the point to verify the result, not assume which disconnected state will prevail.

## Respect personal track needs

Profile preferences do not prove that a required track is included in an offline copy. Each viewer should test the exact item and version. Use the [offline audio preparation checklist](/blog/prepare-audio-options-before-offline/) and its subtitle counterpart when language or accessibility is essential.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating profile count as a simultaneous-use or device limit.
- Assuming local items are automatically isolated by profile.
- Downloading under one profile and playing under another without checking.
- Splitting storage equally despite different trip needs.
- Removing another person's item without agreement.
- Assuming synced language preferences guarantee offline track inclusion.
- Starting conflicting sessions before previous progress reconnects.

Shared-device behaviour can vary by app and operating system. Verify the current Norva interface and device storage view before relying on the plan.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does every profile need its own device?

No. Profiles describe account organisation, while devices are separate. A shared device can serve several people if everyone confirms profile context and storage responsibilities.

### Can household members prepare the same episode?

They can plan it, but check whether one local copy can serve the intended workflow and whether progress will remain correctly associated. Do not assume duplication or sharing behaviour.

### Who should remove finished items?

Assign that decision before the trip. On a shared device, the intended viewer should confirm completion before another person removes the local copy.
