# Recent, Favorites, or All Titles: Which View Should You Use?

> **In short:** Use Recent when the next choice is connected to current activity, Favorites when you want a deliberately saved shortlist, and All Titles when you need complete catalogue scope. None is universally best. Pick the view that answers your first question, then apply filters or sorting only if the remaining set is still too broad.

Three library views can look like minor variations of the same grid. In practice, they represent three different kinds of intent: recency, personal curation, and completeness.

## Choose by the question in your head

Use this short decision tree:

1. **Am I returning to something I touched recently?** Start with Recent.
2. **Am I choosing from titles I deliberately saved?** Start with Favorites.
3. **Am I searching or exploring the whole connected catalogue?** Start with All Titles.

If none applies because you are resuming a partially watched title, Continue Watching may be the more direct route. Read [how Favorites, watchlists, and Continue Watching differ](/blog/favorites-watchlists-continue-watching/).

## Recent is an activity lens

Recent helps when context is still fresh. It can surface items opened, added, or otherwise treated as recent by the current experience and source metadata. Before relying on the label, observe what the current interface means by “recent”; it may not mean only “recently played.”

Good uses include:

- returning to a title you inspected earlier;
- checking newly surfaced catalogue activity;
- recovering context after switching screens;
- reviewing a short sequence of recent choices.

Recent is less useful for long-term curation. Its order changes with activity, so a title you want to keep should usually be added to Favorites instead of being preserved indirectly through repeated opening.

## Favorites is a deliberate shortlist

Favorites answers “What did I choose to keep?” It works well for:

- a household shortlist;
- a themed viewing plan;
- titles worth revisiting;
- candidates that should not be lost as Recent changes.

Norva synchronises favourites across supported devices. That makes Favorites a stable personal layer over a catalogue supplied by your compatible source.

Avoid adding everything that seems mildly interesting. A shortlist that approaches the size of All Titles no longer reduces the decision. Periodically remove entries that no longer represent an intention.

For cross-screen behaviour, see [keeping favourites consistent across screens](/blog/sync-favorites-across-devices/).

## All Titles is the complete search space

All Titles is the right starting point when:

- you know a title but not where it was classified;
- you are auditing duplicates or metadata;
- you want to compare categories;
- a filtered view appears incomplete;
- you are exploring without a preselected shortlist.

Because it exposes the broadest scope, All Titles benefits most from deliberate filters and sorting. Begin with one strong condition and add another only when it improves the choice.

The guide to [sorting a personal media library](/blog/sort-personal-media-library/) helps decide what should appear first after selecting the scope.

## Combine views with filters carefully

The view sets the scope; filters refine it. For example:

- Favorites plus audio language creates a saved shortlist matching tonight's language need.
- All Titles plus year supports an era retrospective.
- Recent plus availability can remove unusable entries from a current activity list.

Do not forget which scope remains active. An unexpectedly small result may be caused by Favorites rather than the visible year or language filter.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating Recent as a permanent watchlist.
- Filling Favorites until it no longer reduces choice.
- Assuming All Titles means every item is currently available.
- Leaving a narrow view active while troubleshooting a missing title.
- Confusing Continue Watching with Favorites.
- Applying several filters before selecting the correct scope.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Favorites the same as Continue Watching?

No. Favorites reflects a deliberate save; Continue Watching reflects playback progress. A title can belong to both, either, or neither.

### Which view is best for discovering something new?

All Titles provides the widest scope, while Favorites provides a trusted shortlist. Choose based on whether breadth or personal curation matters more for that session.

### Why is a title missing from Recent?

The current experience may define recency differently from your expectation, or more recent activity may have moved the title down. Search All Titles before assuming the catalogue entry is missing.
