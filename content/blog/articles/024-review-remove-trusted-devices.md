# How to Review and Remove Trusted Devices

> **In short:** Open Norva’s current account-security or device controls, compare each record with a device you own and still use, and remove devices you sold, lost, returned, or no longer recognise. Preserve a redacted record of an unfamiliar entry before removal, then secure the account and contact support if access may be compromised.

**Draft verification notice:** exact menu labels, device fields, and sign-out behaviour require current human verification.

## Why device review matters

Norva’s privacy policy lists trusted-device records, device tokens, and pairing codes as data used to connect supported screens to one account.

A trusted-device list can outlive the physical device if you replace, sell, or reset it without removing account access. A short review reduces forgotten sessions.

Pairing itself is covered in [How to Pair a TV With Your Norva Account](/blog/pair-tv-with-norva-account/).

## When to review the list

Review devices:

- after pairing or signing in on a new screen;
- before and after selling or returning hardware;
- after losing a phone or TV device;
- when an entry is unfamiliar;
- after changing important account credentials;
- during a periodic account-security review.

Do not infer that profile count equals device count. Norva and Norva Family profile limits are not documented as device or simultaneous-use limits.

## Step 1: open current account controls

Use an official Norva session on a device you control. Navigate to the current trusted-device or security area.

**Observable result:** a list of account-associated devices or sessions is visible.

If no such control appears, check current support information rather than inventing a route.

## Step 2: inventory your real devices

Privately list devices you control, including general type and a recognisable name. Do not publish serial numbers or personal labels.

Compare that inventory with Norva’s records. A record may not use the marketing name printed on the device, so rely on available dates and context carefully.

Classify each entry:

- current and recognised;
- old but recognised;
- temporary or borrowed;
- unknown;
- insufficient information.

## Step 3: preserve an unknown entry safely

Before removing an unfamiliar record, capture only the non-sensitive details needed for support: generic device label, displayed date, and approximate discovery time.

Redact account identifiers, source information, and unrelated devices. Do not keep a suspicious session active merely to gather more data.

## Step 4: remove obsolete access

Use the current remove, revoke, or sign-out control for one device at a time. Confirm the intended record before approval.

**Observable result:** the entry disappears or changes state, and the removed device can no longer use the authenticated account after normal reconnection.

Publication requires this result to be verified rather than assumed.

## Step 5: secure the account when necessary

For an unknown device:

1. remove or revoke it;
2. change the Norva password through the official process;
3. review the remaining device list;
4. inspect account email or security notices;
5. protect source credentials separately if exposure is possible;
6. contact [Norva support](https://norva.tv/support) with redacted evidence.

No security response can guarantee that an account was never accessed. Focus on observable controls and documented recovery.

## Moving or replacing a device

A device migration should preserve account-level state while handling local data separately. Eligible offline downloads are stored on the device and are not uploaded to Norva, according to its privacy policy.

Use [Moving to a New Device](/blog/move-norva-to-new-device/) before wiping the old hardware.

## Device removal is not account deletion

Removing one trusted device should revoke that device’s account relationship; it is not the same as deleting the Norva account.

Norva states that account deletion can be requested in the app or through its [account-deletion page](https://norva.tv/delete-account.html). The exact effects require current policy review.

## Keep a minimal review log

Record date, number of recognised and removed entries, and actions taken. Do not store device tokens or secrets.

The planned [media-player account security guide](/blog/secure-media-player-account/) will combine device, password, and recovery practices.

## Limitations

Device labels may be ambiguous. Do not accuse another person based only on an unfamiliar label. Contact support when records cannot be reconciled.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I remove a device that is temporarily offline?

Not if you still control and use it. Remove it when access is no longer needed or the device is not trusted.

### What if an entry has no clear name?

Use date and context cautiously, preserve a redacted record, and ask support rather than guessing.

### Will removing a device erase its local downloads?

Device revocation and local storage are separate. Verify current behaviour before wiping or transferring hardware.
