# The Complete Guide to Tablet Viewing Workflows

> **In short:** A dependable tablet viewing workflow has five stages: prepare the device, choose a stable position, enter the correct account context, verify the item and playback state, then close or hand off the session deliberately. Treat the tablet as a flexible screen, not as a smaller television, and adapt posture, input, audio, and interruptions to the situation.

Norva is a software media player and organiser. You connect a compatible media source that you own or are authorised to use; the subscription does not include a media catalogue. The guidance below focuses on managing the viewing experience, not on acquiring media.

## Use a five-stage session model

Think of every tablet session as a short sequence:

1. **Prepare:** confirm power, connectivity, storage, audio route, and interruption settings.
2. **Orient:** choose handheld, propped, or docked use and place essential controls within reach.
3. **Enter:** check the account or profile, open the intended library area, and confirm item identity.
4. **Watch and recover:** know what should happen after a lock, rotation, app switch, or network change.
5. **Close or hand off:** pause, confirm progress, clear private traces when appropriate, and leave the next screen in a known state.

The sequence is deliberately platform-neutral. Menu names and background behaviour vary by operating system, app version, and device. Use the current controls visible on your tablet rather than assuming a particular gesture or shortcut exists.

## Match the setup to the session

A ten-minute browse and a long episode need different preparation. Start with four questions.

| Question | If the answer is yes | Practical response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Will you hold the tablet throughout? | Hands and viewing angle may change | Reduce unnecessary reach and plan a stable rest point |
| Will the session be long? | Power and comfort matter more | Use the [long-session preparation workflow](/blog/how-to-prepare-a-tablet-for-a-long-viewing-session/) |
| Is the tablet shared? | Account history and privacy can mix | Confirm the profile before browsing and sign out when required |
| Might you move to another screen? | Identity and progress must remain clear | Record the item, episode, version, and approximate position |

For posture trade-offs, compare [handheld, propped, and docked positions](/blog/handheld-propped-up-or-docked-choose-a-tablet-viewing-position/). No position is universally best. Stability, glare, reach, audio, nearby power, and whether you need a keyboard all influence the choice.

## Build a low-friction browsing layer

Keep the path from the tablet home screen to the library predictable. Place the verified Norva shortcut where it is easy to recognise, remove misleading duplicate shortcuts, and avoid a home screen filled with unrelated alerts. Inside the library, begin broad and narrow only as needed: section, filter, item, then detail screen.

When a library is large, preserve context. Before opening a detail view, note the active filter and your approximate place in the results. If you connect a keyboard, first verify which keys move focus and whether focus is visibly indicated; do not guess undocumented shortcuts.

## Verify playback before settling in

A quick preflight catches many avoidable surprises:

- confirm the title, year, season, and episode when those fields are relevant;
- check the selected version if several variants are grouped;
- verify the available audio and subtitle choices, which depend on the source and media;
- confirm the sound is routed to the intended speakers or headphones;
- test that play, pause, and the visible timeline behave as expected;
- if offline use is planned, verify eligibility and playback while disconnected before leaving.

Norva can preserve progress, history, favourites, and preferences across supported devices under the same account. That does not remove the need to verify the current item. Two similarly named versions or adjacent episodes can still be confused during a fast handoff.

## Plan for interruptions instead of fighting them

Screen lock, rotation, incoming alerts, switching apps, and changing audio outputs can all interrupt a tablet session. Before viewing, decide which interruptions are acceptable. Use the tablet's documented focus or notification controls if available, but preserve alerts you genuinely need.

After any interruption, verify the item identity, playback position, and audio/subtitle state. W3C guidance discourages unnecessary orientation restrictions, but actual app behaviour must still be checked on the supported device.

## Original evidence: the tablet session card

Copy this card into a note and complete it for a real session:

- intended item or browsing goal: ___
- account/profile confirmed: yes / no
- position: handheld / propped / docked
- input: touch / keyboard / other visible control
- power and network checked: yes / no
- audio route: ___
- interruption mode: normal / reduced / custom
- current item, episode, and version: ___
- recovery check after lock or rotation: passed / not checked
- close action: pause / hand off / sign out / remove local item

Use the longer [tablet viewing workflow checklist](/blog/a-tablet-viewing-workflow-checklist/) when preparing travel or a shared-device session.

## Limitations and common mistakes

Avoid treating a tablet as a universal environment. A control available in one operating system or browser may be absent elsewhere. Source compatibility, media tracks, offline eligibility, and rights also vary.

Common mistakes include starting under the wrong profile, assuming the last audio route is still active, rotating without checking context, relying on an unverified keyboard shortcut, filling local storage too closely, and walking away from a shared tablet while still signed in. A workflow reduces these risks, but it cannot guarantee connectivity, source availability, or playback quality.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a separate tablet profile for every viewing situation?

Not necessarily. Use an available profile when separate history, favourites, or preferences are genuinely needed. Check the current Norva plan details and avoid inventing a profile structure that the account does not provide.

### Is landscape always better for video?

No. The useful orientation depends on the media, interface, tablet support, posture, and accessibility needs. Choose the orientation that keeps content and controls usable.

### Can I start on a tablet and continue on a TV?

Norva states that progress can follow the same account across supported devices. Before continuing, confirm the same item, episode, version, and position on the target screen.
