# Episode Numbering Mismatch After Import? Build a Sequence Map

> **In short:** Confirm the exact series and source version, then map source and Norva season labels, episode numbers, titles, durations, specials, first and last boundaries, language cues, profile, device, application version, and import timeline. Classify renamed, shifted, combined, split, special, alternate order, different version, or unknown without assuming one numbering system is universal. Preserve source data and avoid renumbering, renaming, or repeated imports before support reviews the map.

Episode numbers can follow broadcast, production, streaming, absolute, regional, disc, or provider-specific order. The source's visible terminology matters more than a household's remembered sequence.

Keep one unaffected neighboring episode in the map as a control. Its stable label, title, and duration help show where the visible sequence first diverges without requiring a complete season export.

## Confirm the exact series version

Record source label, title code, year, edition, language, artwork, season count, and visible version cues. Similar series titles, remakes, regional releases, and alternate cuts can produce different episode structures.

The [category and metadata handbook](/blog/category-metadata-troubleshooting-handbook/) provides the identity matrix.

## Record source terminology

Through the provider's official authorized route, transcribe season labels, episode labels, titles, specials, and order type where the provider explicitly names it. Do not rename “Specials,” “Season 0,” parts, volumes, or absolute numbers in the evidence.

## Record Norva terminology

Capture season and episode labels in series detail, season selector, episode list, search, progress row, and grouped version only where relevant. A mismatch limited to one view is distinct from a sequence used everywhere.

## Choose sequence boundaries

Map the first two episodes, one transition around the mismatch, the final episode, and specials. This sample often shows an offset, skipped label, combined episode, or moved special without exporting the complete series catalog.

## Pair titles and durations

Numbers alone are weak identity evidence. Record title cues and displayed duration for each mapped row. A combined episode can have a different duration; an alternate title can reveal another order. Use the [runtime guide](/blog/runtime-differs-between-versions/) without treating duration as a unique identifier.

## Build the season gap context

If an entire season appears absent, use the [season gap map](/blog/seasons-missing-after-series-import/) before shifting episode numbers. Missing membership and numbering mismatch are separate problems.

## Freeze view and profile context

Record account, profile, enabled sources, availability, audio, subtitles, search, sorting, grouping, device, operating system, application version, and timestamp. Filters can hide episodes and make a sequence appear to skip numbers.

## Preserve the import timeline

Record source confirmation, import request, visible stages, completion, first mismatch, retries, source edits, and current state. Timing does not prove the import changed numbering. Use current official timing guidance only when published.

## Compare another supported device

Use the same account, profile, series version, filters, grouping, and close timestamp. Record both application versions. A sequence difference on one device is cross-screen evidence, not proof of local metadata storage.

## Avoid renumbering first

Do not rename files, edit season or episode numbers, merge episodes, move specials, repeat imports, or clear application data before saving the map. An edit changes identity cues and can affect progress or favorites.

## Classify the mismatch

Use different series version, source order differs, season label differs, fixed offset, missing episode, combined or split episode, special placement, title-number disagreement, device-specific presentation, or unknown. Do not declare one order correct without authoritative version evidence.

## Prepare support evidence

Use the [metadata evidence pack](/blog/metadata-support-evidence-pack/) with series cues, representative sequence rows, source and Norva terminology, view context, timeline, and actions. Exclude complete episode descriptions and credentials.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming one numbering order is universal.
- Comparing different series editions or languages.
- Mapping numbers without titles and durations.
- Treating missing episodes as a simple offset.
- Renumbering source data before evidence capture.
- Exporting complete private episode metadata.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which episode order is correct?

Use the authoritative source for the exact edition and explicitly labeled order. Do not decide from title familiarity alone.

### Should specials always be Season 0?

Do not assume universal labeling. Record the source and Norva terms exactly and compare membership.

### Can I rename episodes to fix the view?

Not before preserving the sequence map, confirming metadata ownership, and following provider and Norva support guidance.
