# Prepare Offline Storage Before a System Update

> **In short:** Before a system update, record the offline library, required versions and tracks, current app and device storage, account access, and a protected free-space reserve. Follow the device maker's current backup, power, and connectivity instructions, but do not assume a platform backup preserves app-managed offline media. After the update, reconnect safely, open Norva, and repeat the exact offline test.

System updates are important device-maintenance events. They can also coincide with storage checks, restarts, app updates, changed permissions, or a long period when the device is unavailable. A short evidence-based plan reduces surprises.

## Choose a low-risk update window

Do not schedule a major device update immediately before boarding, leaving home connectivity, or starting a planned offline session. Leave time to complete the update, sign in if required, update dependent apps when appropriate, and test the media again.

Follow the current instructions from the device maker. Compatibility, power, connection, and storage requirements can vary by model and operating-system release.

## Record the offline state

Create an inventory similar to the [pre-device-change record](/blog/record-offline-library-before-device-change/). Capture:

- device model and current system version;
- Norva app version;
- account and profile;
- exact local items and versions;
- required audio and subtitle tracks;
- last disconnected test date;
- current device free space and app footprint;
- items that could be prepared again if still eligible.

Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and stored on the device. Treat the inventory as a rebuilding guide, not a backup of the media files.

## Protect enough working space

Review the device storage screen and preserve the [free-space reserve](/blog/set-free-space-reserve-offline-media/) used for normal operation. The update process may present its own current storage requirement or recommendations. Follow those instructions rather than applying a generic number.

Remove finished offline items through the app if space is needed. Do not clear all app storage or uninstall as a quick way to make room; Norva's privacy policy says uninstalling removes downloaded media.

## Back up the device using current guidance

Use the platform's official backup process for the device data it supports. Confirm completion rather than assuming an automatic backup ran. However, do not label app-managed offline media as recoverable unless current Norva and platform documentation expressly confirms it for that situation.

Also confirm that you can access the account and authorized media source after restart without recording credentials in the update worksheet.

## Update, then rebuild confidence

After the system update:

1. verify the expected operating-system version;
2. check date, time, network, audio output, and available storage;
3. open Norva and confirm the intended account and profile;
4. compare the offline inventory with the pre-update record;
5. verify required audio and subtitles;
6. disable connectivity and sample exact items;
7. document any difference before making another change.

If the app also updates, use the [post-app-update storage review](/blog/review-offline-storage-after-app-update/) to separate device-level and app-level changes.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Updating just before a disconnected journey.
- Assuming a device backup includes local encrypted media.
- Removing the reserve to satisfy an improvised item count.
- Clearing all app data before recording the library.
- Forgetting exact audio and subtitle selections.
- Testing only artwork or the first few seconds.
- Changing several settings before documenting the first failure.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will a system update delete offline items?

Do not promise either outcome. Record the library, follow current device guidance, and verify the exact items after the update.

### Should I remove every offline item first?

Not automatically. Remove finished items if storage is needed, preserve the inventory, and follow the update's current requirements.

### Is a device backup enough preparation?

No. It is one layer. You also need an offline inventory, track record, account-access check, and post-update playback test.
