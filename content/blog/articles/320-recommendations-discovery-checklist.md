# A Recommendations and Discovery Quality Checklist

> **In short:** A ready discovery candidate needs a clear brief, appropriate route, verified seed and identity, visible relationship evidence, sufficient metadata, a suitable exact version, current source availability, and required access options. Unknowns in identity, version, availability, or accessibility block Ready status. Record limits rather than inventing recommendation logic.

Use this checklist before publishing discovery guidance, after a source or interface change, or when a recommendation path repeatedly fails to produce usable choices.

Date the review and record source context, profile, supported screen, product version if visible, and filters.

## Gates 1–3: establish direction

- [ ] The brief states viewer, occasion, constraint, and desired outcome.
- [ ] Search, browsing, or recommendations match the starting certainty.
- [ ] The seed is the intended work and version.
- [ ] Route switches have a stated reason.
- [ ] A stop condition exists.

Use [the complete recommendations and discovery guide](/blog/recommendations-discovery-guide/) for the route model.

## Gates 4–6: prove the relationship

- [ ] Work, series, episode, and version identities are not confused.
- [ ] At least one visible field explains why the candidate is related.
- [ ] Meaningful differences are recorded.
- [ ] Blank, conflicting, and not-applicable metadata are separate.
- [ ] Artwork and row order are not primary evidence.
- [ ] No hidden weighting or algorithm is claimed without proof.

DCMI terms and EIDR’s media hierarchy support consistent field names. They do not establish Norva’s internal selection method.

## Gates 7–9: verify readiness

- [ ] Exact edition, season, episode, duration, and source record are checked.
- [ ] Required audio, subtitles, or other media access needs are present.
- [ ] The intended supported screen and profile are confirmed.
- [ ] Current availability is separate from work relevance.
- [ ] Offline use is described only when source, device, media, and rights permit it.
- [ ] An unsuitable or unavailable version is labelled Research, not Ready.

W3C media accessibility requirements establish the importance of captions, subtitles, descriptions, and language alternatives. Apply [the focused-shortlist funnel](/blog/turn-recommendations-into-shortlist/) after these gates pass.

## Gates 10–12: protect decision quality

- [ ] Candidates add meaningful alternatives rather than poster-level duplicates.
- [ ] Recent and familiar titles do not receive unexamined priority.
- [ ] The shortlist has one occasion and expiry.
- [ ] Ready and Research are visibly separate.
- [ ] Private viewing preferences are not exposed unnecessarily.
- [ ] Audit findings state sample, context, date, and limitations.
- [ ] Percentages are not generalised from weak samples.

Use [the relevance audit protocol](/blog/audit-recommendation-relevance/) when several seeds or patterns require structured review.

## Decide whether the path passes

Brief, seed identity, candidate identity, relationship evidence, version, availability, and access needs are critical. Fail or Unknown blocks Ready status. A candidate can remain a useful Research lead while those questions are resolved.

Norva’s public features describe recommendations from a connected compatible source. The subscription does not supply the media library, and exact current behavior requires verification.

## Prioritise failed gates

Resolve identity and access failures before variety or shortlist polish. A wrong episode, unsuitable version, unavailable source item, or missing required subtitle blocks the viewing action; a repetitive but otherwise valid set affects choice quality rather than basic readiness. Record that distinction so the audit produces a sensible repair order instead of treating all twelve gates as equally urgent.

## Original evidence: twelve-gate checklist

Apply the sheet to one three-hop session and one category-browse session. Ask a second reviewer to challenge every Pass from visible evidence. Downgrade unsupported claims to Unknown and route each failure to one diagnostic.

The checklist measures decision and evidence quality in the reviewed sessions. It cannot rank products or guarantee future recommendations.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Passing identity from a matching poster.
- Treating relevance as version readiness.
- Hiding unresolved candidates inside Ready.
- Correcting metadata to improve audit results.
- Claiming variety from a tiny unlabelled sample.
- Leaving a discovery list without an expiry.

## Frequently asked questions

### Must every candidate pass all twelve gates?

Only relevant gates apply, but identity, relationship, version, availability, and access remain mandatory for Ready status.

### Can an unavailable candidate pass relevance?

Yes. Keep relevance and availability separate, then classify it as Research rather than Ready.

### When should the checklist be repeated?

After material source, metadata, device, profile, or interface changes and before publishing broad quality claims.
