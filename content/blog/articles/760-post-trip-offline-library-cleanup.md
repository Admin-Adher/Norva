# A Post-Trip Offline Library Cleanup

> **In short:** Finish the post-travel reconnection routine before deleting local media. Then classify every item as finished, still planned, justified fallback, duplicate, failed, or unexplained. Confirm its owner and profile, remove finished or unnecessary copies through Norva's item-level controls, wait for device storage to refresh, restore the protected reserve, and assign every retained item a new review date and cleanup owner.

Travel batches are built for temporary conditions. Once the trip ends, the same items can become silent storage debt: they occupy space, confuse future estimates, and make it harder to distinguish a current plan from an old fallback.

## Release cleanup only after reconnection

Use the [safe post-travel reconnection routine](/blog/safe-reconnection-after-travel/) first. Confirm desired progress, history, favorites, and preferences before removing local copies. If several devices held different offline progress, preserve the reconciliation ledger until the account state is understood.

Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and device-local, while selected account data can synchronize. Deleting local media too early can remove useful diagnostic evidence even though it should not be treated as the progress record itself.

## Inventory each device separately

Open Norva's offline area on every supported phone or tablet. Record the account, profile, item, version, audio, subtitles, last playback, test date, and owner. Compare with current device free space and app footprint.

Do not infer Device B's inventory from Device A. Local media does not become present everywhere merely because account state synchronized.

## Classify every item

Use six categories:

- **finished:** viewed and no longer needed locally;
- **upcoming:** tied to a named near-term session;
- **fallback:** protects a specific credible failure;
- **duplicate:** another verified device serves the same need;
- **failed:** incomplete or unavailable and requiring a decision;
- **unexplained:** app or storage state does not match the inventory.

An item without a current purpose should not remain because preparation took effort. Ask the owner before removing another profile's batch.

## Remove through the app in small rounds

Follow the [offline item removal guide](/blog/remove-offline-downloads/). Read the exact confirmation, remove a small finished batch, confirm it disappears from the offline area, and wait for the operating-system storage report to update.

Android's official guidance distinguishes clearing temporary cache from clearing all app storage and recommends deleting downloaded media from the app that created it. Platform labels vary. Do not clear all application data or manipulate encrypted files to remove individual items.

## Restore normal storage boundaries

Compare current free space with the normal device reserve for updates, photos, messages, maps, and other apps. Stop cleanup when the reserve and current plan are healthy; do not delete a still-needed item merely to maximize an abstract number.

If storage growth remains unexplained, open an exception in the [recurring offline storage audit](/blog/recurring-offline-storage-audit/) and change one category at a time.

## Reset travel-specific settings

Review roaming, metered-network settings, automatic updates, brightness, battery mode, caption settings, and temporary pairings. Restore only what the household intentionally changed, using current device guidance. Keep accessibility settings that remain useful.

Assign every retained batch a new review date, planned viewer, and cleanup owner.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Deleting local media before progress reconciliation.
- Treating account sync as a local inventory.
- Removing another viewer's batch without confirmation.
- Clearing all app storage to remove a few items.
- Expecting device storage totals to refresh instantly.
- Keeping every travel fallback indefinitely.
- Restoring settings from memory rather than the trip record.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I remove every travel item?

No. Keep items tied to a current session or justified fallback, and give them a new review date. Remove clutter deliberately.

### Why does the app footprint remain larger than the item list?

App code, settings, cache, temporary data, and reporting timing can contribute. Record the difference and investigate one category at a time.

### Is uninstalling a cleanup shortcut?

No. Norva's privacy policy says uninstalling removes downloaded media, and broader app state may also be affected. Use item-level controls first.
