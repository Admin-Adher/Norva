# Created a Duplicate Account? Safe Next Steps

> **In short:** Stop creating accounts or starting purchases. Sign in to each account separately and compare masked email, creation timing, profiles, devices, source setup, and entitlement. Identify the original billing provider and active renewals. Choose the intended primary account, preserve minimal redacted proof, and ask official Norva support what can be recovered or moved. Never assume profiles merge automatically. Cancel and close only after confirming which account and provider control each record.

A duplicate can arise from using another email, a mistyped address, or repeating sign-up while entitlement appears delayed. The safest response is reconciliation, not more experimentation.

## Freeze account-changing actions

Do not start another trial, repeat a purchase, change both emails, delete either account, or disconnect the only working source. These actions can erase clues and create further billing states.

Write down the two masked sign-ins and current date without recording passwords or source credentials.

## Compare each account in isolation

Use a private browser window or sign out fully before switching. Record which profiles, history, progress, favorites, device records, source settings, and subscription status appear. Do not assume the account with more data is the billed account.

The [media-account lifecycle handbook](/blog/media-account-lifecycle-handbook/) provides the categories needed for this inventory.

## Identify every billing relationship

Check official subscription pages for authorized store and payment accounts. Match plan label, renewal date, receipt issuer, and purchase account. The [billing-provider guide](/blog/identify-subscription-billing-provider/) helps identify the source of truth without exposing financial data.

There may be zero, one, or more active subscriptions. Do not infer the count from one Norva screen.

## Choose the primary account deliberately

Consider which sign-in is accessible and secure, which account holds the desired profiles and source, which email should receive notices, and which one is associated with entitlement. If factors conflict, ask official support before changing anything.

Do not promise that progress, profiles, favorites, or entitlement can be merged. Current implementation and provider rules determine available options.

## Build a minimal support packet

Include masked account emails, approximate creation dates, device and application version, profile counts, provider name, redacted purchase date, exact error or status wording, and desired outcome. Exclude passwords, one-time codes, tokens, source addresses, source credentials, card data, and full receipts.

If entitlement is stale, follow the [subscription-status checklist](/blog/subscription-status-not-updating/) before sending the packet.

## Cancel only the unwanted renewal

If more than one subscription exists, use the original provider for the unwanted purchase and record the resulting status and access-through date. Cancellation and Norva account closure remain separate.

Do not request a refund as though it were guaranteed. Use the provider's current policy and support process for the actual transaction.

## Close only after preserving proof

Once the primary account works, billing is reconciled, needed state is preserved, and support questions are resolved, prepare the duplicate for closure. The [closure-evidence guide](/blog/close-account-keep-cancellation-proof/) prevents a confirmation email or receipt from disappearing too early.

Verify the primary account again after the duplicate is closed.

Keep both sign-ins out of shared password fields during reconciliation, label browser sessions clearly, and have one administrator perform the final action. After closure, check that household devices open the chosen account and that no unexpected renewal remains with the provider. Record the final verification date so the same duplicate is not investigated again later.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Buying again to make access appear.
- Deleting the empty-looking account before checking billing.
- Assuming data or entitlement can be merged.
- Changing both email addresses simultaneously.
- Sharing complete receipts with support.
- Canceling the subscription attached to the desired account.
- Treating Norva entitlement as final billing proof.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can Norva merge two accounts?

Do not assume so. Ask official support about current capabilities and describe the desired primary account with minimal evidence.

### Which account should I keep?

Choose only after comparing access, security, profiles, source, entitlement, billing provider, and future email ownership.

### Should I delete the duplicate immediately?

No. First preserve evidence, reconcile subscriptions, resolve state with support, and verify the intended primary account.
