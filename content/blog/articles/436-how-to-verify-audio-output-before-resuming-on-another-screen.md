# How to Verify Audio Output Before Resuming on Another Screen

> **In short:** Keep playback paused, lower the target's level, and check two independent states: the selected media audio track and the physical output route. Connect or select the intended speakers or headphones through documented device controls, then run a short non-sensitive test. Resume normally only after sound comes from the expected output with the intended track.

A handoff can preserve the item and position while still sending sound to tablet speakers, a previously connected accessory, or another output. That matters for privacy and usability.

## Record the intended audio state

Before leaving the source, note:

- selected audio track;
- source output route;
- intended target output;
- subtitle state;
- item, version, and position;
- whether the target is in a shared space.

The source output is context, not a setting that necessarily transfers. TV speakers and tablet headphones are local device states.

## Match item and audio track first

On the supported target, verify account, profile, authorised source, item, and version. Then inspect the audio tracks available for that version.

The [audio-choice handoff guide](/blog/how-to-preserve-an-audio-choice-during-a-device-handoff/) explains why a stored preference cannot create an absent track.

## Reduce level before routing

Use the target's visible or physical control to choose a low comfortable starting point. No universal number applies across devices, recordings, speakers, or listeners.

W3C audio-control guidance supports user control over automatically playing sound, but it does not determine the target's device-specific routing controls.

## Select the intended output

Use current official instructions for the target device. For a wireless output, verify the displayed device identity rather than selecting a similar name. For a wired output, follow manufacturer guidance and inspect equipment for visible damage.

A “connected” state does not always prove media audio uses that route. Keep the pause control reachable.

## Run a privacy-safe test

Resume for a brief non-sensitive section, listen at the intended output, and check that no unintended speaker also plays. Pause again.

If privacy matters, ask another person to stand near the target speakers or use a neutral system sound only when documented and appropriate. Do not expose private dialogue to test routing.

## Recheck subtitles and position

Confirm the media track, subtitles, and approximate timeline after the route change. Output selection and media language are separate.

For tablet targets, the [speaker-and-headphone switching routine](/blog/how-to-switch-safely-between-speakers-and-headphones-on-a-tablet/) provides a device-position and cable check.

## Resume once

When track and route both match, resume once and wait for the timeline to advance. Keep the source paused until the target is confirmed.

The [cross-device state guide](/blog/a-state-by-state-guide-to-cross-device-viewing-handoff/) adds profile, version, source access, and target readiness.

## If sound uses the wrong route

Pause immediately. Capture the displayed route, disconnect or deselect only through documented controls, and test one route at a time. Avoid repeated toggles that hide which action changed the state.

If the same route error repeats with the same verified item and output, prepare a privacy-safe support report.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid testing at the previous screen's level, confusing track with output, selecting a wireless device by a truncated name, and resuming sensitive content before verification.

Accessory support, range, battery, operating-system policy, and device capabilities vary. This workflow cannot certify equipment or guarantee uninterrupted audio.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does the output route follow my Norva profile?

Do not assume it does. Treat output as a local target-device state and verify it every handoff.

### Why is the correct language playing from the wrong speaker?

Language is the media track; speakers are the output route. They are selected in different layers.

### Should I disconnect headphones after the session?

On a shared device, doing so can prevent later audio routing elsewhere. Follow the device and household routine.
