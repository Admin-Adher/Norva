# Plan Viewing for Predictable Connection Gaps

> **In short:** Map the last dependable connection, the expected disconnected window, and the first trusted reconnection. Prepare only the viewing time you can realistically use, confirm each exact item is eligible, and test playback with all network access disabled. Verify required tracks, battery, and storage, then decide how progress and local items will be reviewed after reconnecting.

A known connection gap is easier to manage than an unexpected outage, but only if the plan is tied to its boundaries. “Probably offline sometime tomorrow” is too vague to decide what must be ready and when testing should stop.

## Draw the three boundaries

Record three moments:

1. **last dependable connection:** the final place and time where preparation can be corrected;
2. **disconnected window:** the period when connected playback or account recovery should not be assumed;
3. **first trusted reconnection:** where account state and progress can be checked again.

Examples include a route with known tunnels, planned home network maintenance, or a stay in an area where connectivity is uncertain. Do not treat a forecast as a guarantee; preserve a non-media fallback.

## Plan for the actual gap

Estimate only the sessions inside the disconnected window. A long gap may contain work, sleep, meals, or activities that leave little viewing time. Use the [offline viewing-hours guide](/blog/estimate-offline-viewing-hours/) to set a conservative ceiling.

Choose items that fit those sessions and one modest alternative. If no eligible item fits, use the [no-download fallback plan](/blog/create-no-download-fallback-plan/) rather than overloading the device with uncertain copies.

## Prepare before the boundary

For each intended item:

- open the exact title, episode, and version;
- confirm a current offline action is available;
- verify use is permitted by the connected source and associated rights;
- complete preparation on a trusted connection;
- check the intended audio and subtitle options;
- record observed storage;
- assign a review or removal date.

Norva offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. Eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device, not uploaded to Norva.

## Simulate the gap

Before the last dependable connection, disable Wi-Fi and mobile data. Airplane mode can help, but some devices let a radio be re-enabled, so inspect the actual state.

Close and reopen the app, find the item through its offline area, and sample more than the opening moment. Test essential tracks. The focused [offline availability verification](/blog/verify-offline-availability-before-leaving/) provides a pass-or-recheck record.

If a test fails, fix it before the boundary or remove the item from the plan. A progress indicator or connected preview is not a substitute.

## Protect the device during the gap

Review battery using a representative playback observation, not a generic promise. Preserve power for communication, navigation, tickets, or other essential functions. Keep the device locked when not in use and follow location or transport rules.

On a shared device, confirm the active profile before each session. Avoid creating conflicting progress on the same item across multiple disconnected devices unless you have decided how to reconcile it.

## Plan the first reconnection

Reconnect through a trusted network, keep the intended account and profile active, and allow the app to update before comparing another device. Use the [first-reconnection workflow](/blog/plan-first-reconnection-after-offline/) to verify progress and review the batch without guessing which state won.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Planning for total gap duration rather than usable sessions.
- Waiting until after the last dependable connection to test.
- Assuming airplane mode always reflects every radio state.
- Relying on an item that remains in Recheck.
- Ignoring power needed for essential device functions.
- Reconnecting and immediately changing progress on another device.
- Treating a predictable gap as proof that all conditions will match the forecast.

## Frequently asked questions

### What if connectivity remains available after all?

You can still use the verified local set. Avoid unnecessary changes simply because a network appears; decide whether it is trusted and suitable first.

### Should I prepare the entire gap duration?

No. Prepare for realistic viewing windows within the gap, plus one modest fallback if justified.

### Can I rely on the same plan next time?

Reuse the method, not the old result. Recheck after changes to the item, source, app, device, account, or associated rights.
