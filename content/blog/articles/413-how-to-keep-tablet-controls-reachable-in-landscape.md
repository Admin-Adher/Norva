# How to Keep Tablet Controls Reachable in Landscape

> **In short:** Stabilise the tablet in the position you will actually use, then test pause, return, timeline, audio, subtitle, and filter controls one at a time. Mark which are reachable with the left hand, right hand, both hands, or only when propped. Change the position before relying on an uncomfortable or unstable reach.

Landscape makes a wide image natural for many videos, but it also spreads controls across a larger horizontal area. A centre timeline may be easy on a stand and awkward while handheld. Reachability is a relationship between interface, posture, tablet size, case, and input method.

## Identify essential controls

List only the controls needed during the session:

- reveal and hide playback controls;
- pause and resume;
- return to the detail or library screen;
- move within the timeline when intentionally required;
- inspect audio and subtitle choices;
- adjust a visible filter or episode list;
- exit or lock the tablet safely.

Do not include every control on the screen. The aim is to guarantee access to essential actions, not maximise one-handed use.

## 1. Choose the real posture

Hold, prop, or dock the tablet exactly as it will be used. Sit in the intended place and account for a case, charging cable, headphones, or keyboard. A reach test performed while standing over a desk does not represent sofa or bed use.

Compare positions with the [handheld, propped, or docked decision guide](/blog/handheld-propped-up-or-docked-choose-a-tablet-viewing-position/).

## 2. Stabilise before tapping

For handheld use, identify a grip that does not cover openings or press edge controls. For a stand, confirm that a normal tap does not slide or tip the device. Route cables so they do not pull against the support.

If reaching requires changing the grip, pause first. A two-handed action is preferable to balancing the tablet on fingertips while pressing a distant control.

## 3. Map three touch zones

Divide the screen conceptually into left, centre, and right zones. From the real posture, test one harmless control in each zone. Record whether it is comfortable, possible with a grip change, or unreliable.

W3C target-size guidance applies to web content and establishes accessibility expectations for activation areas. It does not guarantee the size of any tablet control. If a target remains difficult, change posture or input rather than repeatedly tapping near it.

## 4. Test the pause path

Start a non-critical item at low volume. Reveal controls and pause once. Rotate only if needed, then resume. The pause path must work even when a temporary layer, subtitle panel, or keyboard focus is present.

If pause is not reliably reachable, do not proceed with a long session. Move the tablet to a support or keep both hands available.

## 5. Test secondary controls while paused

While playback is paused, open and close audio or subtitle selection, inspect the timeline, and return to the detail screen. Available language and subtitle tracks depend on the source and media.

The [touch navigation guide](/blog/how-to-navigate-a-large-library-with-tablet-touch-gestures/) helps avoid starting swipes on interactive overlays.

## 6. Decide the operating mode

Choose one:

- **handheld, one primary hand:** only if all essential controls are reliable;
- **handheld, two hands:** suitable when centre and opposite-edge controls matter;
- **propped touch:** suitable when the stand resists normal taps;
- **docked with keyboard:** only after visible focus and activation are verified.

The [complete tablet workflow](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-tablet-viewing-workflows/) adds power, profile, network, and interruption checks.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid stretching across an unsupported tablet, testing from a different posture, placing a stand on soft or uneven material, allowing a cable to destabilise the device, and assuming one-handed operation is inherently better.

This workflow cannot assess injury, medical needs, or accessory certification. Follow manufacturer instructions and use accessibility features appropriate to the user. If controls are visually clipped after rotation, document the layout rather than compensating with risky reach.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should every control be reachable with one thumb?

No. Essential actions must be reliably accessible, but two-handed or propped use may be more stable and appropriate.

### Can I move the app's controls closer to an edge?

Only if the current interface explicitly provides that option. Do not assume controls can be customised.

### What if subtitles cover a control?

Pause, capture the screen if authorised, and check orientation or documented display settings. Report persistent overlap rather than tapping through text.
