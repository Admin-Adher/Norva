# A Secure Source-Connection Handoff Between Household Admins

> **In short:** Confirm the source owner authorizes the new administrator and define scope. Map the provider account, Norva account, recovery channels, authorized profiles, devices, protected credential location, catalog baseline, incidents, and review dates without writing secrets. Use provider-supported delegation where available or rotate credentials through the owner rather than sharing an old password. Verify sign-in, recovery, source connection, catalog sample, and device access, then remove the former administrator and audit temporary copies.

Source administration crosses at least two systems: the external source and Norva. A handoff in one does not automatically transfer ownership or access in the other.

## Confirm authority and scope

Use the [source-authorization guide](/blog/confirm-source-authorization-before-connection/) to record owner, approver, permitted users, purpose, review date, and revocation path. Do not assume a household administrator can transfer a source they do not own.

If authority is disputed, stop and use qualified or provider support.

## Inventory responsibilities

List source-provider administration, Norva source settings, account recovery, subscription or access status where relevant, privacy-safe label, endpoint documentation, supported devices, progress baseline, local media, incident records, and support cases.

Exclude credentials, tokens, and full private addresses from the register.

## Use supported delegation

If the source provider offers roles, family access, or delegated administration, use that path. Give the minimum necessary role and verify the new administrator's independent recovery.

Do not share the outgoing administrator's password-manager account.

## Rotate when sharing cannot be undone

If an old administrator knew the reusable credential and should no longer have access, the owner should rotate it and revoke sessions. The [exposure-response guide](/blog/rotate-source-credentials-after-exposure/) provides the sequence without implying malicious behavior.

Store the replacement using the [secure details guide](/blog/collect-source-details-securely/).

## Transfer non-secret operational evidence

Provide provider and support URLs, masked source label, documented address structure, baseline summaries, known outages, maintenance dates, and case references. Redact personal titles, account emails, endpoint hosts, and transaction data.

The new administrator should be able to operate without asking for old chat histories containing secrets.

## Verify access and recovery

Test source-provider sign-in, recovery, Norva connection, categories, small playback sample, audio and subtitles where relevant, progress identity, and supported-device behavior. Confirm the new administrator can complete recovery without the former person's inbox or phone.

Do not bypass stronger-authentication controls during testing.

## Remove former access

Revoke source sessions, remove provider roles, update recovery, review shared password-manager access, sign out personal devices, delete local media where appropriate, and remove temporary files. Do not remove household profiles that still belong to authorized users.

## Schedule follow-up

Use the [maintenance audit](/blog/source-connection-maintenance-audit/) after the handoff to confirm notices, sessions, credentials, endpoint identity, categories, and devices remain correct.

Keep control of Norva separate from control of the external source. The two accounts can have different owners, recovery paths, device lists, and support channels. A person who manages a household profile may not be authorized to change source credentials, billing, or provider recovery. Likewise, removing a Norva role does not prove that an external provider session ended. Verify each system through its current official controls, and ask the relevant owner to perform actions outside the new administrator's authority. This boundary prevents a convenient handoff record from becoming an accidental grant of broader access.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating password sharing as a handoff.
- Assuming the Norva administrator owns the source.
- Copying credentials into the register.
- Forgetting recovery channels and sessions.
- Removing authorized household profiles.
- Keeping former password-manager access.
- Skipping a post-handoff audit.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can the outgoing administrator send the password?

Use supported delegation or owner-controlled rotation instead of transferring an old reusable secret through chat or notes.

### Does a Norva email change transfer the source account?

No such assumption should be made. The source provider maintains separate ownership, recovery, and role controls.

### When is the handoff complete?

After authorization, independent recovery, source and Norva access, devices, evidence, former-access removal, and follow-up ownership are verified.
