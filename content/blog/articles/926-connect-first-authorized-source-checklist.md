# A Checklist for Connecting Your First Authorized Source

> **In short:** Norva is a media player and organizer, not a media catalog. Before connecting anything, confirm that you own the source or are legally authorized to use it. Protect connection details, start with a small and recognizable sample, then check organization and playback before accepting the setup. Record outcomes, but never copy credentials into screenshots or notes.

Connecting a first source is an administrative decision, not merely a form-filling task. A careful sequence protects private details and makes later troubleshooting easier. Complete the [Norva onboarding journey](/blog/norva-onboarding-complete-journey/) first so the account, profile, device, and source roles are understood.

## Confirm authority before configuration

Write down who controls the source and why the account may use it. The record should identify an administrator and an authorization basis, but it should not contain a password, access token, private endpoint, or full account identifier.

If authority is uncertain, stop. A successful technical connection does not establish permission. Norva subscriptions cover the interface, organization, playback, and supported synchronization features; they do not include a media catalog or rights to third-party content.

## Prepare the account and screen

Use a trusted screen where private connection details cannot be observed by guests or captured by an unnecessary recording. Confirm that the intended Norva account and profile are active. Follow the [new-account security guide](/blog/secure-new-norva-account/) before entering any secret.

Keep the official support route available on a separate trusted device. Avoid pasting credentials into chats, browser notes, or support messages. If evidence is later needed, redact source names, addresses, usernames, and tokens.

## Assess source readiness

Before connection, verify that the source is currently reachable through its own authorized workflow and that its administrator expects the connection. Check whether the device, network, and source conditions needed for playback are available.

Use the [source-readiness evaluation](/blog/evaluate-source-readiness-for-norva/) to distinguish a Norva configuration problem from an unavailable or incorrectly administered source. Do not change several systems at once; that makes the cause of a failure harder to identify.

## Connect with the minimum necessary data

Enter only the fields required by the current official interface. Read labels and validation messages rather than assuming that a familiar field has the same meaning. Do not reuse a password from another account, and do not expose secrets in profile or device names.

If the interface reports an error, capture the stage and sanitized wording. Repeatedly submitting different details without a hypothesis can trigger source-side limits and destroys useful diagnostic evidence.

## Begin with a recognizable sample

When the source and interface allow it, validate a small, representative selection first. Choose items you can recognize by title and structure. Include one straightforward movie or episode and, if relevant, one item with expected audio or subtitle options.

Observe whether names, artwork, series structure, and variants are organized plausibly. The source remains responsible for what media and language tracks are available; Norva can only present information it receives and supports.

## Validate playback separately

Catalog visibility and playback are different checkpoints. Open one authorized item, begin a short session, confirm that controls respond, and exit normally. Where supported, note whether progress is retained without claiming that every source, device, or media format behaves identically.

Do not attempt to repair a playback failure by deleting the entire connection immediately. First record the screen, item type, time, network context, and sanitized error.

## Original evidence: first-source connection record

| Stage | Evidence to record | Result | Secret-data check |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Authorization | Administrator and authorization basis | Pass / Fail | No credentials |
| Readiness | Source reachable through authorized workflow | Pass / Fail | Private address redacted |
| Connection | Interface stage and sanitized outcome | Pass / Fail | Token excluded |
| Catalog | Two recognizable items and structure | Pass / Fail | Private titles minimized |
| Playback | Screen, item type, time, outcome | Pass / Fail | Screenshot reviewed |

Accept the connection only when the required rows pass. Mark unsupported checks as not applicable instead of inventing a result.

## Common first-connection mistakes

- Treating technical access as proof of authorization.
- Sharing an unredacted connection screen with support.
- Testing a large, unfamiliar catalog before a small sample.
- Changing source, network, and Norva settings simultaneously.
- Assuming every language track or format must be available.
- Recording passwords or tokens in the evidence log.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does a Norva subscription include a media catalog?

No. The subscription covers Norva's software experience and supported features. Users connect a compatible source they own or are legally authorized to use.

### Should I send my source password to support?

No. Keep passwords, tokens, private addresses, and complete account identifiers out of support evidence. Share only the minimum sanitized context requested through an official route.

### What should I do if catalog visibility works but playback does not?

Record that split result. Test one representative item, preserve the sanitized message, and check the source, device, network, and format conditions separately before changing the connection.
