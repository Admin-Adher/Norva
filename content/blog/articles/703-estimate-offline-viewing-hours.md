# How to Estimate Offline Viewing Hours Before You Leave

> **In short:** Estimate offline viewing hours by adding only dependable viewing windows, then subtracting commitments, transitions, breaks, and shared-device use. Compare that result with observed battery runtime and available storage. Use the lowest practical constraint as your planning ceiling, add a small alternative for uncertainty, and never treat total travel time as guaranteed viewing time.

An accurate estimate reduces unnecessary transfers and makes verification manageable. It also prevents a common planning error: preparing media for every hour away when only a fraction of those hours can be used comfortably.

## Separate trip duration from viewing time

Build a timeline of possible sessions. A journey might include waiting, moving, security checks, conversation, meals, sleep, work, and arrival tasks. Only mark a period as dependable when the device can be used safely and appropriately, you expect to be free, and audio or captions will make viewing practical.

Use three labels:

- **dependable:** likely available with few conflicts;
- **possible:** available only if plans or conditions allow;
- **unavailable:** committed to another activity.

Add dependable windows first. Treat possible windows as a reason for one small fallback, not as hours that must be filled.

## Match windows to complete units

Avoid splitting every session into precise minutes. Instead, match a window with a unit that can be completed or paused naturally: one short episode, part of a film, or a planned continuation.

If an episode is longer than the dependable window, it may still work if stopping mid-episode is acceptable. Record that decision explicitly. The guide to [choosing episodes for a short journey](/blog/choose-episodes-for-short-journey/) focuses on order and natural stopping points.

## Apply the device constraints

Viewing time cannot exceed what the prepared device can support. Compare the timeline with:

- measured battery use from representative local playback;
- current battery condition and starting charge;
- access to permitted charging;
- free local storage after preserving working headroom;
- whether another person needs the device;
- required audio, subtitle, or accessibility options.

Do not use a generic battery-hours promise. Apple and Android both describe power-saving settings, but actual runtime depends on the device and use. Create an observation with the [offline battery budget method](/blog/offline-viewing-battery-budget/).

## Calculate a conservative ceiling

Use this decision rule:

`planning ceiling = the lowest of dependable session time, observed battery allowance, and storage-supported viewing`

The expression is a planning tool, not a universal measurement. If your schedule supports four hours but your conservative battery observation supports less, plan for the lower amount or arrange verified charging. If storage is the constraint, use [limited-storage prioritisation](/blog/prioritize-downloads-limited-storage/) rather than assuming every item has a similar size.

## Convert hours into a small batch

Choose items whose combined durations fit below the ceiling. Leave room for pauses and changing plans. Include variety through one alternative rather than duplicating the entire schedule.

For a weekend, the [offline title selection scorecard](/blog/choose-titles-for-weekend-offline/) helps decide which candidates earn space. For a broader strategy, the [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) connects time with eligibility, storage, tracks, and cleanup.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Counting every travel hour as available.
- Ignoring transitions and setup time.
- Assuming a power-saving mode guarantees a fixed runtime.
- Converting storage into hours with an unverified universal rate.
- Forgetting that another person may need the device.
- Choosing an item that cannot fit a natural session.
- Preparing an oversized fallback library.

The estimate remains a plan, not a promise. Delays can create more time, while fatigue, motion, rules, or changed plans can remove it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should uncertain windows count at all?

Do not add them to the dependable total. Use them only to justify a modest alternative that already fits your storage and battery plan.

### Is item runtime enough to estimate storage?

No. File size can vary with quality, encoding, tracks, and source packaging. Measure completed items on the intended device.

### What if the battery estimate is lower than the schedule?

Reduce the batch or arrange a permitted, tested charging option. Avoid assuming a new setting will close the gap without a representative playback check.
