# Verify Offline Availability Before Leaving Connectivity

> **In short:** Verify the exact title, episode, and version—not just its catalogue card. Confirm the current offline action is permitted, wait for completion, disable Wi-Fi and mobile data, then reopen the item from the offline area and sample at least two positions. Test essential audio and subtitles. If any state is uncertain, do not rely on that item.

Offline verification is an item-level proof exercise. It answers one narrow question: can this exact prepared item be opened and used on this device, under this account and profile, without a connection now?

## Match the identity before testing

Record the title, season and episode where relevant, version, intended profile, and any quality choice. Grouped variants or similar episode artwork can make it easy to inspect one item and prepare another.

Confirm that an offline action exists for the exact item. Norva offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. Visibility in the connected catalogue, a favourite marker, or previous streaming does not establish eligibility.

## Require a completed local state

Start preparation while connected and keep the device available until the app reports completion. A queued label, spinner, or partial percentage is not evidence of offline readiness.

Check the device storage view if the transfer behaves unexpectedly. Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device, so local space is required. Preserve working room for the operating system and normal app activity.

## Remove the network from the test

Enable airplane mode or disable both Wi-Fi and mobile data. Some operating systems let Wi-Fi or Bluetooth be re-enabled after airplane mode, so inspect the actual controls rather than the icon alone.

Then:

1. close the item and return to the app;
2. open the offline or local area;
3. select the recorded version;
4. play near the beginning;
5. move to or resume at a later point;
6. observe video and audio for a representative moment;
7. stop, reopen, and confirm the item still appears.

This is stronger evidence than displaying a poster, but it does not guarantee every frame or future availability.

## Test the required tracks

Open the audio and subtitle menus while still disconnected. Select what the viewer actually needs and observe it in use. The [offline audio checklist](/blog/prepare-audio-options-before-offline/) and [offline subtitle checklist](/blog/prepare-subtitles-before-offline/) provide track-specific records.

If the track is missing, mark the item unsuitable for that session even when the video itself plays.

## Record a pass without overstating it

Use “Pass” only for the tested device, app state, account, profile, item, version, tracks, and date. Use “Recheck” for anything ambiguous. Never convert Recheck into Pass because departure is close.

Add the result to the broader [night-before readiness review](/blog/night-before-offline-readiness-check/), which also covers battery, accessories, free storage, and cleanup. The [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) connects verification to the full strategy.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Testing a catalogue card instead of the exact local version.
- Leaving Wi-Fi enabled after turning on airplane mode.
- Sampling only the first frame.
- Ignoring an incomplete transfer indicator.
- Assuming one episode proves a whole season.
- Treating saved language preferences as proof of local tracks.
- Assuming a past test remains valid after material changes.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does a completed label prove offline playback?

It is necessary evidence, but the decisive check is reopening and sampling the item with network connectivity disabled.

### Must I watch the whole item?

No. Sample at least two representative positions and essential tracks. A short test reduces risk but cannot guarantee every moment.

### When should I repeat verification?

Repeat after a meaningful app, device, source, account, version, or rights change, and before any disconnected period where the item is essential.
