# How to Evaluate Source Readiness Before Trying Norva

> **In short:** Confirm that you own or are legally authorized to use the source, verify current Norva compatibility through official information, and identify a responsible source administrator. Check reachability through the source's own approved method, prepare connection details securely, and document a small known catalog sample with metadata and tracks. Pause the trial if authorization, compatibility, administration, or source stability remains uncertain.

Norva is a software media player and organizer; its subscription does not include media access or a catalog. The connected source therefore determines whether a meaningful product evaluation can begin. A polished trial cannot repair unclear rights, unavailable media, or an unstable source.

## Make authorization the first gate

Write who owns the source, who grants household use, and which terms govern access. Keep evidence minimal and secure. A technical address or working credential does not establish legal permission.

Use the [source authorization checklist](/blog/confirm-source-authorization-before-connection/) when the household role or rights are unclear. Stop if permission cannot be confirmed.

## Confirm current compatibility

Review current Norva product and support information for the source type without assuming that every format or provider works. Do not rely on an old screenshot, forum post, or another user's setup. Record the official page, review date, and the exact non-sensitive source category.

The broader [source connection planning guide](/blog/authorized-source-connection-planning-guide/) helps turn that result into a controlled setup.

## Assign a source administrator

Identify the person authorized to manage connection details, credentials, renewals, source-side outages, and household handoffs. Record a backup contact role without copying secrets. The Norva profile owner and source administrator may be different people, so define who handles which boundary.

If nobody can safely rotate a compromised credential or verify source status, the setup is not ready.

## Inventory connection fields privately

List the types of information required by the current official flow, but do not place actual passwords, tokens, private addresses, or complete identifiers in the readiness sheet. Store secrets in an appropriate password manager or source-approved secure method.

Never paste live credentials into screenshots, ordinary notes, or support drafts.

## Check source reachability independently

Use the source's approved administration or access method to confirm that it is reachable before involving Norva. Record time, broad network context, and result. Avoid publishing private network details.

The [pre-connection reachability guide](/blog/check-source-reachability-before-adding/) separates source availability from player configuration. A single successful check is evidence for that moment, not a universal uptime guarantee.

## Prepare a known catalog baseline

Choose three to five neutral items with known identities. Include a movie, a series hierarchy if relevant, an item with known artwork or metadata, and an item with known audio or subtitle tracks. Record only the attributes needed for later comparison.

A count alone is weak evidence. Identity, hierarchy, metadata, and track presence reveal different readiness layers.

## Review source-side quality

Inspect whether the source itself supplies stable titles, categories, season and episode structure, artwork references, duration, and required tracks. Norva can organize information from a connected source, but it cannot be assumed to invent missing source data or rights.

Label known source limitations before the trial so they are not scored unfairly as Norva failures.

## Decide pass, pause, or stop

Use “pass” when authorization, current compatibility, administration, reachability, safe credentials, and a known sample are all ready. Use “pause” for a resolvable uncertainty with an owner. Use “stop” when rights are absent, support is contradicted, or secrets cannot be handled safely.

Do not start a time-limited evaluation while an essential gate remains unresolved.

## Verify one sample after connection

If the readiness gates pass, connect one source through the current official flow and inspect the prepared sample. The [small-sample verification guide](/blog/verify-sample-after-source-connection/) records identity, categories, artwork, tracks, and playback without adding a second source too early.

Keep source-side and Norva-side observations in separate columns.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating a working credential as proof of rights.
- Assuming compatibility from another source or old page.
- Starting a trial during a known source outage.
- Recording secrets in the readiness worksheet.
- Using only a total catalog count as evidence.
- Blaming Norva for source fields already known to be absent.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does Norva provide a source or catalog?

No. You connect a compatible media source that you own or are legally authorized to use.

### Can a successful connection prove that my source is authorized?

No. Technical success and legal authorization are separate questions.

### How large should the readiness sample be?

Three to five varied, non-sensitive items are usually enough to expose identity, hierarchy, metadata, artwork, and track differences without exporting the catalog.
