# Record Audio and Subtitle Choices Before Redownloading

> **In short:** Before removing an offline item, record its exact visible version, profile, audio language or descriptive track, subtitle language and state, accessibility need, and tested playback positions. Treat the replacement as a new preparation: current eligibility and track availability can differ. Select the tracks deliberately, disable connectivity, and compare the same sample positions before declaring the replacement ready.

Redownloading can solve a separately diagnosed local-item problem, but it can also erase the evidence needed to reproduce a working setup. The title alone is not enough when multiple versions or tracks exist.

## Record the viewing context

Start with the intended viewer and Norva profile. Note the device, app version, compatible media source, exact item, visible version or edition, and why the existing copy is being replaced.

Then record:

- active audio language and label;
- descriptive or alternate audio requirement;
- subtitle language, enabled state, and visible label;
- caption readability settings that matter to the viewer;
- audio output device and relevant system accessibility setting;
- two or three playback positions that passed offline.

The guides to [preparing audio options](/blog/prepare-audio-options-before-offline/) and [preparing subtitles](/blog/prepare-subtitles-before-offline/) explain why a connected preview does not prove that the same track is present in the local copy.

## Confirm that replacement is necessary

Do not remove a working item merely because the storage total looks unexpected. First confirm the symptom, current eligibility, available space, and whether the problem affects the item, track, output route, or app more broadly.

If replacement remains appropriate, protect enough free space for the operation. Avoid clearing all app data or reinstalling; those broader actions affect more than one item and weaken the diagnostic record.

## Remove and prepare through supported controls

Use the app's item-level removal control and read the confirmation. Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and stored on the device, so do not manipulate its internal files.

Return to the exact compatible source and item. Reconfirm eligibility, version, audio, and subtitle choices before preparation. Associated rights and available tracks can change, so the previous receipt is a comparison record, not a guarantee that every option remains offered.

## Verify the replacement offline

After preparation completes:

1. confirm the intended account and profile;
2. open the track selectors and compare visible labels;
3. disable connectivity;
4. sample the recorded playback positions;
5. listen for the intended audio characteristics;
6. inspect subtitle timing and readability;
7. record pass, partial match, or recheck.

If a required track is unavailable, do not substitute silently. Use the [no-download fallback plan](/blog/create-no-download-fallback-plan/) or choose another eligible version if one is legitimately available.

## Preserve accessibility intent, not just labels

Language labels alone may not capture why the viewer selected a track. "English" could mean dialogue audio, descriptive audio, closed captions, or translated subtitles depending on the interface and media. Record the intended outcome in plain language, such as "dialogue plus non-speech cues readable without audio."

This makes the receipt useful even if interface wording changes after an app update.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Recording only the title.
- Assuming the default audio is the previous audio.
- Confusing subtitles with descriptive audio.
- Removing the old copy before confirming replacement eligibility.
- Testing while the device is still connected.
- Treating a matching label as proof of matching behavior.
- Ignoring the viewer's accessibility outcome.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will the replacement automatically keep my track choices?

Do not assume so. Inspect the exact version and selectors again, then verify the result without connectivity.

### Should I save a copy of the encrypted media file?

No. Use a descriptive receipt and supported app controls. App-managed encrypted media should not be moved or edited manually.

### What if the former track is no longer available?

Record the difference, check legitimate eligible alternatives, and revise the plan. Do not label a required accessibility setup ready until the viewer verifies it.
