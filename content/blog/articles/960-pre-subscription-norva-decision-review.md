# Run a Final Norva Decision Review Before Subscribing

> **In short:** Freeze the trial evidence and confirm source authorization, essential screen and access tests, profile needs, privacy acceptance, onboarding effort, current pricing, renewal and cancellation terms, and the official support path. Separate verified results from assumptions. Choose continue only when every non-negotiable passes; choose pause for owned, time-bounded uncertainty; choose decline for failed essentials or unacceptable terms. Record the decision and billing owner.

The final review is not another product tour. It is a controlled decision using evidence already gathered. Its purpose is to catch an expired assumption, hidden source dependency, unsuitable plan, or unresolved essential requirement before a commercial commitment continues.

## Freeze the evidence set

Stop changing filters, devices, sources, and profiles while reviewing. Save the final [trial scorecard](/blog/norva-trial-evaluation-scorecard/) with test dates, supported screens, source context, evidence grades, and unresolved owners. Remove credentials and unnecessary viewing details.

Do not award new points from memory after the trial.

## Reconfirm source authorization

Verify that the connected source remains one you own or are legally authorized to use, that household scope is understood, and that a source administrator can maintain access. Technical success still does not prove rights.

The [source-readiness decision guide](/blog/evaluate-source-readiness-for-norva/) defines pass, pause, and stop gates.

## Check every essential workflow

Read each non-negotiable requirement and its direct evidence. Common essentials include primary-screen navigation, known-media playback, required text or audio tracks, cross-screen continuity, and privacy controls. Confirm that the test used the actual supported context rather than a convenient substitute.

A failed essential remains a stop condition even when the aggregate score looks strong.

## Match profile needs to current plans

Count stable recurring viewing contexts. Current official information describes up to two profiles for Norva and up to five for Norva Family. These are profile capacities, not device or simultaneous-playback limits.

Consult the [profile-based plan guide](/blog/choose-plan-by-profile-needs/) and current pricing page rather than buying capacity for temporary or undefined contexts.

## Accept or reject the privacy trade-off

Review the completed [privacy disclosure map](/blog/evaluate-norva-privacy-disclosures/). Confirm that planned data uses, provider roles, transfer wording, account controls, deletion route, and separate source and device layers are acceptable. A material unanswered privacy question should not be converted into a minor risk without reason.

This decision method is not legal advice.

## Include onboarding and maintenance effort

Compare the observed first tasks with the [onboarding effort estimate](/blog/estimate-norva-onboarding-effort/). Add ongoing source administration, profile hygiene, device updates, storage review, and support evidence. Do not count only the displayed subscription price.

Assign a household owner for account, billing, source, and periodic review.

## Verify current commercial terms

Open the official pricing page and terms at the time of decision. Record chosen plan, billing period, current price and tax context, trial or renewal status, payment provider, cancellation route, reminder date, and any relevant refund wording. Conditions may depend on the provider used to subscribe.

Do not copy payment card data into the register.

## Review support and recovery

Confirm the official support destination and the evidence you could provide safely. Record how the household would handle account lockout, source outage, device loss, and administrator handoff. Do not assume a response time or remedy that official information does not promise.

An acceptable product fit includes a recovery workflow the household can actually administer.

## Classify unresolved risks

For each unknown, record impact, probability only if evidence supports it, owner, next action, and deadline. “Pause” fits a material question with a realistic near-term answer. “Decline” fits failed rights, unsupported essentials, unacceptable policy or terms, or risk beyond the household's tolerance.

Avoid an indefinite pause that silently becomes payment.

## Record one explicit outcome

Write continue, pause, or decline; list the decisive reasons; name the billing owner; and set the next review trigger. If continuing, retain only necessary evidence. If declining, follow the current provider's cancellation route and keep legitimate proof.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Adding untested points from memory.
- Letting a total score hide an essential failure.
- Confusing profile capacity with device limits.
- Using an old price or cancellation instruction.
- Ignoring source and maintenance ownership.
- Allowing an unresolved trial to renew by accident.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does a high trial score guarantee the right decision?

No. Source rights, failed essentials, privacy concerns, current terms, or unowned maintenance can outweigh the total.

### Should a minor unresolved issue block subscription?

Only if its impact or uncertainty exceeds your threshold. Give it an owner and deadline instead of pretending it is resolved.

### Is declining permanent?

Not necessarily. The decision reflects current evidence, product information, source readiness, and household needs; a later material change can justify a new evaluation.
