# How Offline Availability Can Affect Audio Track Choices

> **In short:** An offline choice is limited to the eligible item and media version stored on that supported device and the audio alternatives available in that local context. Before leaving connectivity, verify current eligibility, completion state, exact version, full offline audio list, intended track, and playback with the network unavailable. Do not assume the connected and local lists are identical.

Offline is a separate playback context, not merely the connected screen without a network icon. Plan terms, supported devices, source rights, item eligibility, storage, and current controls all matter.

## Verify current eligibility first

Use Norva's current official features, pricing, terms, and device information. Do not copy an old plan limit or assume every source and title supports offline use.

The user must own or be authorised to use the connected source and media. Offline availability does not change content rights.

## Identify the exact local item

Record source, title, season/episode, media version, account or anonymised profile, device, and app version. Confirm that preparation is complete through the current interface before disabling connectivity.

Do not assume that a grouped alternate version or neighboring episode was stored with it.

## Compare the audio selectors

While connected, capture the full audio list for the selected version. In the eligible local context, capture the list again before changing a track.

Compare:

- exact language and role labels;
- custom titles;
- channels or codecs when shown;
- target entry presence;
- selected marker;
- verified playback role.

Use [the version comparison workflow](/blog/how-version-changes-can-affect-available-audio-tracks/) if the identities differ.

## Original evidence: readiness card

| Field | Connected context | Offline context |
|---|---|---|
| Item/version | Exact identity | Exact identity |
| Full audio list | Exact labels | Exact labels |
| Target available | Yes/no/unclear | Yes/no/unclear |
| Selected entry | Exact label | Exact label |
| Heard role | Verified | Verified |
| Completion/playback | Connected result | No-network result |

Keep “not tested” separate from “not available.”

## Run a no-network sample before departure

After the interface confirms readiness, disable connectivity through the device's ordinary control and open the local item. Sample a dialogue-rich scene with the intended track. Confirm the item opens, the audio entry exists, and the heard language or role is correct.

Restore connectivity afterward if the device is still being prepared. Do not erase the local copy simply because one test fails; preserve evidence first.

## Plan a fallback

If a needed track is absent locally, choose another verified eligible version or item only through authorised controls. For an accessibility requirement, do not rely on a label that was not sampled.

Use [the missing-track diagnostic](/blog/how-to-investigate-a-missing-audio-track/) to separate version, metadata, device, and local-context differences. The [audio management checklist](/blog/an-audio-track-management-checklist/) provides a final pre-departure review.

## Protect privacy and local data

Norva's published privacy information should be the source of truth for current local-data handling. Follow device security and custody practices, especially on shared or borrowed equipment.

Before device sale, return, repair, or handoff, follow the manufacturer and account-owner process for local data and sign-out. Do not treat offline encryption or local storage as a substitute for device access control.

## Report an offline-only difference

Include the paired readiness card, plan/device eligibility source, exact item/version, preparation state, no-network steps, expected result, observed result, and privacy-safe screenshots. Do not attach media, credentials, source addresses, or private history.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid preparing the wrong version, checking only while connected, assuming every online track is stored, deleting evidence immediately, and promising offline availability for all media.

The actual eligible alternatives depend on current Norva terms, device support, the connected source, and the selected media.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will every connected audio track be available offline?

Do not assume so. Verify the local selector and sample the intended track before relying on it.

### Can I change versions after going offline?

Only versions already eligible and available in the current local context can be used; verify before departure.

### Should I delete and recreate the local item when a track is missing?

Preserve the paired evidence first and follow current official guidance before taking a disruptive action.
