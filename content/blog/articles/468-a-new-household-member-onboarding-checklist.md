# A New Household Member Onboarding Checklist

> **In short:** Explain the difference between device, Norva account, profile, connected source, and viewing session; assign only the profile and devices the member needs; keep credentials private; and demonstrate the pre-play, shared-screen exit, troubleshooting, and privacy routines. Finish with teach-back: the new member should explain what they select, what they may change, and who handles account or source issues.

Onboarding should create confidence without giving broad access by default. A person can use an approved viewing context without managing the subscription or knowing source credentials.

## Prepare before the conversation

- [ ] Confirm the member will be a recurring household user.
- [ ] Review current plan profile capacity.
- [ ] Choose an existing profile or approve a new one.
- [ ] Identify trusted supported devices in scope.
- [ ] Decide what private history will be visible.
- [ ] Confirm who handles account and source changes.
- [ ] Remove credentials from shared notes and screens.

Use [the clutter-free profile introduction](/blog/how-to-introduce-a-new-household-profile-without-clutter/) when a new context is justified.

## Explain the five layers

- **Device:** the supported screen and its lock or custody.
- **Account:** subscription and authentication.
- **Profile:** progress, history, favourites, and preferences.
- **Source:** compatible authorised media access.
- **Session:** item, version, position, audio, and subtitles.

A profile is not a password, parental control, or source entitlement.

## Demonstrate profile entry

- [ ] Read the profile text label.
- [ ] Confirm the item or episode.
- [ ] Check proposed progress and version.
- [ ] Verify audio and subtitles.
- [ ] Target play or resume once.

The [profile-boundary explanation](/blog/how-to-explain-profile-boundaries-to-household-members/) provides teach-back prompts.

## Define permitted changes

Agree whether the member may:

- change their own favourites;
- change persistent language preferences;
- use shared profiles;
- prepare eligible local media;
- connect devices;
- request a source change;
- enter account settings.

Do not imply product-enforced roles. These are household rules.

## Protect credentials

The new member should know the request path for access problems, not the password or source secret. Account coordinators and source stewards use official recovery and support routes.

## Teach shared-screen privacy

Show what profile names, history, favourites, and notifications may appear. Explain when sign-out is required and which neutral screen should be left for the next person.

Norva's privacy policy covers product data practices; household consent governs interpersonal review.

## Teach troubleshooting restraint

Preserve the first error, change one condition, and ask the coordinator before reset, reinstall, source removal, or profile deletion. Never post account or source secrets in a support request.

## Run a harmless first session

Choose an authorised, non-sensitive item. Let the new member verify the profile, start, pause, and leave the device in the agreed state. Correct unclear instructions rather than judging speed.

The [shared governance guide](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) contains the household charter.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid giving every credential, creating a profile before purpose is clear, overwhelming the member with every setting, and treating onboarding as consent to surveillance.

This checklist does not grant legal rights, guarantee privacy, or replace current plan and device documentation.

## Verify the onboarding without collecting history

Within the first week, ask the new member to demonstrate four harmless tasks: identify their profile, explain who may connect a source, leave the shared screen in the agreed privacy state, and describe what to do before a troubleshooting change. Record only whether each boundary was understood.

Correct one misunderstanding at a time and repeat the task. Do not inspect private viewing activity as proof of adoption. If the person needs accessibility support, test the required captions, descriptive audio, contrast, focus, or input method during the walkthrough and name who can restore it after an update.

Set a review date and delete the onboarding record once the household no longer needs it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does every new member need a profile?

Only when a recurring viewing context needs separate state and current capacity supports it.

### Should they know the account password?

Not automatically. Provide the approved use path and an account-help contact.

### Should onboarding include private history?

Only the minimum exposure needed and with consent. Structural rules do not require a full history review.
