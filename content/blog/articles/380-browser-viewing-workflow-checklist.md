# A Browser Viewing Workflow Checklist

> **In short:** Verify official browser support, correct account and source, one playback tab, working controls, intended audio output, readable subtitles, suitable display mode, sufficient power, a known recovery path, and shared-device privacy. End with explicit sign-out when required, closed related windows, reviewed downloads, and targeted—not blanket—troubleshooting.

Use this checklist for a real session, not as a claim that every browser behaves the same. Mark each item pass, fail, or not required and stop when a failure affects authorization, privacy, required accessibility, or basic control.

## Supported environment

- The service's current official guidance supports the browser and operating system.
- Browser updates come from the vendor's official channel.
- The browser restarted after an update when required.
- Organization-managed policies are understood rather than bypassed.
- One authorized sample has passed the essential controls.

Use [the browser compatibility preflight](/blog/check-browser-compatibility-first/) for a new device or changed environment.

## Account and source

- The intended browser profile and service account are active.
- The connected source is authorized and correctly selected.
- The exact movie, series, season, episode, or variant is confirmed.
- Similar titles are distinguished with available metadata.
- No credentials or private source details appear in screenshots or reports.

## Playback controls

- Play and pause respond once.
- A short seek behaves predictably.
- Visible loading, empty, and error states are distinct.
- Duplicate playback tabs are closed or paused.
- The page remains controllable after refresh and mode changes.

The HTML media standard defines core browser media concepts, while the service controls its actual workflow.

## Audio

- The intended content track is selected when available.
- Player and browser tab are not unexpectedly muted.
- The operating-system output is explicitly verified.
- Physical speakers or headphones are connected and at moderate volume.
- Output is rechecked after monitor, dock, or wireless changes.

## Subtitles and captions

- The correct available track and language are selected.
- Timing and completeness have been sampled.
- Text remains readable over bright, dark, and detailed scenes.
- Line breaks and placement stay inside the player.
- Browser controls do not cover the text.
- Windowed, fullscreen, and external-display modes have been checked.

## Display and attention

- Windowed or fullscreen mode fits the session context.
- Fullscreen entry, controls, and exit have been tested.
- External display support, cable, layout, and scaling are verified.
- Screen sharing and notifications do not reveal private content.
- Viewing distance keeps interface text and subtitles readable.

## Tabs and workload

- One tab clearly owns playback.
- Only essential companion tabs remain open.
- User-started heavy downloads or tasks are paused when necessary.
- Security, accessibility, and system services remain enabled.
- Low-power browser settings have been tested rather than assumed.

## Power and physical setup

- Battery charge, condition, and session margin are reasonable.
- The approved charger is available when needed.
- Ventilation is clear and cables do not create a trip hazard.
- Temporary sleep or brightness settings remain accessible and comfortable.
- A safe stop threshold leaves time to save context and close.

## Recovery

- Title, approximate position, selected tracks, and visible error are recorded before refresh.
- One normal refresh is attempted before destructive resets.
- Account, source, item, audio, subtitles, and display are verified afterward.
- Persistent failures are isolated by item, extension, profile, or site data one variable at a time.
- Official support receives a privacy-safe before-and-after record.

Use the full [browser viewing workflow guide](/blog/browser-viewing-workflow-guide/) to choose the next diagnostic branch.

## Shared-device privacy

- The device is trusted enough for temporary sign-in.
- A vendor-documented guest or private context is used when appropriate.
- Password and profile-save prompts are declined.
- Downloads and screenshots are understood to persist separately.
- Private mode is not described as anonymous.

## Clean closure

- Playback stops before the window closes.
- Fullscreen exits and all related tabs become visible.
- Service sign-out is completed when required.
- Every related private or guest window closes.
- Downloads and temporary artifacts are reviewed with permission.
- External output and screen sharing stop.
- A fresh context no longer reveals a shared account.

Follow [the clean sign-out routine](/blog/end-browser-session-cleanly/) for borrowed or public devices.

## Build a scorecard

Record environment, sample, each check, evidence, failure impact, owner, and retest result. The checklist should preserve facts, not invent a cause. A passing session on one setup is evidence for that setup, not a universal compatibility guarantee.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating page load as proof of complete support.
- Testing sound and subtitles after a long session begins.
- Assuming fullscreen creates privacy.
- Keeping several playback owners.
- Clearing all browser data as an early fix.
- Closing a shared tab without service sign-out.
- Reporting failures without exact context.

## Frequently asked questions

### Must every check run every time?

Routine sessions can use a shorter subset. Run the full checklist after device, browser, display, account, source, or accessibility changes.

### Which failures should stop the session?

Stop for unsupported environments, uncertain authorization, exposed shared accounts, unusable controls, wrong audio routing, or required subtitles that cannot be read.

### Does a passing checklist guarantee future playback?

No. It documents one environment and moment. Repeat relevant checks after updates or setup changes.
