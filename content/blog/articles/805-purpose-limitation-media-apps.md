# What Purpose Limitation Means for a Media App

> **In short:** Purpose limitation means personal data should be collected for explained purposes rather than an undefined future use, and later use should remain compatible with the original purpose or receive a new basis and explanation under applicable rules. For a media app, map every category—account, source, usage, device, entitlement, technical, and download—to its stated feature or need. Record vague or newly expanded purposes as questions, not proof of misconduct.

This is a data-literacy explanation, not legal advice. Whether a later use is compatible and which notice or basis is required depends on facts and the law that applies.

## Read purpose as a concrete answer to “why”

“Improve services” is broader than “diagnose crashes in this application version.” “Provide the service” is clearer when a notice breaks it into account creation, source connection, progress synchronization, entitlement verification, and reliability.

Use the [ten-question policy guide](/blog/read-privacy-policy-ten-questions/) to record the exact section and date rather than paraphrasing the purpose into something more precise than the notice.

## Build one row per category

Norva's current privacy policy links examples to reasons: account data for account creation and sign-in; source settings for a user-requested connection; usage and preferences for continuity and personalization; device records for pairing; entitlement status for access verification; technical data for delivery, security, and reliability; and eligible local downloads for offline viewing.

Those are policy statements to verify at the current date. They should not be generalized to fields or features the notice does not mention.

## Separate purpose from data amount

Purpose limitation asks whether the use is specific and explained. Data minimisation asks whether the data appears adequate, relevant, and limited for that use. A legitimate-sounding purpose does not answer how much detail is necessary.

Apply the [data-minimisation guide](/blog/data-minimisation-media-services/) after the purpose map so the two questions remain distinct.

## Watch for purpose drift

Purpose drift occurs when a category collected for one reason is later used for a materially different reason without a clear connection or updated explanation. Signals include a new advertising use, a new recipient category, cross-product profiling, an expanded analytics scope, or combining datasets for a new outcome.

A wording change is not automatically purpose drift. Compare old and new policy, feature behavior, provider list, settings, and operator explanation. Record **observed change**, **possible effect**, and **confirmed explanation** separately.

## Ask compatibility questions, not accusations

When a new use appears, ask:

- Is the new use described specifically?
- Which existing category supports it?
- Was that category originally collected for a related purpose?
- Does a new actor or destination appear?
- Can the user control or avoid the new feature?
- Did retention or security language change?

These questions organize evidence. A qualified reviewer determines legal compatibility where needed.

## Apply purpose analysis to connected sources

A user asks Norva to connect to a compatible source they own or are authorized to use. Record the purpose of handling source settings and requests separately from whatever purposes the independent source states in its own notice.

Do not assume that Norva's purpose statement governs the source or that the source's policy describes Norva. Each actor's official disclosure needs its own row.

## Connect purpose to retention

A stated purpose helps interpret why a category remains. Retention may be linked to an active account, a fixed duration, a support case, a legal record, or anonymisation. Use the [retention-language guide](/blog/data-retention-period-questions/) to test whether the event and outcome are described.

When a purpose ends, do not promise immediate deletion unless the current policy says so. Backups, limited records, or anonymisation may be described separately.

## Compare policy changes responsibly

Save the policy date, category, purpose wording, linked feature, actor, and user control. At the next update, compare meaning and scope. A newly clarified sentence can narrow ambiguity without changing processing; a short added phrase can materially expand it.

Ask support with a non-sensitive hypothetical example. Never include account history or source credentials merely to ask what a purpose means.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Accepting a vague purpose without examples.
- Treating purpose and minimisation as identical.
- Inferring an undisclosed use from a field name.
- Calling every policy clarification a new purpose.
- Combining Norva and source purposes.
- Assuming purpose end means immediate deletion.
- Presenting compatibility analysis as legal advice.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is “improve the service” a specific purpose?

It is broad. Look for concrete categories, activities, outcomes, recipients, and controls that explain what improvement means.

### Can data have more than one purpose?

A notice may state multiple purposes. Record each one and the category, actor, retention, and control that relate to it.

### Does a new feature always require a new policy?

Not necessarily. The answer depends on its data use, existing notice, applicable rules, and facts; compare evidence and seek qualified review when needed.
