# How to Document Your Next Subscription Renewal Date

> **In short:** Copy the next renewal or expiry directly from the original provider's active-subscription screen. Record its exact status wording, timezone, selected plan, billing interval, displayed amount, currency, tax treatment, purchase account in masked form, and evidence-check date. Distinguish renewal from trial end, cancellation date, receipt date, and access-through date. Add a private reminder, then update the record after any cancellation, plan, provider, price, or tax change.

A date without provenance can mislead a household. The original provider controls the billing schedule, while Norva may display entitlement for access.

## Identify the provider first

Use the [billing-provider guide](/blog/identify-subscription-billing-provider/) to confirm the store or payment provider and correct purchase account. Do not document a date from a different household member's subscription.

The provider's active-subscription screen is stronger evidence than memory or a historical marketing page.

## Copy status and date together

Record the exact status surrounding the date: renews, expires, active until, trial ends, or another provider-defined phrase. A date can mean something different after cancellation.

Do not rewrite provider wording into a guarantee. Preserve a redacted confirmation or secure reference where appropriate.

## State the timezone and timestamp

If the provider displays a timezone or exact time, capture it. If only a calendar date appears, write "time not displayed" rather than choosing midnight. Record when you checked the screen so later readers can judge freshness.

Automatic device time helps troubleshoot display inconsistencies, but the provider remains the billing source of truth.

## Record commercial context

Add plan label, billing interval, displayed amount, currency, and tax wording. Do not store card numbers or complete transaction identifiers. A renewal date without the plan and interval may be confused with another subscription.

The [receipt-organization guide](/blog/organize-subscription-receipts/) keeps supporting records minimal and searchable.

## Separate related dates

Trial start, trial end, first paid renewal, receipt issue, cancellation action, access-through date, and next renewal are distinct. Put them in separate fields. Never replace one with another to fill a blank.

Use the [trial-reminder guide](/blog/set-trial-renewal-reminder/) to create an early decision checkpoint from the confirmed trial end.

## Update after every change

Recheck the provider after cancellation, reactivation, plan switch, payment recovery, refund decision, or provider migration. Preserve old entries in a change log rather than silently overwriting them.

Do not predict prorating, credits, or effective dates. Record the provider's actual result.

## Share only what the household needs

A shared household calendar may need plan label, provider, renewal date, and action owner. Keep account identifiers, amounts if sensitive, receipts, and payment details in protected storage. Use neutral notification text on lock screens.

## Assign household ownership

Name one person responsible for checking the provider and updating the shared date. Record a backup contact without sharing store credentials. If billing ownership changes, treat that as a provider or account-administration handoff and reverify the subscription. A calendar entry without an accountable owner can remain technically accurate while no one performs the decision or cancellation.

## Verify the action state

A reminder does not prove that renewal remains on or off. At each checkpoint, reopen the provider and update the exact status. If Norva's entitlement disagrees, use the [subscription-status troubleshooting guide](/blog/subscription-status-not-updating/).

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Calculating a date instead of copying the provider display.
- Omitting status wording or timezone.
- Confusing trial end with paid renewal.
- Mixing receipt date and access-through date.
- Storing card data beside the reminder.
- Overwriting history after a plan change.
- Treating the Norva screen as final billing evidence.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which date should I trust?

Use the current original-provider subscription screen and preserve its exact status wording, then clarify conflicts through official support.

### What if no time is displayed?

Record the calendar date and explicitly note that the provider did not display a time or timezone.

### Should I update the record after cancellation?

Yes. Replace the future-renewal field with the provider's resulting status and access-through date while preserving change history.
