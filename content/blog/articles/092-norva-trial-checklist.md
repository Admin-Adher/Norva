# Your Norva Trial Checklist: What to Test Before Deciding

> **In short:** Test Norva with your own compatible authorised source, real devices, and essential household workflows. Prioritise source setup, search, playback, remote navigation, profiles, cross-device progress, languages, and eligible offline access. Record each result as pass, recheck, unavailable, or not applicable before the current trial ends.

A trial is most useful when it answers your decision questions. Random browsing can show the interface, but it may miss the one workflow that matters every evening.

## Before the trial starts

Verify that you have:

- a compatible media source you own or are legally authorised to use;
- access credentials and a working recovery route;
- the supported phone, browser, and TV devices you intend to test;
- several known media items with different metadata or versions;
- an item with known audio or subtitle choices, if languages matter;
- enough time to test before any current trial deadline.

Review [what Norva includes and what you provide](/blog/what-norva-includes/) before setup. The software subscription does not include a catalogue or media access.

## Define pass conditions

Write three to five critical outcomes. Examples:

- “I can find a known item by title on TV with the remote.”
- “A playback checkpoint created on mobile appears under the same profile on TV.”
- “Two household profiles keep separate favourites.”
- “My required subtitle track can be selected on a representative item.”

These are test statements, not promises. They are valid only for your setup and selected items.

## Test setup and catalogue understanding

Connect the source through the current official workflow. Then inspect:

1. whether expected categories appear;
2. whether known items are searchable;
3. whether artwork and metadata are understandable;
4. whether variants are grouped usefully when present;
5. whether unavailable items are clearly distinguishable;
6. whether a refresh reflects a known source change.

Missing metadata or guide information may originate from the source. Record the symptom before changing several settings.

## Test playback and controls

Use a small representative set:

- one film or long item;
- one episode if series are important;
- one item with multiple versions if available;
- one item with known language options;
- one item likely to expose a source limitation.

Test start, pause, resume, seeking, back navigation, and returning to the library. On TV, use only the remote for a complete session. The [ten-feature Norva test](/blog/norva-features-to-test/) expands this into feature-specific evidence.

## Test cross-device continuity

Create one clean checkpoint:

1. choose the intended profile;
2. play a known item on Device A;
3. pause at a memorable position;
4. allow the connected state to settle;
5. open the same item and version on Device B;
6. record the displayed position before playing.

Repeat with one favourite and one preference. Do not claim instant sync; observe and record the real result.

## Test profiles and plans

Create only the profiles the household needs. Give them different favourites or progress states and verify separation. Norva includes up to two profiles; Norva Family up to five.

Profile counts are not device or simultaneous-playback limits. Use the [Norva plans decision worksheet](/blog/norva-plans-explained/) and check current pricing and terms on the official page.

## Test conditional features honestly

Language and subtitle availability depends on the exact source media. Offline access depends on the device, source, media, and associated rights. Mark a feature “unavailable for this test item” instead of declaring the whole product broken or universally compatible.

For eligible offline access, complete a transfer, enable airplane mode, and play more than the first frame. The [offline playback guide](/blog/offline-playback-explained/) explains the full verification.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Testing only the visual design.
- Using unknown items with no baseline expectation.
- Changing account, profile, version, and device at once.
- Treating source limitations as universal product results.
- Inferring device limits from profile capacity.
- Waiting until the last trial hour to test TV or cancellation.
- Relying on outdated prices or terms.

Verify current trial, billing, renewal, and cancellation information immediately before subscribing.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many items should I test?

A small representative set is better than random volume. Choose items that expose the workflows and edge cases important to your household.

### What if one feature is unavailable?

Determine whether it is essential and whether the limitation comes from the device, source, media, rights, or product. Record the evidence and ask support when appropriate.

### Should I decide from one device?

Only if that is genuinely your whole use case. Cross-device and TV workflows should be tested on the actual supported screens you intend to use.
