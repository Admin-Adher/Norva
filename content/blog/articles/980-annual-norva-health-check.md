# An Annual Norva Account and Device Health Check

> **In short:** Once a year, reconcile account control, profiles, authorized sources, trusted devices, catalog organization, representative playback, preferences, progress, favorites, conditional offline storage, privacy records, support cases, and current plan terms. Preserve verified components, remove obsolete access and evidence, and assign an owner and trigger to every unresolved item.

An annual review is not a full reset. Its value comes from comparing the present household workflow with the previous verified state and current official information. Routine monthly checks can provide most evidence; the annual review connects those records to longer-term decisions.

Use the [maintenance and audit handbook](/blog/media-app-maintenance-audit-handbook/) to define scope and avoid uncontrolled testing.

## Review account control

Confirm that the account holder controls the current sign-in and recovery routes. Review unfamiliar activity and security notifications through official channels. Do not copy credentials or recovery material into the health-check record.

If a password recently changed, use the [post-password-change device audit](/blog/trusted-device-audit-after-password-change/) before continuing ordinary maintenance.

## Reconcile profiles and responsibilities

List profile purposes, not private histories. Confirm that each profile is still needed, clearly labeled, and assigned an administrator for rename or removal decisions. Check current official plan limits before creating or consolidating contexts.

Profiles organize viewing context; do not assume they are separate security accounts.

## Reconfirm source authority

For each connected source, record the administrator, authorization basis, current need, and broad health result. Verify reachability through the source's own authorized workflow. Exclude source addresses, usernames, passwords, and tokens from the scorecard.

Norva is software for organization and playback and does not include a media catalog or rights to third-party media.

## Reconcile devices and sessions

Compare current device records with known household equipment and recent events. Remove obsolete or uncontrolled access through current supported controls, then verify the result. Use neutral codes and broad device categories.

The [monthly account and device review](/blog/monthly-account-device-review/) should supply recent evidence, reducing the need for a large annual screenshot collection.

## Sample catalog and playback

Choose a known movie, multi-season series, variant case where available, and item with relevant language tracks. Reset filters and inspect identity, hierarchy, and version selection. Run start, pause, seek, track selection, clean exit, and same-screen resume on the primary screen.

Repeat one essential item on a secondary supported screen. Record actual results without claiming universal format or device compatibility.

## Check progress and favorites

Create one distinctive progress position and one temporary favorite state under the intended profile. Test A to B and B to A only on screens the household actually uses. Record displayed and actual positions, icon and list state, and recovery actions separately.

Return temporary favorite state to the household's intended value after testing.

## Review audio, subtitles, and accessibility

Use source-verified media to test essential audio and subtitle choices. Inspect readability at the real viewing distance. A preference cannot add a track absent from the media, and versions can contain different tracks.

Treat a failed accessibility requirement as a priority, not a minor average-score deduction.

## Review conditional offline storage

Where supported and authorized, identify prepared items, device storage, purpose, age, and deletion trigger. Test a small item in a genuinely disconnected state before relying on it. Remove local copies whose need has ended.

Current official privacy claims and feature eligibility require human verification before publication and each decision.

## Review privacy and support artifacts

Read the current privacy policy and terms. Delete redundant screenshots, recordings, exported logs, pairing-code images, and resolved support attachments. Retain only the minimum decision history and official case references.

Review any policy changes since the last annual check instead of relying on a stored summary.

## Review current plan fit

Open the official pricing page. Compare current limits, billing period, cancellation terms, and household needs. Do not embed an old fixed price in the annual record. Record whether the current plan remains suitable and who owns any follow-up.

## Common annual-review mistakes

- Resetting a verified setup instead of sampling it.
- Keeping obsolete profiles and sessions for convenience.
- Treating technical source access as authorization.
- Testing language tracks that the media does not contain.
- Retaining raw support evidence indefinitely.
- Using outdated pricing or policy summaries.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is an annual check enough by itself?

No. Use monthly or event-triggered reviews for account, device, update, or source changes. The annual check consolidates and challenges those records.

### Must every catalog item be tested?

No. Use a representative risk-based sample and expand only when evidence shows a broader pattern.

### What should happen to unresolved findings?

Assign an owner, impact, next test or official support route, and deadline. Record unknown rather than inventing a pass.
