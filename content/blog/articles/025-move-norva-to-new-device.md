# Moving to a New Device? A Safe Norva Setup Checklist

> **In short:** Inventory the old device, install Norva through an official route on the new supported device, sign in to the same account, verify catalogue, progress, favourites, and preferences, and treat offline downloads as local data. Only after the new baseline works should you sign out and remove the old device from trusted access.

A migration is easier when account data, source settings, and local device data are handled as separate layers.

## Before touching the old device

Do not immediately uninstall or reset it. Record:

- Norva account and profile;
- connected source names without credentials;
- one known favourite;
- one progress point;
- language or subtitle preference;
- any eligible offline items stored locally;
- visible app version;
- whether the device appears in trusted-device controls.

Norva states that catalogue, progress, history, favourites, and preferences can stay associated with one account on supported devices. Exact migration behaviour still needs observation.

The general model appears in [How Cross-Device Media Players Work](/blog/cross-device-media-player-explained/).

## Understand what may not transfer

Norva’s privacy policy says eligible offline downloads are encrypted and stored on the device rather than uploaded to Norva. Do not expect the downloaded file to appear on new hardware.

Temporary interface state, cached artwork, browser sessions, and device permissions can also remain local.

Source settings may be associated with the account, device, or both depending on current product behaviour. Verify rather than copying secrets into an informal note.

## Step 1: prepare the new device

Confirm it is supported, install current system security updates, and use Norva’s official web or app destination.

Use a trusted connection and enough storage for intended use. Do not disable platform protections to force installation.

**Observable result:** the official Norva account or pairing screen opens.

## Step 2: sign in or pair

Authenticate to the intended account. On TV, follow the current official pairing flow and protect temporary codes.

The [TV pairing guide](/blog/pair-tv-with-norva-account/) provides safe handling principles.

**Observable result:** the correct profile and account context are visible.

## Step 3: let the catalogue stabilise

Allow source sections to load. Locate the same known item recorded on the old device.

Check title identity and category before judging favourites or progress. A changed source record can look like failed synchronisation.

Mark observed, missing, different, or not applicable. Do not invent a sync time; allow normal reconnection and check once.

## Step 5: test one playback path

Open the reference item and begin briefly. Confirm controls and return behaviour on the new device.

A successful account sync does not prove every media format or source item works on the new hardware. Device capabilities can differ.

## Step 6: handle offline data separately

Remove eligible offline downloads from the old device using current controls when they are no longer needed. On the new device, download again only when device, source, and associated rights permit.

Do not attempt to copy encrypted app files manually or claim that a local backup will restore them.

## Step 7: revoke the old device

Once the new setup is confirmed:

1. sign out on the old device if available;
2. remove the Norva account from that device;
3. review trusted devices;
4. revoke the old record;
5. remove source secrets and local downloads;
6. follow the manufacturer’s reset process if transferring ownership.

Use [How to Review and Remove Trusted Devices](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) for unknown or obsolete records.

## If the old device is lost

Do not wait for a local sign-out. From a controlled session, review trusted devices, revoke the lost device where supported, change the account password, and consider changing exposed source credentials through the source owner.

Contact [Norva support](https://norva.tv/support) with a redacted account-security summary if access remains unclear.

## Limitations

The migration ledger is a reproducible checklist, not proof that every field always syncs. Current device support, source identity, network, and product version matter.

Profile limits must not be interpreted as device limits. Check current plan terms for any separate restrictions.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I copy Norva’s offline files to the new device?

Do not assume so. Norva says eligible downloads are encrypted and stored locally. Use documented download controls on the new device when permitted.

### Should I erase the old device first?

No. Confirm the new baseline and preserve needed account evidence before signing out and securely resetting the old hardware.

### What if favourites sync but progress does not?

Verify the same account, profile, and item version. Account states can fail for different reasons.
