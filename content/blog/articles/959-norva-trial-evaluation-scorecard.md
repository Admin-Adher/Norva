# How to Score Your Norva Trial Without Guesswork

> **In short:** Define and weight requirements before the trial, mark non-negotiable conditions, and test them with one authorized source and representative supported screens. Score only observed, repeatable results; grade evidence quality and assign unresolved items. A failed essential requirement overrides the total. Review current pricing, renewal, cancellation, and provider terms before the decision deadline, then record continue, pause, or decline with reasons.

A trial creates evidence only when the household knows what it is testing. Without a scorecard, recent impressions, interface polish, or one frustrating incident can dominate the decision. A small preweighted model keeps requirements and limits visible.

## Freeze requirements before testing

List five to eight requirements covering source readiness, primary-screen navigation, catalog organization, playback, required tracks, continuity, profiles, privacy, and support. Weight each from one to five and mark essentials.

The [Norva evaluation framework](/blog/norva-evaluation-framework/) helps define criteria before the scorecard starts.

## Record current trial and billing terms

Consult the official pricing page and terms on the day you begin. Record trial length, payment requirement, renewal date, billing provider, cancellation route, and any applicable refund wording. Public conditions can change, so do not rely on an old article or memory.

Set a private decision reminder before renewal. Do not include payment card details in the worksheet.

## Establish source and screen baselines

Use one compatible source you own or are authorized to use. Confirm reachability and prepare three to five known items. Record the primary supported screen and one secondary screen only when it represents an essential workflow.

If source readiness is uncertain, pause and use the [source-readiness gate](/blog/evaluate-source-readiness-for-norva/). An unstable source makes product scoring unreliable.

## Turn requirements into tests

Every row needs a short action and observable result. Replace “good TV experience” with “complete the five-step remote route with visible focus from normal distance.” Replace “syncs well” with a controlled progress marker inspected on a second supported screen.

Use the [cross-screen method](/blog/evaluate-norva-cross-screen-continuity/) when continuity has meaningful weight.

## Use a simple result scale

Score each requirement:

- 0: failed or not available for the required context.
- 1: limited, inconsistent, or unresolved.
- 2: passed with repeatable evidence.

Multiply by the preassigned weight. Untested rows receive no score. Do not give partial credit merely because a feature appears in marketing copy.

## Grade evidence quality

Mark evidence A for repeated direct observation with stable context, B for one direct observation, C for current official documentation that you have not tested, and D for memory or third-party information. Use A or B for essential workflow decisions where possible.

Keep screenshots redacted and avoid storing source credentials, private addresses, or detailed viewing history.

## Apply stop conditions

A failed essential source, caption, privacy, or primary-screen requirement should stop the positive decision regardless of the aggregate. The [caption-dependent evaluation](/blog/norva-for-caption-dependent-viewing/) demonstrates this principle for an access requirement.

Record whether the failure is product fit, source limitation, unsupported device context, or unknown. Do not force a binary explanation without evidence.

## Assign unresolved tests

Every unresolved row needs an owner, next action, official source, and deadline. Remove low-priority tests that do not affect the decision. If a material question cannot be answered before renewal, choose pause or decline rather than converting uncertainty into a pass.

Contact support with a redacted, reproducible description where appropriate.

## Complete the final review

Read current pricing, terms, privacy, and support information again. Confirm profile needs against current plans and include setup and maintenance effort. Then use the [pre-subscription decision review](/blog/pre-subscription-norva-decision-review/) to separate observed fit from unresolved risk.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Choosing weights after seeing results.
- Scoring untested claims.
- Letting total points hide an essential failure.
- Testing during a source outage.
- Missing the current renewal deadline.
- Saving secrets in trial evidence.

## Frequently asked questions

### What score means I should subscribe?

There is no universal threshold. Set your own before testing, and let essential stop conditions override the total.

### Should interface design receive a score?

Only if it maps to a real requirement, such as readable focus or an efficient essential route. Avoid a vague polish bonus.

### Can official documentation replace testing?

It can answer availability and policy questions, but essential personal workflows should be observed on the intended supported setup where possible.
