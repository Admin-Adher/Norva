# What to Check Before Changing a Subscription Provider

> **In short:** Treat a provider change as ending one billing relationship and starting another, not as an automatic transfer. Confirm the old provider, purchase account, cancellation result, access-through date, and receipts. Separately verify the new provider, plan, price, tax, interval, eligibility, purchase account, and cancellation terms. Ask whether Norva account state and entitlement remain associated. Avoid overlapping purchases or access gaps, preserve redacted proof, and verify both provider statuses after the handoff.

A billing channel can affect subscription management even when the media account remains the same. Current Norva terms direct management to the original provider.

## Clarify why the provider should change

Common reasons include household billing ownership, device ecosystem, payment method, regional availability, or account consolidation. Write the actual objective so the household can verify success.

Do not change merely to troubleshoot stale entitlement. Use the [subscription-status guide](/blog/subscription-status-not-updating/) first.

## Inventory the current relationship

Identify the old provider, masked purchase account, plan, renewal status, price, interval, currency, tax, access-through date, and receipt location. The [billing-provider guide](/blog/identify-subscription-billing-provider/) resolves uncertainty before any cancellation.

Preserve minimal proof because deleting the old store account or email can make later support harder.

## Verify the new offer independently

Check the current plan, profile cap, supported region, price, tax, interval, payment method, and cancellation route on the new provider's final screen. Do not assume a trial, discount, refund, or migration credit applies to an existing subscriber.

Norva currently publishes two profile-based plans, but all live commercial claims need verification at the moment of purchase.

## Choose a handoff sequence

Use exact provider dates to decide whether to cancel first, wait for access to end, or start the new subscription at another point. There is no universal correct overlap because provider terms and tolerance for an access gap differ.

The [original-provider cancellation guide](/blog/cancel-subscription-original-provider/) explains how to record status without promising timing.

## Protect account continuity

Confirm the intended Norva account and profiles before buying through the new channel. Do not create another Norva account unless official guidance requires it. Ask support how entitlement maps if the purchase identities differ.

Progress, favorites, source settings, and profiles should not be assumed transferable between separate accounts.

## Preserve both receipt trails

Keep provider, plan, purchase date, cancellation result, access dates, amount, currency, tax, and redacted references. The [receipt-organization guide](/blog/organize-subscription-receipts/) separates useful evidence from payment secrets.

Do not combine records so thoroughly that it becomes unclear which provider issued which transaction.

## Verify after the switch

Check that the old provider shows no unintended future renewal and the new provider shows the expected active status. Then verify Norva entitlement, profiles, source connection, and supported devices. Record exact wording and timestamps.

If status is wrong, stop further purchases and contact official support with redacted evidence.

## Define a rollback boundary

Before acting, decide which step can still be reversed without a second charge or lost evidence. Once the old provider confirms cancellation or access expiry, do not assume reactivation will restore the same terms. Keep the old account recoverable until the new entitlement is verified, while avoiding overlapping renewal.

## Review privacy and household ownership

The new provider may process account and transaction data under its own notice. Confirm which household member owns the purchase account, who can cancel, and what happens if that person leaves the household.

Never share store credentials to simplify administration; use provider-supported family or delegation controls where available.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating a provider change as an automatic transfer.
- Assuming a new trial or discount applies.
- Starting a second purchase before identifying the first.
- Creating another Norva account unnecessarily.
- Losing old cancellation and receipt evidence.
- Predicting refunds, prorating, or effective dates.
- Sharing store credentials with a household administrator.

## Frequently asked questions

### Will my subscription automatically move to the new provider?

Do not assume so. Treat the two provider relationships separately and verify current Norva support guidance.

### Am I eligible for another trial?

Eligibility depends on the current offer, account, and provider; do not rely on a general landing-page statement.

### Can the switch create duplicate billing?

Yes, if both relationships renew. Record exact dates and verify the old and new statuses independently.
