# Create an Offline Viewing Plan for a Shared Device

> **In short:** A shared-device plan should name who carries and charges the device, which profile each person uses, how local storage is allocated, and who may remove each item. Verify every person's essential audio or subtitles offline, protect the screen lock, avoid conflicting progress, and leave power for essential tasks. Do not assume local items are private or profile-isolated.

One shared device concentrates several resources: storage, battery, account access, viewing history, and physical custody. A fair plan is based on actual needs and clear responsibility, not an automatic equal split.

## Assign physical responsibility

Name the person who will:

- carry the device;
- keep it charged;
- know the screen-lock method where appropriate;
- connect approved headphones or adapters;
- run the final offline test;
- reconnect and review progress;
- coordinate cleanup afterwards.

Avoid placing passwords or sensitive source credentials in a shared worksheet. The [media-player security checklist](/blog/media-player-security-checklist/) provides a broader account and device review.

## Map people to profiles

Record the intended Norva profile for each viewer and confirm it before preparation. Norva plans currently provide up to two profiles on Norva and up to five on Norva Family; these are profile capacities, not guarantees about devices or simultaneous playback.

Use [profile-aware offline planning](/blog/plan-offline-viewing-by-profile/) when progress, favourites, or language preferences have already become mixed. On the shared device, verify the current behaviour rather than assuming every local item is hidden from other profiles.

## Allocate storage by need

Ask each viewer for realistic session windows, one preferred item, required tracks, and one fallback only if necessary. Measure completed local sizes instead of assigning the same number of items to everyone.

Protect a device-level free-space reserve before dividing the remainder. A longer trip or accessibility requirement may justify a different share. Document the reason so the allocation can be reviewed without turning personal viewing choices into a competition.

If more than one supported device is available, [divide offline storage across devices](/blog/divide-offline-storage-across-devices/) by reliability and use case rather than duplicating everything.

## Prepare each person's essentials

For each item:

1. open the exact title, episode, and version;
2. confirm item-level offline eligibility;
3. complete the transfer;
4. select the intended profile;
5. test required audio and subtitles;
6. disable connectivity and sample playback;
7. record who owns the removal decision.

Offline access remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device.

## Plan session handovers

Agree when the device changes hands, how much battery must remain, and whether headphones need to be re-paired. Before playback, each person should confirm the active profile and exact episode.

Avoid two disconnected viewers changing the same item's progress under one profile. If that happens, document both states and wait until reconnection before continuing elsewhere.

## Respect shared-device privacy

A shared screen can expose catalogue choices, progress, notifications, or account information. Use the screen lock, keep notifications appropriate for the setting, and avoid browsing another person's history. Do not assume profiles create a private security boundary.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Splitting item counts equally without measuring size or need.
- Sharing account credentials in the planning sheet.
- Assuming profiles hide local media from one another.
- Removing an item without the intended viewer's confirmation.
- Forgetting battery needed for tickets, maps, or communication.
- Mixing progress by leaving the wrong profile active.
- Preparing every item on every available device.

Device and app behaviour can change. Repeat the checks after updates, migration, or a change in household arrangements.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should each person receive the same storage amount?

Not necessarily. Allocate from realistic sessions, measured item sizes, and essential track needs while protecting a shared device reserve.

### Are profiles private from other device users?

Do not treat profiles as a security boundary. Use the device screen lock and respectful household practices, and verify what the current interface exposes.

### Who should reconnect the device?

Assign one person to confirm the account, profile, and resulting progress before anyone continues on another supported device.
