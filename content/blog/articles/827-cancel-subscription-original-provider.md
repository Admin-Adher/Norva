# Why Subscription Cancellation Starts With the Original Provider

> **In short:** A store or payment provider that created the subscription controls its renewal and cancellation status. First identify that provider and the correct signed-in store account, then use its current official cancellation flow. Record the exact resulting status and access-through date, retaining minimal redacted proof. Treat Norva account deletion, profile removal, sign-out, application uninstall, source disconnection, and local-file deletion as separate actions that do not themselves prove billing cancellation.

Norva's current terms direct subscription management to the original store or payment provider. This division explains why a cancellation control may not appear in the media application itself.

## Find the subscription's source of truth

Open the subscription-management area for the account used at purchase. Compare the plan name, receipt issuer, renewal date, and transaction channel. The [billing-provider identification guide](/blog/identify-subscription-billing-provider/) offers a safe sequence when the household is uncertain.

Do not ask another person to send full receipt or card details in chat. A provider name and redacted date are normally enough for coordination.

## Use only current official instructions

Apple and Google publish subscription-management instructions for their own ecosystems. Other providers have separate flows. Start from the official account portal or help page rather than a search result, unsolicited message, or third-party cancellation service.

Menus and wording can change. This article deliberately does not reproduce a fixed sequence of buttons.

## Read the confirmation literally

After the action, the provider may display a status and an access end date. Record that exact wording. "Canceled," "expires on," "active until," and "renewal off" can describe related but distinct states, depending on the provider.

Do not promise immediate loss of access, a refund, a credit, or a particular effective date unless the provider confirms it for the transaction.

## Keep minimal cancellation evidence

Save the provider, action date, status, access-through date, official help URL, and a redacted confirmation reference. Exclude full receipt numbers, store credentials, payment instrument data, and tokens.

The [account-closure evidence guide](/blog/close-account-keep-cancellation-proof/) explains when to preserve this record before deleting an email address or account needed to retrieve it.

## Keep account closure separate

Canceling renewal does not necessarily delete the Norva account or profiles. Conversely, closing a Norva account does not necessarily cancel a store subscription. A complete exit plan separately addresses subscription, account, connected source, signed-in devices, eligible downloads, and any data held by the provider.

Use the [media-account lifecycle handbook](/blog/media-account-lifecycle-handbook/) to map every layer.

## Verify later if timing matters

If the subscription remains usable until a stated date, set a private check after the status change. Confirm that no new renewal appears and that the expected access state is shown. The [renewal-date guide](/blog/document-next-renewal-date/) helps preserve the provider's displayed date without guessing.

If status conflicts across screens, contact the original provider first with redacted evidence, then Norva support if entitlement inside Norva remains stale.

Tell other household administrators that renewal was changed, but share only the provider, status, and access-through date. Keep the redacted confirmation with the person responsible for billing. This prevents another person from restarting or replacing the subscription because they misread continued access as an active renewal.

## Original evidence: provider cancellation verification record

| Evidence | Record safely | Exclude |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Original provider | Provider name and official URL | Store password |
| Purchase account | Partially masked account reference | Full email on shared notes |
| Action | Date and action type | Session token |
| Result | Exact status wording | Full transaction ID |
| Access | Displayed through-date | Card details |

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Looking for cancellation only inside the media application.
- Using a different store account from the purchase account.
- Treating uninstall as cancellation.
- Assuming account deletion stops provider billing.
- Predicting refund or access timing.
- Saving complete receipts in shared storage.
- Failing to verify the status shown after action.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why cannot Norva always cancel a store subscription?

The original provider controls its own billing relationship and subscription interface; Norva's current terms direct management to that provider.

### Does cancellation delete my Norva profiles?

Not necessarily. Subscription status and account or profile data are separate lifecycle layers.

### What proof should I keep?

Keep provider, date, exact status, access-through date, and a redacted confirmation securely, without payment credentials or full transaction values.
