# How to Approve a New Source Before It Is Connected

> **In short:** Do not connect a source until the household verifies who owns or controls it, that its use is authorised, that Norva compatibility has current evidence, that credentials can be entered privately, and that affected profiles understand the change. Define a limited test, success criteria, and disconnect path. Any unresolved ownership, rights, or credential question blocks approval.

Norva is a software media player and organiser. Its subscription does not provide a media catalogue or replace the source's own terms.

## Identify the source owner

Name the person or organisation that controls the source and the household member responsible for it. Record a safe label, not a private URL or credential.

If ownership or permission is disputed, stop the process.

## Verify authorisation and terms

The source owner should confirm that household use fits the source's current terms and associated rights. Do not treat technical access as legal authorisation.

Norva's terms require responsible use of connected sources. Sensitive legal questions need appropriate review.

## Verify compatibility evidence

Use current Norva product documentation or support confirmation for the connection method. Do not infer compatibility because another app recognises the source or because a mockup contains a logo.

Record the source of truth and date. “Expected to work” is not approval evidence.

## Assign the source steward

Choose a willing person to handle private credential entry, planned refreshes, outages, and removal. The [source-responsibility card](/blog/how-to-assign-source-connection-responsibilities-at-home/) defines the role.

## Protect credential entry

Follow [the household credential boundary](/blog/how-to-keep-source-credentials-out-of-household-sharing/). Enter secrets only through verified controls on a trusted device, away from shared viewers and recording tools.

Never place credentials in the approval form.

## Assess household impact

List:

- profiles that will browse the source;
- devices expected to use it;
- possible duplicate or grouped versions;
- language and subtitle expectations;
- eligible local-media implications;
- categories or favourites that may change;
- privacy concerns on shared screens.

Do not promise tracks, quality, or availability before observing the authorised source.

## Define a limited test

Choose one privacy-safe item with clear metadata. Test:

1. source connection appears under the intended account;
2. item identity is accurate enough to recognise;
3. a short playback action works on one supported device;
4. visible audio and subtitles match source availability;
5. disconnect or rollback control is understood.

A test passes only the documented scope.

## Plan rollback before connection

Write who can disconnect the source, what active sessions must pause, how eligible local media is reviewed, and who informs affected profile owners. A rollback that depends on unknown controls is not ready.

## Communicate the result

Tell the household what was approved, which profiles and devices are in scope, what remains unverified, and how to report a problem. Do not share credentials or private source identifiers.

The [governance pillar](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) provides decision thresholds.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid connecting first and asking permission later, using technical access as rights evidence, assuming universal compatibility, and testing across every profile at once.

Source terms, availability, metadata, and Norva support can change. Approval documents one decision at one time; it is not a permanent guarantee.

## Define acceptance evidence before connecting

Write the smallest safe test set: successful authorization, expected library scope, one ordinary browse, one authorised playback version, language and subtitle visibility, and clean removal. Assign an owner to each result and set a time limit for the trial.

If a mandatory check fails or the source requests unexplained permissions, stop and roll back through verified controls. Do not widen access, copy credentials to another device, or add more household profiles to “see if it works.”

## Frequently asked questions

### Can a household approve a source because it already works elsewhere?

No. Verify Norva compatibility and authorised use independently.

### Should every profile be tested?

Start with one privacy-safe profile and item. Expand only after the limited test passes and owners agree.

### What if the disconnect path is unclear?

Defer connection and contact support. Rollback readiness is part of approval.
