# Ten Norva Features to Test With Your Own Authorized Source

> **In short:** Test Norva with known items from your own compatible source, not a random catalogue sample. Verify organisation, search, variants, playback, progress, favourites, profiles, language preferences, TV navigation, and eligible offline access. Record each result and distinguish a Norva issue from a source, device, network, media, or rights limitation.

Norva's subscription covers the software experience, not a media catalogue or media access. The strongest evaluation therefore uses a source you own or are legally authorised to use and items whose expected metadata and tracks you already understand.

## Prepare a representative test set

Choose:

- one frequently watched item;
- one title with several versions, if the source has them;
- one episode;
- one item with known audio or subtitle options;
- one item intentionally unavailable or incomplete, if safely identifiable;
- two supported devices, including TV if it matters.

Read [what Norva includes and what you provide](/blog/what-norva-includes/) before interpreting a missing source capability as a product failure.

## The ten tests

### 1. Catalogue organisation

Confirm expected categories and known titles appear in a structure you understand. Record missing or duplicated entries rather than immediately editing the source.

### 2. Search

Search for an exact known title, a partial title, and a person or category only if the current interface supports that field. Note response and result relevance; do not invent a speed target.

### 3. Grouped variants

Open a title known to have multiple versions. Verify that the versions are distinguishable and that selecting one does not silently open another. This depends on source metadata.

### 4. Basic playback controls

Start, pause, seek, resume, and exit one known item. Confirm Back returns to the expected context on the tested device.

### 5. Playback progress

Pause at a memorable point on Device A. Open the same profile, item, and version on Device B and record the displayed position before playing. Do not assume immediate sync.

### 6. Favourites and history

Add one favourite and play one distinct item. Check the same profile on another supported device. Then remove the favourite and verify the intended state.

### 7. Profile separation

Give two profiles different favourites or checkpoints. Norva supports up to two profiles; Norva Family up to five. Verify separation without treating profiles as device limits.

### 8. Audio and subtitle preferences

Use an item with known tracks. Save a preference, then test another supported device. The preference can persist, but actual tracks depend on the source version and media.

### 9. TV remote navigation

Using only the remote, move from launch to search, details, playback, and back to the library. Record any focus trap or unexpected exit. The TV interface is designed for remote navigation.

### 10. Eligible offline access

Only when the device, source, media, and associated rights permit it, prepare one item and test it in airplane mode. Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted on the device and not uploaded to Norva.

The [offline playback guide](/blog/offline-playback-explained/) gives the full lifecycle.

Mark “Unavailable” when an input such as a media track or eligible offline control is absent. Mark “Recheck” when the expected capability exists but the observed result is unclear. Do not turn unknowns into passes.

## Interpret the results

An essential failure matters more than several optional passes. For each recheck, classify the likely layer:

- Norva interface or account;
- connected source;
- device or operating system;
- network;
- exact media version;
- associated rights;
- unknown.

Use the [Norva troubleshooting checklist](/blog/norva-troubleshooting-checklist/) before broad resets. For a complete purchase decision, combine this sheet with the [Norva trial checklist](/blog/norva-trial-checklist/).

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Testing unknown titles with no expected baseline.
- Changing several settings between attempts.
- Treating absent source tracks as a failed preference feature.
- Assuming profile counts equal devices or streams.
- Testing offline playback while Wi-Fi remains enabled.
- Sharing credentials in evidence screenshots.
- Averaging an essential failure into a high score.
- Publishing one household's result as universal compatibility.

Features and terms can change. Recheck the official pages and repeat critical tests before a final decision.

## Frequently asked questions

### Must every test pass?

No. Not-applicable and source-unavailable results can be valid. Every workflow you marked essential should pass or receive a satisfactory explanation.

### How many devices should I use?

Use the supported devices central to your real routine. Two are enough to test cross-device state; include TV when remote navigation matters.

### What evidence should I keep?

Keep item names, device versions, times, expected state, and observed result. Never record passwords, one-time codes, recovery codes, or secret source details.
