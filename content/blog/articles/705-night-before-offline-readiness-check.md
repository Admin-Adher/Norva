# The Night-Before Offline Readiness Check

> **In short:** The night before, confirm the intended account, profile, device, and exact offline items. Make sure transfers are complete, disable connectivity, reopen and sample every essential item, and verify required audio and subtitles. Recheck free storage, battery, charging, headphones, and the cleanup date. Anything uncertain stays “recheck,” not “ready.”

This final review is not the time to expand the batch. Its purpose is to find preventable failures while a trusted connection and enough time to correct them are still available.

## Freeze the intended batch

List the exact titles, episodes, and versions you expect to use. Confirm that each serves a real session. Move speculative extras out of the active set, especially when storage is limited.

Check that the intended Norva account and profile are active. On a shared device, identify who owns each selection and who may remove it later. The [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) provides the larger decision framework if the batch still feels unresolved.

## Check completion and local storage

Open the app's offline area. Do not rely on a queue label or partial indicator. Each essential item should show a completed local state. Then open the operating system's storage view and verify that enough working space remains for normal device use.

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored on the device. Availability remains conditional on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. If an item has no current offline control or did not finish, replace it with an already verified alternative rather than assuming it will work later.

## Run the disconnected test

Use airplane mode or disable both Wi-Fi and mobile data. On some devices, Wi-Fi or Bluetooth can be re-enabled while airplane mode remains visible, so inspect the actual radio state.

For every essential item:

1. close and reopen the app;
2. find the item through its offline area;
3. play near the beginning;
4. seek to or resume from a later point;
5. confirm video and audio continue;
6. stop and reopen once more if the item is critical.

Use the focused [offline availability verification](/blog/verify-offline-availability-before-leaving/) when an item has multiple versions or an ambiguous state.

## Verify language and accessibility needs

Do not assume tracks shown while connected are packaged in the local copy. Test the exact audio and subtitle options that matter. If more than one person will use the device, check each person's essential choice.

The [offline audio preparation guide](/blog/prepare-audio-options-before-offline/) and [offline subtitle preparation guide](/blog/prepare-subtitles-before-offline/) offer separate track-level checklists. If a required option is missing, mark the item unsuitable for the planned session.

## Prepare the device around the media

Complete these practical checks:

- charge the device and inspect its current battery condition;
- pack a compatible cable and permitted charging option;
- test headphones and any needed adapter;
- choose comfortable brightness and volume;
- review whether power-saving settings change required behaviour;
- check screen lock and account access;
- avoid beginning a major device update immediately before departure;
- record when the offline batch will be reviewed or removed.

Avoid promising a fixed runtime. Android and Apple document battery-saving controls, but actual playback duration depends on hardware, battery condition, display, temperature, and use.

## Original evidence: pass-or-recheck card

| Check | Evidence | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Exact account and profile | Visible account context | Pass / Recheck |
| Every transfer complete | In-app completed state | Pass / Recheck |
| Network truly unavailable | Wi-Fi and mobile data disabled | Pass / Recheck |
| Essential items sampled | Beginning and later point play | Pass / Recheck |
| Required tracks work | Audio and subtitles tested | Pass / Recheck |
| Device ready | Storage, battery, charger, headphones | Pass / Recheck |
| Cleanup assigned | Review date recorded | Pass / Recheck |

Do not change a Recheck result to Pass merely because departure is close. Remove the dependency or correct and repeat the test.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Adding an untested item after the final check.
- Assuming airplane mode always disables every radio in its current configuration.
- Testing only the first frame.
- Forgetting a required subtitle or alternate audio track.
- Starting an app or operating-system update too late.
- Depending on one nearly empty battery or an untested cable.
- Treating a completed indicator as permanent availability.

The checklist reflects one device and one moment. Repeat it after material changes to the app, device, source, account, or rights.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I sign out after testing?

Do not change account state unless necessary. Confirm the intended account and profile, then leave the tested setup stable for departure.

### Is one playback sample enough?

Sample more than the opening moment. A later point gives better evidence that the local copy is usable, although no short test can guarantee every frame.

### What if only one essential item fails?

Retry while connected if there is time. Otherwise remove it from the plan and use an already verified fallback; do not rely on an uncertain copy.
