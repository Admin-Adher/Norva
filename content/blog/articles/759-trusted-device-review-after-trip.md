# Review Trusted Devices After Every Trip

> **In short:** On a trusted connection, compare every device or session shown by Norva, the device platform, primary email, and authorized source with the trip itinerary. Match names, approximate locations, times, browsers, and temporary televisions to known activity. Remove devices you no longer use through current official controls, investigate ambiguous entries before trusting them, and secure the affected accounts immediately when activity is genuinely unrecognized.

Travel creates legitimate account noise: airport locations can appear approximate, one phone can create several sessions, and a temporary TV may use a generic name. The review should reconcile evidence rather than deleting everything unfamiliar on sight.

## Gather the travel ledger

List every device carried, borrowed, lost, replaced, sold, or used temporarily. Include accommodation televisions, public or shared browsers, repaired devices, and phones used only for account recovery. Record dates, approximate locations, and the person responsible.

Bring the [temporary-TV sign-out receipt](/blog/sign-out-accommodation-tv-checklist/) and any lost-device incident record. These explain entries that would otherwise look suspicious.

## Review each official account separately

Open known official destinations on a trusted device. Check current Norva account controls documented by support, the platform account, the recovery email, and the compatible media source. Do not assume removing a device from one service removes it from all related accounts.

Apple and Google both document account device or session lists, but the displayed details and removal effects differ. Read the confirmation before removing anything.

## Match entries to evidence

For each device or session, compare:

- device name and type;
- browser or app when shown;
- approximate location;
- first or last activity time;
- traveller and purpose;
- whether sign-out was observed;
- whether the device is still owned and trusted.

Google notes that one device can show multiple sessions and that recent activity can include background communication. A location can also represent a nearby place rather than the exact physical position. Label uncertain entries for investigation instead of inventing certainty.

## Decide keep, remove, or escalate

Keep a device only when it remains owned, recognized, appropriately secured, and needed. Remove or sign out a device that was temporary, lost, sold, no longer used, or not under the household's control, using the service's current official option.

The general [trusted-device removal guide](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) helps document the effect. If current Norva controls do not expose a remote action, contact official support rather than claiming it happened.

Escalate unrecognized activity: preserve evidence, change the affected password through the official destination, secure the recovery email, review other sessions, and protect the authorized source. Do not send credentials in a support report.

## Treat a lost phone as a separate incident

If a device was lost, use the [phone-loss media account response](/blog/secure-media-account-after-phone-loss/) and the platform's locate, secure, or erase service. A removal from an account list does not prove the physical device was erased.

Keep requested, pending, and confirmed action statuses separate.

## Repeat after systems catch up

Account lists can update on their own schedules. Perform the first review soon after return and a second review after temporary devices have reconnected or enough time has passed for account records to refresh. Do not use a universal waiting period; follow current service guidance.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating an approximate location as exact proof.
- Assuming one session equals one physical device.
- Removing entries without checking the itinerary.
- Believing one service's removal secures every account.
- Calling a remote action complete before confirmation.
- Forgetting temporary TVs and shared browsers.
- Sending credentials with a support request.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why do I see several sessions for one device?

A service may create separate sessions for apps, browsers, sign-ins, or background activity. Review the provider's current explanation and compare details.

### Should I change my password after every trip?

Not automatically. Change it when exposure, reuse, an unclosed session, or unrecognized activity creates a reason, and secure the recovery path too.

### Is removing a lost phone the same as erasing it?

No. Account removal and device erasure are different controls. Use official platform lost-device guidance and verify the displayed status.
