# Duplicate Channels in the Guide? Compare Source Identifiers

> **In short:** Select two or three duplicate-looking rows and record each source label, source and visible identifier, name, number, logo, region or version cue, channel group, current and next schedule, filters, device, application version, and timestamp. Verify each through the authorized source and compare another supported device. Classify alternate source, regional or version difference, same-looking row, device-specific duplication, or unknown before hiding, renaming, or deleting anything.

Two rows with the same name and logo may represent separate sources, regions, resolutions, languages, or channel versions. “Duplicate” should describe observed similarity, not an assumed shared identity.

## Freeze guide context

Record account, profile, enabled sources, channel group, favorites or availability view, filters, search, selected date and time window, device clock and zone, device, and application version.

The [TV guide handbook](/blog/tv-guide-troubleshooting-handbook/) provides the wider evidence matrix.

## Select representative rows

Choose two duplicate-looking rows and one control. Assign privacy-safe labels A, B, and C. Record row positions and whether duplicates appear in all channels, a group, search, favorites, or only one view.

## Compare source identifiers

Through the provider's official authorized route, record source identifiers or numbers only where visible and safe, plus source account or label in masked form. Do not expose full source addresses or hidden databases.

## Compare visible identity cues

Record display name, number, logo, region, language, source version, and any resolution or edition cue that the interface explicitly shows. Do not infer quality or capability from a name suffix alone.

## Compare schedules

Capture current and next program title cues and start/end times for A and B at the same timestamp. Different schedules suggest distinct channel contexts; matching samples do not prove complete identity.

## Compare groups and filters

Record channel group, favorites state, source selection, and visible filters for each row. A channel exposed by two sources can appear in the same combined view without being an exact duplicate.

## Check wrong-program mapping

If one row consistently carries the other row's schedule, use the [channel-program crosswalk](/blog/wrong-program-mapped-channel/) before deleting it. Mapping and duplication are separate observations.

## Check partial coverage

If one duplicate-looking row has schedule data and the other remains blank, use the [partial guide coverage](/blog/one-channel-guide-others-blank/) to compare identifiers and source schedule availability.

## Compare household state

Record favorites or other visible household state only when relevant. Do not unfavorite, reorder, hide, or open several rows merely to test identity. Preserve the original guide arrangement.

## Compare another supported device

Use the same account, profile, sources, group, filters, guide window, and close timestamp. Record both application versions. A duplicate visible on one device only is cross-screen evidence.

## Preserve the timeline

Record source addition or update, import or refresh, first duplicate observation, application update, device change, and current state. Do not claim that the preceding event created the duplicate without further evidence.

## Avoid bulk cleanup

Do not delete, hide, rename, merge, reorder, remap, or remove a source before saving the table. A duplicate-looking row may hold a distinct schedule or authorized source relationship.

## Classify the pair

Use different source, different identifier, different region or version, different schedule, same visible cues with unknown identity, one row blank, one-device duplication, appeared after documented event, or unknown. Avoid claims about Norva's deduplication logic.

## Prepare support evidence

Use the [guide evidence template](/blog/guide-issue-support-evidence/) with the A/B table, source verification, schedule sample, filters, devices, versions, timeline, and actions.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Matching channels by name and logo alone.
- Ignoring source, region, group, or schedule.
- Treating a matching sample as complete identity proof.
- Deleting a row before preserving its schedule.
- Comparing different filters or guide windows.
- Exposing full source identifiers or addresses.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are same-name rows always duplicates?

No. Compare source, visible identifiers, version cues, schedule, group, and context before classifying them.

### Should I hide one row immediately?

No. Preserve identity, schedule, and household-state evidence, then follow current source and Norva support guidance.

### What if both rows look identical?

Record the identical visible cues and mark internal identity unknown. Do not infer a matching or deduplication rule.
