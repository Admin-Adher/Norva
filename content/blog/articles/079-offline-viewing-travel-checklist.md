# Before You Travel: A Responsible Offline-Viewing Checklist

> **In short:** Prepare at least early enough to fix a failed transfer. Confirm that each item is eligible, reserve storage, complete the download on a trusted connection, and test playback with the device disconnected. Check audio, subtitles, battery, and account state. Offline access remains conditional on the device, source, media, and associated rights.

A title visible in a connected library is not automatically ready for a flight or train tunnel. Responsible preparation means testing the exact item without a network and keeping only the local copies you genuinely need.

## Forty-eight hours before departure

Start with the trip, not the catalogue. Estimate how much viewing time is realistic, then choose a small set of items. This avoids filling the device with unused copies.

For each item:

1. confirm that you are using the intended account and profile;
2. open the exact title, episode, and version;
3. check whether an offline control is available;
4. confirm that your use is permitted by the source and associated rights;
5. note essential audio or subtitle needs;
6. check free device storage before starting.

Norva offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, and rights associated with the media. The [offline playback explainer](/blog/offline-playback-explained/) describes the complete lifecycle.

## Download on a connection you trust

Use a stable trusted network when possible. Keep the device powered and avoid starting an operating-system update at the same time. Let one transfer reach a completed state before deciding that the workflow works.

Do not rely on a spinning indicator, percentage, or queued label alone. The app should report that the item is ready locally. If the transfer stalls, use the [source, storage, and network checklist](/blog/offline-download-failed-checklist/) while you still have time to retry.

Public Wi-Fi can be convenient but should be treated as a shared environment. If it is unavoidable, verify the network name and use the [public Wi-Fi safety checklist](/blog/media-player-public-wifi-safety/).

## Run a real disconnected test

The decisive test is simple:

1. finish the download;
2. close the item;
3. enable airplane mode or disable both Wi-Fi and mobile data;
4. reopen the app;
5. find the item through the offline area;
6. play a section near the beginning and another later point;
7. test the required audio and subtitle options;
8. stop playback and reconnect.

Testing only the first frame can miss an incomplete copy. You do not need to watch the whole item, but sample more than one point.

## Prepare the device itself

Before packing:

- charge the device and any permitted power bank;
- pack the correct charging cable and audio adapter;
- install updates early enough to complete and verify them;
- check available storage again;
- set a comfortable brightness and volume;
- confirm headphones connect without relying on cabin Wi-Fi;
- review mobile-data settings to avoid an unexpected fallback;
- keep the screen lock active.

Avoid promising a fixed battery duration. Display brightness, radio use, device age, temperature, and playback workload all affect runtime. The [long-journey phone and tablet setup](/blog/phone-tablet-long-journey-setup/) covers device preparation in more detail.

## Plan reconnection and cleanup

After travel, reconnect through a trusted network and allow the intended progress state to update before comparing another device. Then remove finished or unneeded offline items through the app's own controls.

Do not assume deleting an app-managed file through a file manager is supported. The [offline storage cleanup guide](/blog/remove-offline-downloads/) explains the safer removal order.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Starting every transfer minutes before departure.
- Assuming a library listing proves offline eligibility.
- Testing while Wi-Fi or mobile data is still active.
- Forgetting that episodes can have different track options.
- Filling nearly all device storage.
- Depending on public Wi-Fi for a last-minute account recovery.
- Keeping old offline items indefinitely.
- Treating a local copy as permission beyond the source's terms and rights.

Offline controls and storage behaviour vary by device and source. Recheck after major app or operating-system changes.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many items should I prepare?

Base the choice on realistic travel time and measured item sizes on your device. Leave working space rather than trying to fill every available unit of storage.

### Can I test offline playback without travelling?

Yes. Airplane mode creates a controlled disconnected test. Confirm that Wi-Fi has not been manually re-enabled.

### Will progress sync during the journey?

Not while the device has no connection. Reconnect later, use the same account and profile, and verify the resulting state before changing another device.
