# What to Do About Offline Media After a Device Is Lost

> **In short:** Put personal safety first, then use the device maker's official lost-device service to locate, mark, secure, or erase the device when those options are available and appropriate. Review Norva, email, platform, and authorized-source access from a trusted device. Treat a remote command as pending until its status is confirmed; encryption helps, but it does not replace prompt account and device response.

A lost viewing device can contain more than offline media: notifications, email, authentication apps, travel documents, saved sessions, and other personal data may matter more. Respond to the whole device rather than focusing only on one app.

## Start with safety and a trusted connection

Do not confront a suspected finder or thief. Use another trusted device and a network you control where possible. Record the time and location where the device was last seen, its model, identifier information you already maintain, battery state if visible, and the time loss was discovered.

If theft, immediate danger, or sensitive work data is involved, follow local authority or organizational procedures.

## Use official lost-device tools

Apple and Android provide official services that may help locate, mark, secure, or erase a device when they were configured and the device meets current service conditions. Follow the current platform instructions for the exact model and account.

Remote actions can depend on connectivity, power, sign-in state, and prior settings. A request to lock or erase is not the same as confirmed completion. Capture the displayed status and check it again according to platform guidance.

## Protect the related accounts

From a known-good device, review the [Norva account security checklist](/blog/secure-media-player-account/), primary email, platform account, mobile carrier account where relevant, and the compatible media source you connected. Change credentials when risk justifies it and use unique credentials for separate services.

Review the [trusted-device list](/blog/review-trusted-device-list/) and remove or revoke the lost device when the current service provides that option. Protect [authorized-source credentials](/blog/protect-media-source-credentials/) separately; do not send them to a finder or include them in support messages.

## Understand what encryption does and does not do

Norva states that eligible offline media is encrypted and stored on the device, not uploaded to Norva. Encryption is a meaningful control, but its protection depends on implementation, device state, access controls, and the broader security environment. Do not treat it as a reason to delay locking the device, reviewing accounts, or issuing an erase when appropriate.

Also remember that progress and preferences can be account data distinct from the local media. Securing the account and securing the device are related but separate actions.

## Record remote-action uncertainty

Use precise statuses:

- **requested:** the command was submitted;
- **pending:** the service has not shown completion;
- **confirmed by platform:** the official service reports completion;
- **recovered:** the device is physically back and inspected;
- **unknown:** current evidence is insufficient.

Do not tell household members that local data is gone while the command remains pending. If the device later returns, follow platform guidance before reconnecting or trusting it.

## Restore viewing on another device carefully

Sign in only on a trusted, supported device. Account progress or preferences may synchronize, but encrypted local media should not be assumed to transfer. Reconnect the compatible authorized source and prepare eligible items again under current conditions.

Avoid rushing into broad account deletion solely because offline media existed on the lost device. Choose account actions based on the whole incident and current Norva support guidance.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Risking personal safety to retrieve the device.
- Assuming a remote command completed immediately.
- Securing Norva while ignoring email or platform accounts.
- Treating local encryption as a complete incident response.
- Sharing credentials with a finder or support channel.
- Assuming account sync restores local media elsewhere.
- Deleting unrelated accounts before assessing the incident.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can Norva erase the whole lost device?

Use the device maker's official lost-device tools for device-level actions. Consult Norva support for Norva-specific account questions.

### Is encrypted offline media safe on its own?

Encryption helps, but it is one control. Secure the device and connected accounts promptly and follow official incident steps.

### What if the remote erase remains pending?

Keep the device marked lost, maintain account protections, record the pending status, and follow current platform instructions. Do not describe the data as erased yet.
