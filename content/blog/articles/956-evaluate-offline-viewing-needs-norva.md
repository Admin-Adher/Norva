# How to Evaluate Your Offline-Viewing Needs for Norva

> **In short:** Start with a real disconnected scenario: where, for how long, on which supported device, and with which authorized media. Confirm that the device, source, and associated rights permit offline access, then check storage, preparation time, playback verification, and an acceptable fallback. Treat an essential unsupported scenario as a stop condition; one successful item does not prove that every title or screen will work offline.

Offline viewing sounds like one feature, but it is a chain of conditions. Norva supports offline access only when the device, source, and associated media rights allow it. The decision should begin with your actual disconnected periods, not a general desire to keep everything available everywhere.

## Describe real disconnected scenarios

List the next three situations in which connectivity may be unavailable, unreliable, expensive, or intentionally disabled. Record duration, device, likely viewing time, number of viewers, and how far in advance preparation can happen. Avoid precise travel locations in a shared worksheet.

Use the [frequent-travel evaluation](/blog/norva-for-frequent-travel/) when travel rules, network transitions, and device handoffs are central.

## Classify the requirement

Mark each scenario essential, valuable, or optional. “A child must have one known program during a long trip” is different from “it would be convenient to browse the whole catalog without a network.” A clear priority prevents an occasional convenience from outweighing source or privacy concerns.

Place the result in the [Norva evaluation framework](/blog/norva-evaluation-framework/) as a separate criterion.

## Confirm every condition independently

For each scenario, verify four questions through current official information and source terms:

1. Is the intended device supported?
2. Does the connected source permit the required offline action?
3. Do the rights associated with the chosen media permit it?
4. Does the current product flow expose the relevant option?

Do not infer a yes for one item, device, or source from another.

## Choose a known media sample

Select one short and one longer item whose source identity and rights are understood. Record title using a neutral code, approximate duration, required audio or subtitle track, and why the sample represents the scenario. Languages and subtitles still depend on the source and media.

If the expected option is missing, label the condition unknown or unsupported rather than repeatedly changing settings.

## Set a storage budget

Record free device space before the test and a minimum reserve you do not want to consume. Do not estimate file size from duration alone unless the actual device or source presents a verified value. After legitimate preparation, record the observed storage change.

The [mobile storage audit](/blog/mobile-media-storage-audit/) and [TV storage audit](/blog/tv-media-storage-audit/) address device-specific maintenance without promising offline support on either category.

## Test preparation and disconnected playback

Where all conditions permit it, prepare one sample through the current documented flow. Verify the intended track and play a short segment before disconnecting. Then use a controlled disconnected state, open the same profile, and test start, pause, seek, and clean exit. Reconnect through the normal method afterward.

Do not simulate disconnection during an account or source update. Keep credentials and private addresses out of notes.

## Consider availability over time

Source permissions, rights, account status, device storage, or application state may change. Do not promise indefinite offline access. Record any visible availability information and verify again before a critical trip. If the interface gives no clear answer, plan a fallback.

The [offline cleanup cadence](/blog/offline-storage-cleanup-cadence/) helps prevent old copies from silently consuming the storage budget.

## Define a fallback

Choose a lawful alternative for each essential scenario: reliable connectivity, different authorized media, another supported device, or no viewing. A fallback makes the decision resilient without pretending that every condition is controllable.

If no acceptable fallback exists, the offline requirement deserves a stricter pass threshold.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating offline access as universal downloading.
- Assuming one supported item proves the catalog.
- Ignoring device storage reserve.
- Failing to verify the required audio or subtitle track.
- Promising indefinite availability.
- Testing without an authorized source and rights basis.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can every Norva item be used offline?

Do not assume so. Offline access depends on the device, source, and associated rights for the media.

### Is a mobile success evidence for TV?

Only for the specific mobile scenario. Device support, storage, source behavior, and rights must be verified independently for another screen.

### How often should I retest before travel?

Verify close enough to the real scenario to catch changed rights, storage, or account conditions, while leaving time for a lawful fallback.
