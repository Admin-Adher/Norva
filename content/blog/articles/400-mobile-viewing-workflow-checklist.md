# A Mobile Viewing Workflow Checklist

> **In short:** Confirm supported software, the intended account and source, a known item, usable controls, sufficient battery, stable connectivity, readable display, private audio, required subtitles, and accessibility settings. Rehearse one interruption, protect the device in shared or public settings, and close with explicit sign-out when required. Diagnose failures one layer at a time.

Use this checklist for a real phone or tablet session, not as a claim that all mobile clients behave identically. Mark each line pass, fail, or not required. Stop when a failure affects authorization, privacy, required accessibility, device safety, or essential control.

For deeper rationale, begin with the [complete mobile viewing guide](/blog/mobile-viewing-workflow-guide/).

## Supported environment

- The app came from the official store or the site address is correct and documented.
- The operating system, device, region, and client meet current support guidance.
- The installed version is recorded after a recent update.
- New permission prompts were reviewed rather than accepted automatically.
- Organization or family policies remain intact.

After an update, use the [post-update regression check](/blog/review-mobile-app-after-update/) before a high-stakes session.

## Account, profile, and source

- The intended service account and profile are visible.
- The authorized connected source is correct.
- Content or age restrictions are understood and not bypassed.
- The exact title, version, season, and episode are confirmed.
- Shared-device activity will not change another person's data unexpectedly.
- A sign-out or handback plan exists when needed.

## Discovery and metadata

- The search query is correctly spelled and active filters are visible.
- Similar titles are distinguished by year, format, episode, or source.
- Runtime fits the available time.
- Availability is checked now rather than inferred from a past save.
- Required audio and subtitle labels are visible before commitment when offered.
- A shortlist has a stopping rule instead of growing indefinitely.

Use the [mobile search-and-save funnel](/blog/search-save-watch-later-mobile/) when discovery is the main task.

## Battery, temperature, and connection

- Starting charge, planned duration, reserve, and stop threshold are recorded.
- A compatible charger or power bank is available when required.
- Cables do not cross walkways or strain connectors.
- The device is not unusually hot and is not covered.
- Brightness is comfortable and leaves sensible battery headroom.
- The intended Wi-Fi or mobile connection is understood and stable enough for a sample.
- Power-saving behavior has been tested with the controls and accessibility settings needed.

## Display, orientation, and controls

- The device is stable at a comfortable distance and angle.
- Glare is reduced before brightness is raised.
- Essential metadata and controls are readable with current text settings.
- Portrait or landscape layout preserves required actions when rotation is used.
- Play, pause, resume, small seeks, stop, and return work on a short sample.
- Touch targets can be activated without frequent adjacent taps.
- Fullscreen or overlays can be exited reliably when offered.

## Audio and text tracks

- Volume starts low.
- The intended headphones, speaker, or hearing device is the active media output.
- Both channels or intentional mono output work.
- The selected audio track is verified by label and sample.
- Required subtitles or captions are present, synchronized, and readable across bright and dark scenes.
- Accessibility audio settings, balance, and accommodations remain intentional.
- A disconnect does not cause an uncontrolled speaker start.

W3C distinguishes captions from dialogue-only subtitles by including relevant non-speech information. Verify the actual labeled track; do not promise its availability from the title alone.

## Accessibility and comfort

- The viewer's real text, display, contrast, motion, and audio settings are active.
- Screen reader, voice, switch, keyboard, or other chosen input reaches the essential path.
- Focus and selected states are understandable.
- Controls remain usable after text enlargement or rotation.
- Timing, animation, or auto-advancing content does not block the task.
- A break and stop point are planned for a longer session.

Run the [needs-led mobile accessibility preflight](/blog/mobile-accessibility-preflight/) when any assistive configuration is essential.

## Interruption and recovery

- One ordinary lock, background, rotation, or call interruption has been tested safely.
- The return state clearly identifies the item and position.
- Audio output and track remain correct or can be restored without guesswork.
- Subtitles remain selected and readable or are deliberately reselected.
- Controls respond after return.
- Expected behavior is recorded without assuming cross-device or background continuity.

## Privacy and closure

- A strong device lock is active.
- Sensitive notification previews are limited appropriately.
- Screen position and headphone route fit the location.
- Credentials are not saved on a borrowed device.
- Temporary downloads or notes are reviewed.
- Playback is stopped, account details are closed, and sign-out is verified when required.
- The phone or tablet is locked and physically secured.

For travel or a café, add the [public-place privacy workflow](/blog/protect-mobile-session-in-public/).

## Evidence-led troubleshooting

If a check fails, record app and operating-system versions, device, time, item, source, exact action, expected result, observed result, and visible error text. Exclude passwords, tokens, account identifiers, and private notifications.

Retest one layer at a time: support and update, account and source, item, network, output, track, lifecycle, then accessibility. Avoid broad storage clearing, permission expansion, security disabling, or repeated reinstallations as first steps. Escalate concise evidence through current support.

## FAQ

### Must every checklist item pass?

Every item required for this session must pass. Mark irrelevant items not required; do not let optional features hide a failure in authorization, privacy, accessibility, or essential control.

### How long should the preflight take?

A familiar setup may take about a minute; a new device, update, audio route, accessibility configuration, or public location deserves a longer representative sample.

### What should I do if behavior differs from this checklist?

Follow current device and service documentation. Record the exact difference and context, avoid destructive changes, and contact support with non-sensitive evidence.
