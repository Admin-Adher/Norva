# How to Organize Favorite Series Without Saving Every Episode

> **In short:** Favorite the series for durable follow, rewatch, or discovery intent. Keep the next episode in progress or next-up, not as another favorite. Save an individual episode only when it has a distinct reference, replay, recommendation, or version-comparison purpose. Verify scope before removing redundant episode favorites.

A series can generate dozens of episode cards, but most do not represent dozens of different preferences. The hierarchy should carry the relationship while each state keeps one job.

## Build the series-anchor map

| State | Exact target | Purpose | Review trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Series favorite | Series | Durable preference/follow | Season or quarterly review |
| Progress | Episode and position | What happened | Playback update |
| Next-up | Next verified episode | Near-term commitment | Deadline/completion |
| Episode exception | Specific episode/version | Reference or replay | Purpose completed |
| Version note | Edit/source/tracks | Retrieval requirement | Source change |

The map exposes redundancy before any cleanup.

## Anchor durable interest at series level

Use one series favorite when the future action concerns the overall work: follow new seasons, revisit the series, or use it for discovery. EIDR’s public hierarchy distinguishes series, seasons, episodes, edits, and manifestations. That structure explains why the series is not simply the first visible episode.

Use [the series-or-episode decision guide](/blog/favorite-series-or-episode/) to verify target scope.

## Keep progress outside favorites

Progress identifies the last completed or in-progress episode for a viewer. It changes frequently and should not require favoriting every step. Likewise, [the long-term-versus-next-up model](/blog/long-term-favorites-vs-next-up/) gives the immediate next episode a short-lived commitment lane.

Do not remove progress while cleaning favorite cards.

## Define valid episode exceptions

An episode deserves a separate favorite when it supports a unique action:

- replay a standalone episode;
- reference a tutorial, performance, or interview;
- recommend one episode without the whole series;
- compare a specific edit or language version;
- preserve a meaningful series special.

Record series, season, number, title, and version. “Best episode” is a preference, but it still needs precise identity.

## Audit existing episode saves

For every episode favorite, ask:

1. Does it have an action different from the series favorite?
2. Is the exact episode and version confirmed?
3. Is it merely the next unwatched item?
4. Does another viewer own it?
5. Will removal affect only favorite state?

Keep, move, or remove one at a time. Use [the post-watch favorite review](/blog/review-favorites-after-watching/) when completion changed intent.

## Handle version needs

DCMI metadata terms distinguish identifier, relation, format, and language. Preserve these fields for a version-specific episode. If the product saves only a work-level anchor, keep a minimal private note rather than assuming the exact version will reopen.

## Test retrieval

After cleanup:

- open the series from favorites;
- find the current season and next episode;
- retrieve each retained episode exception;
- confirm progress and history remain;
- check another supported device if synchronization matters.

Norva can organize series hierarchy, variants, favorites, and progress from compatible sources, subject to current product behavior and source metadata.

## Keep the list readable

Display the series anchor once and expose episode exceptions as a separate count or collection where supported. If no such product feature exists, use a small external exception list. Do not invent custom grouping behavior.

## Define the exception exit rule

Every episode exception needs an ending condition. Remove its separate favorite after the planned replay, reference, recommendation, or comparison is complete unless a new durable purpose replaces it. Verify the exact episode and viewer first, then confirm that the series anchor and progress remain available. This prevents a valid exception from quietly becoming the same episode-level clutter the hierarchy was designed to avoid.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Favoriting each episode to track progress.
- Saving the open episode when intent concerns the series.
- Removing another viewer’s episode preference.
- Losing a version-specific exception.
- Treating a series favorite as a next-up queue.
- Bulk-removing without a retrieval test.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can a pilot stay favorited separately?

Yes, if it has a durable standalone purpose. Sampling progress alone is not enough.

### What about anthology series?

More episode exceptions may be useful because entries stand alone, but each still needs a future action.

### Should a completed series remain the anchor?

Keep it when durable preference remains; otherwise review and remove it without erasing completion history.
