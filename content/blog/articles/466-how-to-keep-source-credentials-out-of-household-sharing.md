# How to Keep Source Credentials Out of Household Sharing

> **In short:** Share the approved viewing outcome, not the source password, token, or private URL. Assign one authorised source steward to enter credentials privately through verified controls, and give household members a request path for connection changes. Keep secrets out of chats, agreements, screenshots, and device inventories. If exposure occurs, preserve the context, use official source recovery controls, and review affected devices.

Norva organises a compatible media source the user owns or is authorised to use. Household convenience does not change the source's terms or the need to protect access credentials.

## Separate access from secret knowledge

A household member may need to browse authorised media without needing the underlying source credential. State the desired outcome—“connect the approved household source on this trusted device”—instead of forwarding secret values.

The [source-responsibility guide](/blog/how-to-assign-source-connection-responsibilities-at-home/) defines a source steward who handles those outcomes.

## Name the credential custodian

Record the steward and source owner in a secret-free document. Do not record the password, token, recovery code, private endpoint, or payment details.

The steward should follow source-provider terms and current recovery guidance. This household role does not create a Norva administrator permission.

## Enter credentials privately

Before connection:

- verify the Norva route and source prompt;
- move away from shared-screen viewers;
- close screen recording and unrelated notifications;
- avoid copying the secret through a shared clipboard;
- confirm the trusted device and intended account;
- clear temporary exposure according to official device controls.

Do not type credentials into unexpected links or messages.

## Keep shared records secret-free

Review household chats, agreements, troubleshooting notes, screenshots, and device inventories. Replace secret values with neutral labels such as “Source A — steward notified.”

Do not preserve a redacted screenshot when metadata around the redaction still reveals a private URL or account name.

## Create a request process

Household members should request:

- connect or reconnect an approved source;
- review a source outage;
- remove access from a device;
- approve a new source;
- update source ownership after a household change.

The steward replies with status and impact, not credentials.

## Approve new connections

Use [the new-source approval workflow](/blog/how-to-approve-a-new-source-before-it-is-connected/) to verify ownership, authorisation, compatibility evidence, affected profiles, and rollback before any secret is entered.

## Respond to exposure

If a credential appears in a chat, screenshot, or unknown device:

1. stop further sharing;
2. record where and when exposure occurred without copying the secret;
3. notify the source owner and account coordinator;
4. follow the source provider's official recovery or credential-change process;
5. review trusted devices and active connections through verified controls;
6. remove exposed copies where authorised;
7. contact Norva support when the Norva connection state is unclear.

Do not announce the secret again while explaining the incident.

## Include offboarding

When a steward or member leaves, remove their organisational role, review devices, and follow official source and account controls. Do not assume deleting a Norva profile revokes source credentials elsewhere.

The [shared governance guide](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) separates account, source, profile, and device layers.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid “temporary” passwords in group chats, secrets in screenshots, one shared note for all credentials, and granting broad access because troubleshooting feels urgent.

This process reduces household exposure but cannot guarantee security, control the source provider, or replace its current terms and recovery instructions.

## Audit the shared note itself

Search the household procedure for passwords, tokens, recovery codes, source URLs, screenshots, and autofill exports before distributing it. Replace secrets with the custodian's contact route and the approved request process.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should every adult know the source password?

No. Give people the approved access outcome and request path appropriate to the source terms.

### Can credentials be stored in the household agreement?

No. The agreement should name roles and processes only.

### What if the source owner is unavailable?

Use the source provider's legitimate recovery and ownership process. Do not bypass access controls.
