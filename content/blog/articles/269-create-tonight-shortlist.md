# How to Create a Temporary Shortlist for Tonight

> **In short:** Set the viewing window and decision deadline first. Gather each participant’s hard constraints, cap the shortlist, verify exact versions and current authorized availability, then vote once with a declared tie-breaker. Start the winner and expire the shortlist afterward. Do not convert every candidate into a permanent favorite.

Tonight’s list solves one decision under time pressure. Its success measure is not how many attractive cards it contains; it is how quickly the group reaches a workable choice without losing the viewing window.

Reserve a short decision window inside the evening rather than using all available time for browsing.

## Gather hard constraints before titles

Ask each participant for requirements, not a long list of suggestions:

- latest acceptable finish;
- media type or episode commitment;
- language and subtitle needs;
- accessibility needs;
- explicit exclusions;
- willingness to rewatch.

Keep personal explanations optional. The group needs the constraint, not private justification.

## Cap proposals

Give each person one proposal or set a small total cap. Candidates can come from personal favorites, a shared list, or current discovery. If profile separation exists, verify its scope with [the personal-favorites test](/blog/separate-favorites-by-profile/); do not assume every person sees the same list.

## Verify every candidate

Record exact work and version, runtime, required tracks, connected source, and current availability. DCMI metadata terms distinguish format, language, type, and identifier—fields that matter to tonight’s constraints.

Remove or mark unknown any candidate that cannot be verified before the deadline. Do not spend the entire decision window troubleshooting one card.

## Use one selection rule

Options include:

- unanimous consent;
- ranked top two, then final vote;
- rotating chooser;
- random choice among candidates that pass every constraint.

Declare the tie-breaker before votes are visible. The method should be simple enough to complete once.

## Keep favorite and shortlist actions distinct

A candidate can enter tonight’s shortlist without becoming a long-term favorite. A durable favorite can lose tonight’s vote without being removed. W3C label guidance supports action names such as “Add to tonight” and “Add to favorites” rather than two identical plus icons.

For a reusable occasion, use [the themed-shortlist brief](/blog/build-themed-favorites-shortlist/). For near-term individual intent beyond tonight, use [the next-up separation workflow](/blog/long-term-favorites-vs-next-up/).

## Choose a fallback

The fallback should already pass all hard constraints and be verified available. Use it if the winner fails at playback or a last-minute household need changes. Avoid reopening the full catalogue.

Test the fallback on the intended device before voting closes, including runtime, required audio or subtitle track, and source access. A fallback that still needs troubleshooting is another candidate, not a contingency.

Norva organizes media from compatible sources users own or are authorized to access. Availability and tracks depend on the connected source; verify them on the intended device.

## Expire and record one outcome

At the end of the evening:

- record selected and started;
- note any unavailable or wrong-version candidate;
- clear temporary candidates;
- leave durable favorites unchanged;
- review only titles whose future intent genuinely changed.

The shortlist should not survive by default. A new evening gets new constraints.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Browsing before setting the end time.
- Letting everyone propose many titles.
- Voting before checking runtime and tracks.
- Treating every candidate as a favorite.
- Choosing a tie-break after seeing votes.
- Keeping tonight’s list permanently.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many candidates should the list contain?

Use the smallest cap that gives everyone a credible option. Four to six may work for many groups, but the method does not require a universal number.

### What if no candidate passes every constraint?

Use the pre-agreed fallback activity or relax a constraint only with the affected participant’s consent.

### Should the winner enter favorites afterward?

Only if it gains a durable future action independent of tonight’s selection.
