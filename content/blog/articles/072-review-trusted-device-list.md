# Why You Should Review Your Trusted-Device List Regularly

> **In short:** A trusted-device review catches televisions, phones, and browsers that were sold, lost, borrowed, or simply forgotten. Review after any device change and on a cadence your household can maintain. Keep only devices you recognise, investigate uncertainty before deleting evidence, and secure the account if an unfamiliar device suggests unauthorised access.

Norva’s Privacy Policy confirms that trusted-device records and device tokens help phones and televisions share one account and synchronise playback. A useful device list should reflect the equipment that still belongs in your current account context.

## Review by event, not only by calendar

“Regularly” does not require an invented universal interval. Trigger a review when:

- a phone, television, computer, or streaming device is sold or recycled;
- a device is lost or stolen;
- you sign in on temporary accommodation or borrowed hardware;
- a household member moves in or out;
- a repair centre or buyer will receive the device;
- you approve a new pairing request;
- account activity looks unfamiliar;
- you change the recovery email or password after a security concern.

Add a calendar reminder at a frequency proportionate to how often your household changes devices. A stable one-device setup and a frequently travelling household do not need identical schedules.

## Classify before removing

### Keep

The device is recognised, still controlled by the household, and has a current purpose.

### Investigate

The label is ambiguous or the time looks unexpected. Ask household members and compare the approximate pairing or sign-in event before assuming an intrusion.

### Sign out or remove

The device is no longer controlled, no longer used, or was temporary. Use the current official account control where available.

### Transfer securely

If ownership changes, sign out, remove local offline data through supported controls, and follow the manufacturer’s secure erase process.

The [account-security checklist](https://norva.tv/blog/secure-media-player-account/) covers the wider credential and update steps.

## Handle an unfamiliar device calmly

1. Record its displayed label and any non-sensitive timestamp.
2. Ask whether another household member paired it.
3. Remove or sign it out through official controls where available.
4. Change the account password if unauthorised access is plausible.
5. Secure the recovery email.
6. Contact support with the device context and approximate time.

Do not share screenshots containing device tokens, pairing codes, account email, or source credentials. NIST guidance emphasises protected authentication channels and reauthentication after risk events; the service’s available controls determine the exact response.

## Close the loop after pairing

Every approved television pairing should produce an intentional device relationship. Follow the [TV pairing-code checklist](https://norva.tv/blog/tv-pairing-code-security/) at the time of approval, then confirm that the resulting record is recognisable.

If you cannot distinguish two similar televisions, use neutral location-based labels where the product permits, such as “Living room TV,” without including an address or person’s sensitive details.

## Understand what the record represents

A trusted-device record helps the service recognise account relationships across screens. It is not proof that a device is physically safe, fully updated, or still in the owner’s possession. A familiar label can become stale after a sale or reset.

The explainer on [data used by cross-device media players](https://norva.tv/blog/cross-device-media-player-data/) maps device records to their stated purpose.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Keeping a temporary accommodation television trusted indefinitely.
- Removing an unfamiliar label before recording enough context to investigate.
- Treating a recognised name as proof of current ownership.
- Including personal or location details in a device label.
- Claiming a fixed review interval is right for every household.
- Giving navigation instructions that have not been verified in the current product.

The exact location and wording of trusted-device controls must be checked before publication. If a required control is unavailable, contact Norva Support rather than inventing a route.

## Frequently asked questions

### How often should I review devices?

Review after every material device or household change, then choose a recurring interval that matches your use. The public documentation does not prescribe one universal schedule.

### Should I remove a device I do not immediately recognise?

Record its non-sensitive details and ask other account users first. If it remains unfamiliar, use official removal controls, secure credentials, and contact support.

### Is deleting a profile enough to remove a device?

Do not assume so. Profiles organise viewing context; trusted-device relationships are an account-level security concern.

### What if I sold a device without signing out?

Review trusted-device controls immediately, secure the account, and follow support guidance. Also use the device maker’s remote-management options if they were previously enabled.
