# What to Do When a Signed-In TV Device Is Stolen

> **In short:** Protect people first; do not pursue the television. Preserve model, serial reference, device label, last known time, ownership evidence, and the authority or insurer case. From a trusted device, review Norva access, secure recovery, and handle every compatible authorized source and platform account independently. Change exposed or reused credentials, review paired controllers and networks, and mark removal or sign-out actions pending until confirmed.

A stolen television differs from a lost phone: it may lack a general-purpose remote locate service, but it can still retain Norva, source applications, platform accounts, pairing relationships, local storage, and a recognizable device label.

## Handle safety and physical evidence

Do not enter a dangerous location or confront anyone. Contact local authorities, building management, insurer, retailer, or logistics provider as appropriate. Preserve receipts, photographs, model, serial reference, neutral device label, and last known time.

Do not post serial numbers, account screenshots, or exact home details publicly. Store the authority or claim reference in the incident log without unnecessary personal data.

## Identify the exact television boundary

Use the household inventory and [smart-TV setup card](/blog/secure-smart-tv-before-sign-in/) to distinguish the stolen television from similar displays. Record operating platform, Norva installation route, platform account, directly installed source applications, attached storage, casting relationships, and paired phones or remotes.

A generic name such as “Living Room TV” may appear on several dashboards. Match model, setup date, and last activity where official controls provide them.

## Review Norva through official support

Open Norva from a known trusted device. Use current account, device, or session controls where documented, or contact official support. Request removal or sign-out for the identified television and preserve the case result.

Do not assume that a password change automatically closes the stolen television's session. If a credential was exposed or reused, create a unique replacement and perform the [post-password session review](/blog/post-password-change-session-review/).

## Secure recovery and platform accounts

Review the recovery email, its sessions and devices, forwarding, filters, and recovery methods. Inspect the television platform account for known and unknown devices, sign-ins, payment access, or remote controls using its current official guidance.

Remove the stolen device only after understanding whether that action affects any platform protection, evidence, or recovery ability. Television capabilities vary widely.

## Handle authorized sources independently

List each compatible source used through Norva and each source application installed directly on the television. Review devices or sessions at each source's official destination and request removal where available.

Norva does not supply the household's media and does not control source credentials. A Norva action is not evidence that a source session ended. Never paste source passwords into a Norva support ticket.

## Review pairing, casting, and network context

Remove or reclassify the stolen television from trusted casting lists, home-control systems, phone remotes, voice assistants, and router device labels where current controls support it. Change a household network credential if the television or attached note exposed it, not merely as an automatic ritual.

Check whether attached cameras, microphones, USB storage, or external boxes were also taken and create separate rows for their accounts and local data.

## Assess local media and data

Apply the [local-media loss guide](/blog/local-media-response-lost-device/). Eligible Norva offline media may be encrypted locally when device, source, media, and rights allow, but television screenshots, browser data, source downloads, and external storage may follow different rules.

Do not claim a remote wipe unless the exact platform provides one and confirms completion.

## Close only confirmed boundaries

Run the [viewing-device audit](/blog/viewing-device-security-audit/) against replacement hardware before restoring accounts. Update the inventory so the stolen label cannot be mistaken for the new screen.

Close the incident when safety and reporting are addressed, Norva and sources have known states, recovery is secure, paired systems are reviewed, and any local-data limitations are documented.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Pursuing the stolen device personally.
- Posting serial or account details publicly.
- Removing the wrong television with a similar label.
- Assuming a password change ends every session.
- Reviewing Norva but not direct source applications.
- Claiming remote erase without platform confirmation.
- Reusing the stolen device label for its replacement.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I change my Norva password immediately?

Change it if exposure or reuse is plausible; still review the stolen device or session because password-change behavior is service-specific.

### Can Norva remove my source accounts from the television?

Treat each source independently through its official controls. Do not assume a Norva action removes direct source access.

### Is a television factory reset possible remotely?

Capabilities vary. Use only manufacturer-documented controls and treat a command as pending until the platform confirms it.
