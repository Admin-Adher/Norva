# A Secure Disposal Checklist for a Viewing Device

> **In short:** Before sale, donation, recycling, or disposal, inventory the device and storage, back up authorized data, and remove Norva, platform, recovery, browser, password-manager, and source access separately. Follow the manufacturer's ownership-transfer and erase instructions, choose appropriate sanitization or qualified destruction for removable or failed media, verify the resulting setup or destruction state, and document custody. Never transfer a device based only on a reset request.

“Disposal” includes sale, trade-in, donation, return, recycling, parts recovery, and discarding a failed device. Each path transfers physical custody, so account cleanup and data sanitization must finish before the handoff whenever possible.

## Inventory the device and storage

Record manufacturer, model, serial reference, owner, operating platform, internal storage, memory cards, USB drives, external boxes, and paired accessories. Classify data without listing values: Norva, source applications, browser sessions, email, password manager, screenshots, downloads, personal media, and backups.

Use the [local-media loss guide](/blog/local-media-response-lost-device/) to distinguish eligible encrypted offline media from other local files. Do not assume one application's protection covers the whole device.

## Decide what to retain

Back up data the owner needs and is authorized to keep under current source, rights, privacy, and platform rules. Verify the backup before erasing. Do not keep bulk exports or screenshots merely because storage is available.

If a device belongs to work, school, or another organization, follow its disposal process and obtain authorization before removing management or data.

## Remove accounts and relationships

Sign out of Norva using current controls and review the device or session from a trusted account where available. Remove each compatible authorized source independently. Review platform account, browser synchronization, password manager, recovery email, payment, casting, voice assistant, and home-control relationships.

An app uninstall is not an account deletion, and a Norva sign-out does not remove direct source sessions. Record each boundary separately.

## Follow exact ownership-transfer instructions

Use the manufacturer’s current sell, give-away, trade-in, reset, or recycle documentation for the exact model and software version. Understand activation locks, account protection, eSIM or SIM, paired accessories, and removable media before proceeding.

The [factory-reset verification guide](/blog/verify-factory-reset-before-sale/) provides a two-person handoff test. Do not improvise with service-menu codes or third-party “cleaning” tools.

## Choose sanitization for the storage medium

A supported device reset may be the manufacturer-approved method for an intact consumer device. Failed drives, removable storage, or higher-sensitivity data may require another clear, purge, or destroy approach determined by storage technology and risk.

NIST SP 800-88 Rev. 2 provides a media-sanitization framework. Individuals should still use manufacturer guidance or a qualified service; physically damaging lithium-battery devices or storage without proper expertise can cause injury and environmental harm.

## Verify, do not merely initiate

Restart the device and confirm it reaches the expected new-owner setup state without showing the former account, profiles, media history, networks, or files. Do not complete setup with personal credentials merely to test it.

For a failed device that cannot be verified, document the limitation and choose a recycler or destruction service appropriate to the storage and sensitivity. A broken screen is not proof that data is inaccessible.

## Transfer custody responsibly

Record recipient or certified recycler, date, receipt, device identifiers, sanitization evidence, and unresolved exception. Remove household inventory labels and update trusted-device records.

Never include passwords, recovery codes, live reset links, or unredacted evidence in the handoff certificate. Use the [pre-repair access guide](/blog/remove-media-access-before-device-repair/) when a third party must access the device before final disposal.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating app deletion as account removal.
- Leaving a memory card or attached storage behind.
- Resetting without understanding activation protection.
- Assuming a broken device contains no accessible data.
- Using unsafe physical destruction methods.
- Entering personal credentials after reset to “test” it.
- Handing over the device before verification.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a factory reset always enough?

Use the exact manufacturer guidance and consider storage type and data sensitivity; failed or removable media may require a different sanitization method.

### Should I delete cloud data before disposal?

Device retirement and cloud-account deletion are separate decisions. Remove device access without deleting synchronized data you still need.

### What if the device will not turn on?

Do not assume data is gone. Use a qualified recycler or sanitization service and document the inability to verify an on-device reset.
