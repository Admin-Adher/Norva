# Long-Term Favorites or Next-Up List: Separate the Intent

> **In short:** Favorites answer “what do I want to retain as a preference or future reference?” Next-up answers “what do I intend to watch soon?” Give each lane its own admission and exit rule. A title can belong to both only when two distinct reasons exist; completing it should normally resolve next-up, while favorite status gets a separate review.

One list cannot prioritize tonight and preserve years of taste equally well. Mixing the intents makes old favorites look urgent and near-term candidates disappear into history.

## Use the two-lane state model

| Field | Long-term favorites | Next-up |
|---|---|---|
| Core intent | Durable preference or retrieval | Near-term viewing commitment |
| Horizon | Months or open-ended | Days or a defined near period |
| Priority | Usually unranked or lightly grouped | Ordered or date-bound |
| Admission | Exact identity plus durable future action | Exact identity plus realistic viewing window |
| Completion response | Review, not automatic removal | Mark resolved or remove |
| Expiry | Audit trigger | Short deadline |
| Unavailable item | Preserve or investigate | Replan promptly |

This is a conceptual model; verify which list types the current product actually exposes.

## Admit to favorites

Use [the favorite admission card](/blog/decide-what-to-favorite/). A durable action can be rewatch, follow, recommend, compare, or preserve as a reference. “Watch this weekend” alone belongs in next-up.

## Admit to next-up

Require:

- exact work or episode;
- viewer or household owner;
- intended time window;
- current authorized availability;
- required version and tracks;
- an expiry or replanning date.

Do not add every interesting title. Next-up is a commitment queue, not a popularity ranking.

## Allow dual membership deliberately

A beloved series can remain a favorite while its next unwatched episode enters next-up. Record two reasons:

- Favorite: “retain for rewatch and discovery.”
- Next-up: “watch S2E4 by Sunday.”

Removing the next-up item after completion should not unfavorite the series. Likewise, unfavoriting should not erase an active episode commitment without an explicit decision.

## Define transitions

### Candidate → next-up

The viewer chooses a realistic time and verifies availability.

### Next-up → completed

Playback reaches the defined boundary for the correct viewer and version.

### Next-up → deferred

The time window expires or availability changes; move it to a dated waiting state rather than leaving false urgency.

### Favorite → next-up

A durable preference gains a concrete viewing plan.

### Next-up → favorite

After viewing, the title gains a durable future purpose. Do not promote automatically.

Use [the post-watch review](/blog/review-favorites-after-watching/) to decide.

## Keep tonight even shorter-lived

[The tonight shortlist](/blog/create-tonight-shortlist/) is a candidate set before one selection. Only the winner or a deliberate runner-up should enter next-up. The rejected candidates should expire unless they have another purpose.

## Expose state clearly

W3C label guidance supports controls that state both action and target list: “Save to favorites,” “Add to next-up,” and “Remove from tonight.” One generic heart cannot communicate all three.

DCMI metadata distinctions for identifier, type, relation, format, and language help ensure the same exact entity moves between lanes.

## Review on different clocks

Review next-up weekly or when its commitment window ends. Review durable favorites on a slower cadence such as quarterly. Do not purge favorites because next-up is overdue; repair the lane that owns the problem.

Norva may retain favorites and progress across supported devices under the same account. Exact next-up controls and synchronization should be verified rather than assumed.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating every favorite as urgent.
- Filling next-up with unbounded curiosity.
- Removing favorite state at completion automatically.
- Allowing dual membership with one vague reason.
- Giving both lanes the same review cadence.
- Assuming list controls exist without verification.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can an unwatched title be a long-term favorite?

It can have durable follow or reference intent, but distinguish that from preference based on viewing.

### What if next-up becomes unavailable?

Replan promptly: verify another authorized version, defer with a date, or remove the commitment while preserving durable favorite intent.

### Should next-up be strictly ranked?

Only if ranking helps decisions. A small dated queue can work without a total order.
