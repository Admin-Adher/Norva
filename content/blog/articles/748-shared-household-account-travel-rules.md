# Set Travel Rules for a Shared Household Account

> **In short:** Give every traveller a named profile, supported device, viewing batch, connectivity rule, and cleanup owner. Keep account and authorized-source credentials with the permitted account owner, follow current Norva and source terms, and never invent simultaneous-use or device limits. Record offline sessions by profile, avoid conflicting progress on several disconnected devices, and designate the first device that will reconnect after travel.

Shared travel viewing fails when responsibility is shared vaguely. One person prepares items, another removes them, two viewers use the same profile, and nobody knows which device should reconnect first. A household charter makes those decisions explicit.

## Start with the current terms and permissions

Review Norva's current terms, privacy information, and support guidance, along with the terms of the compatible media source. Norva is a software player and organizer; users connect a source they own or are authorized to use.

Do not create household rules that assume unlimited devices, simultaneous sessions, permanent access, or permissions not stated in current documentation. If a limit or right matters, verify it from the relevant service rather than guessing.

## Assign one profile to each viewer

Use the intended viewer's profile consistently where available. Record display name, device, planned sessions, and any age-appropriate or accessibility requirements without storing sensitive personal data.

The [profile-based offline plan](/blog/plan-offline-viewing-by-profile/) helps separate viewing choices and progress. Avoid a generic travel profile if it makes ownership or history harder to interpret.

## Give every device an owner and role

Assign primary, shared, essential-use, or fallback roles. The owner is responsible for screen lock, charging, storage reserve, offline verification, physical custody, and return-trip cleanup.

For a device used by several people, follow the [shared-device offline plan](/blog/shared-device-offline-viewing-plan/). Record the session order and require a handoff check so one viewer does not remove another's item or change the wrong profile.

## Keep credentials within permitted boundaries

Do not write account passwords, recovery codes, or authorized-source credentials on a travel worksheet or share them casually among travellers. Use supported sign-in methods, unique credentials, and the account owner's approved recovery process.

If a person needs access beyond the current household rules, stop and consult the relevant terms or support. Convenience does not create authorization.

## Define offline and data responsibilities

For each batch, name who confirms eligibility, selects audio and subtitles, tests without connectivity, monitors storage, and removes finished items. Current offline use depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

Also decide which person may enable roaming, join hotel Wi-Fi, or start a large transfer. Shared devices should not change network policy without notifying the owner.

## Prevent progress conflicts

Avoid playing the same item under one profile on several disconnected devices unless the household has a written reason and conflict plan. Record starting position, device, local date and time, and intended outcome.

Norva can synchronize progress, history, favorites, and preferences across supported devices, while eligible offline media remains device-local. Use the [first-reconnection routine](/blog/plan-first-reconnection-after-offline/) to bring one device online, confirm state, and then continue in a known order.

## Plan exceptions and incidents

Write who acts if a device is lost, a required item fails, a traveller changes plans, or an account prompt appears. The response should use official platform tools and Norva support without exposing credentials in a public place.

At trip end, review trusted devices, reconnect in sequence, confirm progress, and remove expired local batches through the app.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Assuming household access overrides current service terms.
- Letting several viewers use one profile without a progress plan.
- Writing credentials into the travel worksheet.
- Leaving a shared device without an owner.
- Inventing device or simultaneous-use limits.
- Reconnecting every device at once.
- Removing another person's local batch without confirmation.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can everyone in the household share the same credentials?

Follow current Norva and source terms and supported account methods. Do not broaden access or expose credentials merely because travel makes it convenient.

### Should each traveller have the same offline items?

No. Allocate by viewer, journey, storage, battery, tracks, and fallback value. Verify each local copy independently.

### Who reconnects first after the trip?

Choose the device with the authoritative progress record before departure or before the first connection. Confirm its state, then reconnect the others in order.
