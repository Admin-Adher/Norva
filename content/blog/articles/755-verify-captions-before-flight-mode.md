# Verify Captions Before Enabling Flight Mode

> **In short:** Confirm the intended profile, exact item and version, caption or subtitle label, language, and enabled state while still on trusted connectivity. Complete eligible offline preparation, then disable Wi-Fi and mobile data and test dialogue, speaker changes, non-speech cues, timing, and readability at several positions. Record the viewer's approval and keep a verified alternate item because a connected caption preview does not prove offline availability.

Captions can be essential for access, noisy cabins, sleeping companions, or headphones that cannot be used. A visible caption menu is only the start; the actual track must appear in the prepared item under disconnected conditions.

## Define what the viewer needs

Write the intended outcome in plain language. Does the viewer need translated dialogue, same-language dialogue, speaker identification, or non-speech information? W3C distinguishes captions from subtitles and other media-accessibility modes because they do not necessarily provide the same information.

Do not approve a track from a language label alone. "English" may describe different forms of text depending on the item and interface.

## Confirm the exact item context

Open the intended Norva account and profile. Record the compatible source, item, visible version or edition, caption label, subtitle label, and any display setting the viewer relies on.

Use the [subtitle preparation guide](/blog/prepare-subtitles-before-offline/) before completing eligible offline preparation. Current availability depends on the exact device, compatible source, media, and associated rights.

## Test with connectivity actually unavailable

After preparation completes, use the platform's current flight or airplane-mode controls and verify that the relevant external connections are unavailable. Apple notes that Wi-Fi and Bluetooth can be managed while Airplane Mode is active, so the icon alone does not prove a disconnected test.

Sample at least three scenes:

- ordinary dialogue with more than one speaker;
- a quiet or non-speech moment where cues matter;
- a later position that proves the track is not limited to the opening.

Pause, seek, and resume. Confirm captions remain enabled and synchronized enough for the intended viewer. Record the observed result instead of promising perfection for the entire item.

## Check readability in the travel setup

Use the actual phone or tablet, expected orientation, stand, seating distance, brightness, and corrective aids. The guide to [making captions easier to read](/blog/make-captions-easier-to-read/) covers text size, contrast, background, and positioning where current platform and app controls allow them.

Avoid changing several accessibility settings after the offline test without retesting. A setting that improves one item may obscure another interface element or affect battery use.

## Coordinate audio and fallback access

Captions can complement audio but may not replace descriptive audio or another required access mode. The [accessible travel audio plan](/blog/pack-accessible-audio-for-travel/) checks the full outcome with headphones, adapters, and power.

Prepare one fallback item whose caption track has independently passed. A fallback that relies on the same unverified version or network connection does not reduce the risk.

## Recheck at the airport without changing the plan

Perform a short confirmation before leaving the trusted network or at a calm pre-departure point. Do not start a large replacement transfer at the gate unless eligibility, time, power, storage, and network conditions are understood.

If the required captions fail, revise the session rather than treating visible artwork as readiness.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Treating a language label as proof of caption content.
- Testing while mobile data remains active.
- Sampling only the opening scene.
- Ignoring non-speech cues and speaker changes.
- Changing readability settings without retesting.
- Assuming captions replace descriptive audio.
- Keeping an unverified fallback.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are captions and subtitles the same?

Not necessarily. They can serve different needs and include different information. Verify the exact label, behavior, and viewer outcome.

### Does Airplane Mode guarantee an offline test?

No. Confirm the relevant connections are unavailable because platform controls may allow Wi-Fi or Bluetooth to remain active.

### What if captions work while connected but not offline?

Do not approve the item. Check current eligibility and track selection, prepare another legitimate version when available, or use a verified fallback.
