# Who Should Manage the Shared Media Account?

> **In short:** Choose one willing account coordinator who understands the current plan, protects credentials, respects profile owners, documents major changes, and can contact support. The role should follow the actual account holder and public terms, not household seniority or technical confidence alone. A backup can know the process without receiving secrets through an unsafe channel.

“Manager” here is a household responsibility. It does not claim that Norva provides multiple administrator roles or delegated permissions.

The household should also define what the coordinator must never do alone: inspect unrelated profile history, disclose credentials, remove an authorised source, or delete shared state without the required owner or household approval.

## Define the coordinator's work

The coordinator should:

- keep account contact and plan information current;
- review official terms, privacy, and pricing pages;
- coordinate subscription and support questions;
- protect authentication and recovery information;
- approve or route major shared changes;
- maintain the secret-free household agreement;
- start onboarding and offboarding routines;
- avoid inspecting private profile history unnecessarily.

Profile owners still decide their own favourites and preferences.

## Choose by practical criteria

A good candidate is willing, reachable, careful with private information, comfortable following documented steps, and able to explain changes. Technical expertise is useful only when paired with restraint.

Do not choose someone who wants broad access but refuses documentation or consent.

## Respect the actual account holder

Confirm who legally and operationally holds the account and payment relationship under current Norva terms. A household label cannot transfer contractual responsibility.

Sensitive legal, billing, or ownership disputes require appropriate advice, not a blog checklist.

## Separate account and source roles

The account coordinator need not know or handle every source credential. A source steward can verify authorisation and changes while the account coordinator tracks the outcome.

Use [the credential-boundary guide](/blog/how-to-keep-source-credentials-out-of-household-sharing/) to avoid circulating secrets.

## Plan a backup process

A backup should know:

- where official documentation is;
- how to contact Norva support;
- where the agreement and device inventory live;
- who owns each source and profile;
- how to begin offboarding.

Do not put passwords or recovery codes in the agreement. Use appropriate secure recovery and succession methods consistent with the account provider and household circumstances.

Run a tabletop handover using only process information. The backup should be able to find official support, identify source and device custodians, preserve an incident state, and pause a risky change without knowing any secret value.

## Document decision rights

Write which actions the coordinator can take alone, which require profile-owner notice, and which require household approval. Removing a source or profile is different from fixing a personal favourite.

The [one-page agreement](/blog/create-a-one-page-household-media-agreement/) captures these thresholds. The [governance pillar](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) maps all roles.

## Review the role after change

Revisit the coordinator when the account holder, payment method, household composition, source ownership, or availability changes. Do not rotate responsibility solely for symmetry.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid sharing credentials with everyone, confusing coordinator with product administrator, centralising profile decisions, and selecting someone without consent.

This framework cannot grant account rights, resolve ownership disputes, or replace current terms and support guidance.

## Test the backup path once

The backup coordinator should be able to locate official recovery guidance, contact the account holder, and identify source stewards without possessing every credential. Run a harmless tabletop exercise and record any missing decision right before an urgent incident exposes it.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should the most technical person manage the account?

Only if they are also willing, privacy-conscious, reliable, and aligned with the actual account relationship.

### Can two people be co-managers?

Household coordination can be shared, but do not assume Norva exposes co-administrator permissions. Define responsibilities and credential handling carefully.

### Should the manager see every profile's history?

No. Access only the minimum information needed for a specific account task.
