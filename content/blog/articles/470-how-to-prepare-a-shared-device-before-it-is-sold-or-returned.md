# How to Prepare a Shared Device Before It Is Sold or Returned

> **In short:** Confirm ownership and the handover deadline, preserve only data the owner is authorised to keep, sign out of Norva and connected account contexts, remove eligible local media, review browser and saved-credential state, then follow the device manufacturer's current erase or factory-reset instructions. Verify the device no longer exposes the household account and remove it from the trusted-device inventory.

Selling, returning, repairing, or transferring a device changes custody. Closing the Norva app is not enough when the operating system, browser, or source access retains a session.

## Confirm authority and destination

Identify the device owner, next custodian, and whether it is being sold, returned, repaired, or reassigned. Obtain consent before erasing a shared or borrowed device.

Check contractual return instructions and manufacturer guidance. This article is not legal or hardware-specific advice.

## Preserve authorised owner data

Ask each owner to back up personal files through their verified process. Do not copy another person's media, history, messages, or credentials.

Record what was preserved and who approved deletion. A full-device reset can be irreversible.

## Inventory shared account state

Check:

- Norva sign-in;
- compatible-browser sessions;
- profile context;
- connected source access;
- saved credentials;
- eligible local media;
- notifications and account emails;
- payment or store account state managed by the device platform.

Do not record secret values in the checklist.

## Sign out through verified controls

Use Norva's visible sign-out path and current account controls. Close browser sessions and remove saved access according to official browser and device instructions.

Do not assume profile switching signs out the account. If sign-out remains unclear, contact Norva support before the handover.

## Remove eligible local media

Norva states that eligible offline items are encrypted and stored locally rather than uploaded to Norva. Remove them through documented app controls before reset when authorised.

Do not browse private app folders or manually delete unknown system files.

## Handle source credentials

If source access was saved, follow the source provider's official process to remove or revoke the device where legitimately available. Do not copy credentials to a replacement through an insecure note.

The [account offboarding routine](/blog/an-account-offboarding-routine-when-someone-moves-out/) separates source, account, profile, and device layers.

## Follow manufacturer reset instructions

Only after authorised data and account state are handled, use the manufacturer's current instructions for erase, factory reset, activation locks, and ownership transfer. Exact controls vary by device and update.

Do not claim that a reset is complete until the device displays the manufacturer's expected setup or handover state.

## Verify and close the inventory

Check that the Norva account is not visible after the authorised reset, remove the device from [the trusted-device inventory](/blog/build-a-trusted-device-inventory-for-the-household/), and record the new custodian or disposal status.

The [governance guide](/blog/the-complete-guide-to-shared-household-media-governance/) defines who approves the final handover.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid resetting without owner consent, assuming app closure is sign-out, forgetting browsers or source sessions, and following generic reset instructions for the wrong model.

This workflow cannot guarantee data erasure beyond verified manufacturer and provider controls. Seek manufacturer, source-provider, or Norva support when the state is uncertain.

## Separate return proof from personal proof

Keep a receipt or device-return confirmation separate from screenshots of accounts, profiles, sources, or settings. The transaction record should establish custody transfer without preserving unnecessary household data.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should I delete the profile before reset?

Not automatically. Profile state belongs to the account layer and may be used elsewhere. Sign out and handle device data separately.

### Is a factory reset always required?

Follow the current manufacturer and transaction instructions for the specific handover. Do not rely on a universal rule.

### What if the device cannot be powered on?

Record custody, use available legitimate account controls, and contact the manufacturer or support for the appropriate process.
