# A Sofa-Distance Caption Check for Television

> **In short:** Sit in the normal viewing position with ordinary room lighting and the actual remote. Test caption size, contrast, block height, visual overlap, selector discovery, focus visibility, and reading completion across bright, dark, dense, and action scenes. Do not approve TV captions from a close-up screenshot or a mouse-driven desktop simulation.

Television accessibility depends on physical distance and shared-room conditions. A caption that appears large on a developer monitor can be too small or visually unstable from the sofa.

## Record the room context

Capture display model or size where appropriate, supported TV or client context, viewing distance, usual seat, room lighting, glare, caption size, font, colour, edge, background, placement, track, and playback speed.

Do not move closer during the test unless the viewer normally does so.

## Choose representative scenes

Use:

- short dialogue;
- dense or rapid cues;
- bright and dark frames;
- detailed motion behind text;
- a three-line block;
- faces, signs, or lower-thirds near captions;
- a scene requiring speaker or sound identification.

Replay at normal speed.

## Test physical readability

Ask whether the viewer finishes cues without leaning forward, squinting, or repeated rewinds. Record missed words, effort, and whether visual action remains understandable.

Use [the readable caption-size guide](/blog/how-to-choose-a-readable-caption-size/) to adjust supported settings one step at a time.

## Test contrast in the room

Ambient light and glare can reduce perceived separation. Compare representative bright, dark, and mixed scenes with current edge or background treatment. Do not increase screen brightness as the only fix without considering comfort and household settings.

## Original evidence: sofa task card

| Task | Result | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Read dense cue | Completed/missed | Timestamp |
| Follow visual action | Yes/no | Scene note |
| Identify speaker/sound | Result | Cue note |
| Open caption selector | Success/issue | Remote path |
| See focused item | Clear/unclear | Screenshot/photo note |
| Select and return | Success/issue | Focus destination |
| Resume and recheck | State/track | Paired result |

Record distance and lighting once at the top.

## Check block height and overlap

Large TV text can create a tall block. Verify that captions do not hide important faces, signs, or controls. Use [the visual-overlap guide](/blog/how-to-keep-captions-clear-of-important-visual-content/) before shrinking text.

## Test the actual remote

Navigate into the player, open the caption selector, move through entries, select a track, close the menu, and return to playback. Check Back behavior and boundary movement. Focused and selected states should be distinguishable.

Use [the shared-screen focus guide](/blog/how-to-check-focus-visibility-on-a-shared-screen/) for a detailed focus audit.

## Include shared viewers

Ask the person with the greatest reading need to choose the inclusive room setting. Do not request a diagnosis. If one person prefers a different style, use personal contexts where supported or agree a session setting.

## Recheck at night and day only when relevant

If room lighting changes materially between typical sessions, repeat a small contrast sample. Do not create a universal brightness prescription from one room.

## Report a TV-specific barrier

Include supported client/device context, distance, lighting, exact settings, scene set, remote path, expected outcome, observed missed text or focus, and privacy-safe screenshots or room notes.

## Common mistakes and limitations

Avoid testing close to the display, using a mouse, judging paused frames only, and shrinking captions to avoid overlap.

Current TV support, controls, and presentation must be verified through official Norva information. One room does not represent every television or viewer.

## Repeat from every regular seat

A centered seat can hide angle, glare, and distance barriers experienced elsewhere in the room. Label each regular position, keep the same scene and settings, and ask viewers to identify punctuation, speaker changes, and short words without moving closer. Include a bright and dark scene from the same media version. If results differ, report the seat and conditions rather than averaging them into a vague household score.

Use the actual remote to show and dismiss controls during the check. Confirm captions remain readable when progress bars, action prompts, or system overlays appear, and that returning to playback restores the expected caption state.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is screen size enough to choose caption size?

No. Viewing distance, resolution, styling, room light, cue density, and viewer needs also matter.

### Should TV captions always be larger than mobile captions?

Named settings are not directly comparable. Choose by physical readability at the actual distance.

### Can a desktop keyboard test replace a remote test?

No. It misses remote focus paths, Back behavior, directional boundaries, and real sofa-distance perception.
