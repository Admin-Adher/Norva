# How to Delete Your Norva Account and Associated Data

> **In short:** Use the deletion option inside Norva or the official account-deletion page, but prepare first. Review any active subscription with the provider through which you subscribed, remove offline items if appropriate, and record anything you may need later. Account deletion, app removal, subscription management, and control of your connected media source are separate actions.

Deleting an account should be deliberate rather than mysterious. The safest approach is to identify what Norva controls, what your subscription provider controls, and what remains part of your own media source before you submit the request.

## Before you request deletion

Work through these checks while you can still sign in:

1. **Review your subscription.** Check where you originally subscribed and read the cancellation terms shown by that provider. Refund and cancellation rules can vary by provider, so do not assume that deleting an account completes a separate billing action.
2. **Review connected devices.** If your goal is only to remove access from an old screen, [reviewing trusted devices](/blog/review-remove-trusted-devices/) may solve the problem without deleting the account.
3. **Remove local offline items if needed.** Offline availability is conditional on the device, source, and associated rights. Local items are stored on the device, so consider clearing them before giving that device away.
4. **Record support details.** Save the account email and any relevant subscription receipt or provider reference. Do not place passwords or full payment details in support messages.
5. **Confirm the intended scope.** Norva is a software player and organiser. Your connected media source is separate, so manage that source through its own authorised controls.

If security rather than departure is the concern, start with the [account security checklist](/blog/secure-media-player-account/) before choosing permanent deletion.

## Use the official deletion route

Norva's public privacy information says an account-deletion request can be made from the application or through the dedicated deletion page. Use whichever official route you can access:

- open the account controls in the app and locate the deletion option; or
- use the [official account-deletion page](https://norva.tv/delete-account.html).

Follow the instructions displayed at the time of the request. Interfaces can change, so an article should not substitute guessed button names for the live flow. If you cannot complete it, contact [Norva Support](https://norva.tv/support) from an address associated with the account and explain where the process stops.

## The four-part exit checklist

This framework prevents one action from being mistaken for another:

| Area | Question to answer | Where to act |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Norva account | Have I submitted the official deletion request? | Norva app or deletion page |
| Subscription | Have I reviewed cancellation with the original provider? | The provider used to subscribe |
| Devices | Have I removed access or local items where appropriate? | Device and account controls |
| Media source | Do I need to change anything at the source itself? | The source's own authorised controls |

Keep the four answers separate. Uninstalling an app is not the same as submitting an account-deletion request. Likewise, removing a device is a narrower action than deleting the account.

## After submitting the request

Retain any confirmation you receive until the process is resolved. If the live flow gives you a reference or asks you to verify your identity, follow only the official instructions. Avoid sending extra personal information unless support explains why it is required.

The [Norva Privacy Policy](https://norva.tv/privacy) is the source to consult for the categories of information handled by the service and the controls available to you. It is more reliable than assumptions about what the word “data” covers. For subscription and service conditions, consult the [Norva Terms](https://norva.tv/terms) and the terms of the provider used to subscribe.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Deleting because one device is lost.** First consider removing or reviewing that device.
- **Assuming deletion manages billing everywhere.** Verify the subscription separately.
- **Treating the connected source as part of Norva.** It remains governed by its own access and terms.
- **Sharing sensitive credentials with support.** Provide only what is requested through official channels.
- **Following an old screenshot instead of the live flow.** Use current in-app controls or the official page.

For a deeper distinction between deletion and data removal, see [what account deletion can cover](/blog/account-deletion-data-removal/).

## Frequently asked questions

### Does deleting my Norva account automatically cancel my subscription?

Do not assume that it does. Subscription cancellation and refund rules may depend on the provider through which you subscribed. Review that provider's current controls and Norva's current terms before submitting deletion.

### Does the request delete my connected media source?

Norva does not include a media catalogue or media access in its subscription. A compatible source that you are authorised to use remains separate and must be managed through its own controls.

### Is uninstalling Norva enough to delete the account?

No. Removing an application from a device and requesting account deletion are different actions. Use the official in-app deletion control or the dedicated deletion page.
