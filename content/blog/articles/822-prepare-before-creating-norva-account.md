# What to Prepare Before Creating a Norva Account

> **In short:** Before creating a Norva account, verify the official site, current terms and privacy notice, supported devices, and compatible-source requirements. Prepare a protected email account, unique password, password manager, authorized source, realistic recurring profile count, and original billing-provider record. If using the trial, capture the exact start, end, price, tax, renewal, and cancellation terms shown at sign-up. Never store source credentials or payment details in your checklist.

Preparation reduces setup errors and makes later billing, device, and privacy reviews easier. Norva is software for organizing and playing media from a compatible source the user owns or is authorized to use; the account does not include media.

## Verify the official entry point

Use the current Norva domain or official application listing. Check the address carefully, avoid search ads or messages that imitate a sign-in page, and open terms, privacy, and support from first-party navigation.

Record page dates and URLs, not screenshots containing personal details. The [media-account lifecycle handbook](/blog/media-account-lifecycle-handbook/) explains how this evidence remains useful through closure.

## Secure the email account first

The sign-in email can become a recovery and notice channel. Protect it with a unique password and multi-factor authentication where supported. Confirm that recovery addresses and phone numbers are current.

Then generate a separate unique Norva password with a password manager. The [account-and-source credential guide](/blog/separate-account-and-source-credentials/) explains why account protection should precede source setup.

Do not share passwords, one-time codes, or recovery information with household members through plain notes or messaging.

## Confirm source authorization and compatibility

Identify the source you plan to connect, confirm that you own it or are authorized to use it, and check current compatibility information. Secure the source account independently and know its official recovery path.

Do not enter source credentials until the official Norva flow requests the necessary information. Avoid saving credentials in a readiness worksheet, screenshot, or support ticket.

## Check the device environment

Norva currently advertises availability across Web, mobile, and TV on supported devices. Confirm the current operating-system, browser, store, storage, and network requirements for the device you intend to use.

Availability on a platform family is not proof that every model or version is supported. If offline use matters, confirm device, source, media, rights, storage, and current feature conditions before choosing a plan.

## Count recurring profiles

List household members who need separate persistent history, progress, favorites, or preferences. Current Norva material states up to two profiles for Norva and up to five for Norva Family. Recheck the live plan page because this draft marks product claims unverified.

Use the [plan-by-profile guide](/blog/choose-plan-by-profile-needs/) and [Norva-versus-Family framework](/blog/norva-vs-norva-family-decision-framework/) before deciding. Do not infer simultaneous streams or device limits from profile counts.

## Record trial and billing facts

The current offer describes a seven-day free trial with a payment method, followed by the selected paid plan unless canceled. Before confirming, record the displayed price, tax information, currency, trial start and end, renewal date, provider, reminder method, and cancellation route.

The [seven-day trial checklist](/blog/seven-day-trial-start-checklist/) provides a decision record. Verify every value on the final purchase screen because offers and store rules can change.

## Prepare minimal non-secret evidence

Keep an account setup record with date, official URL, plan, provider, profile count, first device label, policy dates, and support link. Exclude passwords, source details, recovery codes, transaction tokens, card numbers, and full receipts.

This record helps identify the original provider if cancellation or entitlement support is needed later.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Creating the media account before securing the email account.
- Assuming the service supplies media.
- Entering source credentials into an unofficial page.
- Choosing a plan from temporary guests.
- Treating profile limits as simultaneous-use limits.
- Starting a trial without recording provider and end date.
- Storing secrets in the readiness scorecard.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need a compatible source before creating the account?

Confirm the current onboarding sequence, but plan to use only a compatible source you own or are authorized to access.

### Should I choose Family for more devices?

Do not assume that. Current plan differentiation is stated through profile counts; verify any device information separately.

### What trial information should I save?

Keep dates, displayed price, tax, currency, renewal, provider, and cancellation route, without recording payment credentials or sensitive receipt values.
