# Create a Fallback When an Offline Copy Is Unavailable

> **In short:** When the preferred offline copy is unavailable, switch to one already verified local alternative. If a trusted connection later exists and the source and rights permit connected playback, reassess then. Otherwise postpone the item and use a non-media activity. Do not bypass app controls, change source credentials, clear app data, or rely on an untested copy just before leaving.

A fallback plan is not a way to force unavailable media onto a device. It is a decision made in advance so that one failed item does not trigger rushed security, storage, or account changes.

## Identify why the preferred item is unavailable

Record the exact observed state:

- no offline control appears;
- the transfer cannot start;
- the transfer remains incomplete;
- required audio or subtitles are absent;
- the item fails a disconnected test;
- storage or battery makes preparation impractical;
- account, source, media, or rights state requires review.

Do not claim a cause without evidence. Norva offline availability depends on the device, compatible source, media, and associated rights. The [offline availability verification](/blog/verify-offline-availability-before-leaving/) helps separate these states.

## Use a three-level fallback hierarchy

### Level 1: one verified local alternative

Choose an eligible item that already passes the disconnected test and fits the same viewing window. It can be shorter or a different format, but required audio, subtitles, profile context, storage, and battery still matter.

Avoid preparing several alternatives for the same problem. One useful fallback is easier to test and less likely to create clutter.

### Level 2: connected viewing only when conditions permit

If a trusted connection becomes available, connected playback may be reconsidered through the authorised compatible source. Check network suitability, source terms, data implications, and current item availability. Do not treat public or unknown Wi-Fi as automatically appropriate.

The [predictable connection-gap plan](/blog/plan-for-predictable-connection-gaps/) clarifies where the next trusted connection is expected.

### Level 3: a non-media alternative

Prepare something that does not depend on the unavailable copy, connectivity, or remaining screen battery: rest, conversation, a physical book, notes, or another activity appropriate to the setting. This protects essential device power and removes pressure to attempt risky fixes.

## Set a troubleshooting stop rule

Stop and change the plan when:

- departure is close and the item remains unverified;
- the next step would clear app storage or remove account data;
- source credentials would need to be changed without time to validate them;
- the only available network is untrusted or unsuitable;
- device free space would fall below your reserve;
- repeated retries threaten the battery budget.

Use Norva support after collecting the item, version, device, app version, time, and observed message. Do not invent a diagnosis.

## Revisit the failed item later

After the trip or gap, reconnect on a trusted network and document whether the issue persists. Review source, account, storage, and app state one layer at a time. The [trip-plan audit](/blog/audit-offline-plan-before-trip/) can reveal whether the fallback was needed because of a planning gap or an item-level condition.

Keep the [offline planning handbook](/blog/offline-viewing-planning-handbook/) as the parent process for future batches.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Retrying an ineligible item repeatedly.
- Clearing all app data to solve one local-copy problem.
- Re-entering credentials in a rushed or public setting.
- Choosing a fallback with untested tracks.
- Consuming essential battery on repeated transfers.
- Assuming connected playback is always acceptable or affordable.
- Treating a fallback as evidence that the original item should work.

## Frequently asked questions

### Should the fallback be the same type of media?

Not necessarily. It should fit the session, viewer, tracks, storage, and battery while being independently verified.

### Can I troubleshoot after connectivity disappears?

Only local observations remain available. Avoid destructive changes; record the state and investigate after a trusted reconnection.

### What if no local alternative is eligible?

Use the non-media fallback. Offline access is conditional, and a responsible plan allows for no eligible copy.
