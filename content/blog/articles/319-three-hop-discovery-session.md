# Build a Three-Hop Discovery Session Without Endless Browsing

> **In short:** Write one discovery question and choose a verified seed. Allow at most three transitions, and require each candidate to preserve one useful attribute while adding one new direction. Verify work identity and version readiness before promoting a card to the next seed. Stop early when the relationship, brief fit, or availability breaks.

Three hops create enough room for surprise while preserving a visible chain back to the original intent. The number is a facilitation boundary, not a claim about attention or interface quality.

## Prepare the session brief

State:

- intended viewer or group;
- source and supported screen;
- quality to preserve from the seed;
- attribute to broaden;
- required language, subtitles, format, or duration;
- final outcome: one ready title or a short shortlist.

Use search to locate the exact seed when needed. [The route selector](/blog/recommendations-search-or-browse/) explains why a verified starting item matters.

DCMI terms for creator, subject, date, type, relation, format, and language provide useful relationship labels. EIDR’s hierarchy helps prevent a version or episode from being mistaken for a separate work-level direction.

## Promote candidates deliberately

At each row:

1. identify the exact candidate;
2. write one visible relationship to the current seed;
3. name the attribute preserved;
4. name the new direction introduced;
5. verify version, availability, language, and subtitles;
6. decide promote, shortlist, research, or release.

Only Promote makes the candidate the next seed. Shortlist preserves a ready option without changing the path. Research holds a credible but unresolved item.

## Use early stop conditions

End the session when:

- one title clearly answers the brief;
- the next relationship is unsupported;
- candidates repeat the old loop;
- source metadata is too incomplete;
- a required version is unavailable;
- the group’s constraints change;
- three hops are complete.

Stopping is an outcome, not a failure. If repetition appears, use a search or browsing reset instead of a fourth hop.

## Handle a surprising pivot

When Hop 1 introduces a compelling new attribute, apply [the surprising-title pivot method](/blog/use-surprising-title-as-discovery-pivot/). Keep the original brief on the card so novelty does not become randomness.

Norva publicly describes source-based recommendations. Exact card order and internal relationship logic require current evidence, and coverage remains bounded by the connected compatible source.

## Convert the session into action

After Hop 3 or an early stop, apply [the focused-shortlist funnel](/blog/turn-recommendations-into-shortlist/). Keep only candidates that are explainable, ready, and distinct. Give Research items one missing question and expiry.

Remove the session card when its occasion ends unless it documents a support or metadata issue. Do not turn every path into permanent favorite history.

At each handoff, read the new seed and relationship sentence before opening another row. This small pause catches accidental version switches and makes it clear whether the next step still belongs to the same discovery chain.

## Original evidence: three-hop card

Run the card once and ask another reviewer to trace the seed-to-hop chain without card order or artwork. If any transition cannot be explained from visible metadata, downgrade it to Unknown and stop at the previous row.

The card documents a bounded discovery decision. It cannot establish algorithmic causality or claim that three hops suit every viewer.

## Common mistakes and limitations

- Starting with an unverified seed version.
- Promoting a candidate solely because it looks novel.
- Carrying several new directions into one hop.
- Continuing beyond a broken relationship.
- Mixing Ready and Research in the final choice.
- Saving every hop as a favorite.

## Frequently asked questions

### Must every session use exactly three hops?

No. Stop earlier when the brief is answered or evidence breaks. Three is the maximum for this workflow.

### Can category browsing count as a hop?

A hop is a seed-to-candidate relationship. Browsing can supply the candidate, but record the relation explicitly.

### What if all three candidates are unavailable?

Keep relevant identities in Research, document the source boundary, and do not present the session as producing a ready choice.
