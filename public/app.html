    Connect your TV service

    Add the service you subscribed to. Providers usually give you either a login or a playlist link — add whichever you have. You can connect more than one.

    Provider login

    The most common option. Use the server address, username and password your provider gave you (sometimes called an “Xtream” login).

    Playlist link

    Use this if your provider gave you a single web link instead of a login (an address ending in .m3u or .m3u8).

    TV guide · optional

    A program guide (EPG) shows what’s on now and next. Many provider logins already include it — only add one here if your channels have no guide.

    Guide & catalogue updates

    Your taste & recommendations

    Help Norva put the right things first. These guide the order of titles and the version played by default — they never hide anything.

    Your region Organises Live TV, logos, categories and recommendations for your area. It never blocks access to any content.
      Preferred audio language When several versions exist, Norva favours this language for the default pick, and it powers the “Best for my languages” sort on Movies & Series. Unknown languages stay visible.
      Preferred subtitle language A gentle nudge toward versions with subtitles in this language (provider subtitles are burned into the picture). Also powers the “Best for my languages” sort on Movies & Series.
      Favourite genres Tap the genres you enjoy — Norva highlights more of them on Home, Movies and Series. Pick as many as you like.
      Preferred quality Which version to play by default when several qualities exist.
      ▸ Advanced settings For power users — the defaults above suit almost everyone.
      Strict language matching Push down versions confirmed to be in the wrong language. Unknown language data is still kept, so good content is never hidden.
      TMDB API key Optional, for self-hosted setups — adds richer posters, real genres and smarter matching. Free key at themoviedb.org → Settings → API. (On norva.tv this is handled for you automatically.)
      Metadata enrichment Runs automatically after each sync when a TMDB key is set.
      Restore incorrectly hidden titles If “Hide broken” hid some titles after a temporary connection refusal (401/403), click to bring them back — they were never really broken.

      Playback & quality

      Norva is set up to play smoothly on its own — you usually don't need to change anything here. These options help you fine-tune playback or fix a channel that won't play right.

      Picture quality

      Sharper looks better but uses more internet and power.

      Maximum picture quality How sharp the video is allowed to get. 1080p looks great on most screens; pick lower if your connection struggles.
      Quality vs. data use Higher gives a crisper picture but needs more data and a stronger connection.
      Sharpen older & low-quality channels Boost grainy or standard-definition channels closer to HD. Looks nicer but uses a bit more power.
      Sharpening method “Faster” is lighter on your device; “Best quality” looks a little sharper.
      Sharpen up to The quality level Norva aims for when sharpening.

      Having trouble with a channel?

      Tell Norva what you're seeing and it'll switch on the right fix — or set the toggles below yourself.

      What happens when you try to watch?

      Picture plays but no sound Turn this on if you can see a channel but hear nothing — it fixes surround-sound formats (Dolby / AC3) that browsers can't play.
      A channel stays black or won't start Turn this on to have Norva fetch streams through its own servers, which gets past blocks that can stop a channel loading.
      ▸ Advanced settings For power users — the defaults above are right for almost everyone.
      Smart processing Detect each stream and only convert it when needed. Recommended on; adds a ~1–3s check on first play.
      Always re-encode video Force a full video conversion (e.g. to burn in subtitles). Much heavier — only enable if you need it.
      Repackage raw streams (remux) Repackage raw .ts streams into MP4 without re-encoding — fast. Helps with some IPTV middleware (m3u-editor, dispatcharr).
      Video chip (encoder) Which GPU handles conversion. Leave on Auto unless you know your hardware — NVENC = NVIDIA, AMF = AMD, QuickSync = Intel, VAAPI = Linux.
      Surround-sound mixdown How 5.1 / 7.1 audio is folded into stereo for playback.
      Connection identity Some providers only allow certain apps. Switch this if your provider blocks the default.
      Custom connection identity Enter the exact identifier (User-Agent) your provider expects.
      Detected hardware Graphics acceleration found on the device running Norva.
